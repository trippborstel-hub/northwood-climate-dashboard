2 Portfolio-Wide Systematic Gaps

These gaps affect all or most portcos and represent structural holes in the data environment — not portco-specific issues.

G-01: No Monthly Granularity — Annual Totals Only
All 10 Portfolio Companies
Critical for Step 3

What's Missing

Every portco reports annual aggregate electricity, gas, and fuel. No monthly or quarterly breakdowns exist anywhere in the data environment. The CSV has one row per facility with annual totals.

Why It Matters

  • Seasonal patterns invisible: Cannot identify heating vs. cooling peaks, which drives HVAC decarbonization economics
  • Demand charges hidden: Cannot assess peak demand, which determines solar/battery ROI
  • Trend detection impossible: Cannot detect month-over-month changes that signal operational issues
  • BAU modeling weakened: Step 3 BAU projections require understanding of load shape, not just annual totals

What We Need

12 months of utility bill data (kWh, therms, and cost) per facility per portco. Ideally: meter-level data or utility account exports.

Fallback If Unavailable

Quarterly totals would provide seasonal shape. If neither is available, we apply ASHRAE climate zone heating/cooling degree-day profiles to model seasonal allocation from annual totals — lower accuracy but defensible.

Affects

Step 2 dashboard design (monthly trending), Step 3 BAU modeling and decarbonization pathway timing, LP reporting granularity.

G-02: No Financial Data — P&L and Balance Sheet Missing
All 10 Portfolio Companies
Blocks Step 3

What's Missing

Zero financial data received for any portco. No revenue, EBITDA, operating costs, capex budgets, energy cost breakdowns, or balance sheet items.

Why It Matters

  • Carbon intensity metrics: Cannot calculate mt CO2e per $M revenue — the metric LPs and buyers actually benchmark
  • Decarbonization ROI: Cannot size investments as % of EBITDA or payback against energy cost savings
  • Materiality screening: Cannot determine if climate costs are material relative to portco financials
  • Exit narrative: Cannot build the "ESG creates value" story for Meridian's exit without financial context

What We Need

For the 3 priority portcos (Meridian, Sterling, Keystone): Annual P&L summary (revenue, COGS, EBITDA), annual energy costs, and current capex budget or VCP capex allocation.

For the remaining 7: Revenue and EBITDA sufficient for carbon intensity calculation.

Sensitivity

Financial data is sensitive. We can work with rounded or banded figures (e.g., "Revenue: $50-60M") if exact numbers require IC approval. The Step 3 model uses ratios, not absolute values.

G-03: No Value Creation Plans
All 10 Portfolio Companies (priority: Meridian, Sterling, Keystone)
Blocks Step 3

What's Missing

No VCPs, 100-day plans, or strategic roadmaps received for any portco. We don't know what operational, growth, or capital initiatives are already planned.

Why It Matters

  • Alignment: Climate initiatives compete for the same capex, management attention, and operating budget as VCP initiatives. Without VCPs, we can't identify synergies or conflicts.
  • Growth assumptions: BAU emissions projections need revenue/capacity growth assumptions. VCPs contain these.
  • Keystone (100-day): At 100-day stage, the entire operating plan is being designed now. Climate data architecture should be embedded, not bolted on later.

What We Need

VCP or equivalent strategic plan for Meridian, Sterling, and Keystone. Key sections: planned capex, growth targets, operational efficiency initiatives, facility expansion/consolidation plans.

Fallback

If formal VCPs aren't shareable, a 30-minute call with each deal lead covering growth trajectory, planned capex, and facility changes would provide the inputs we need for BAU modeling.

G-04: No CC Environment Access
Engagement Infrastructure
Blocks Step 2

What's Missing

The proposal specifies building the climate data system in Northwood's CC environment. No workspace access, credentials, or environment URL has been provided. We're currently working locally.

Impact

G-05: No Waste Manifests or Hauler Data
All 10 Portfolio Companies (critical: Sterling, Harvest Ridge, Clearview)
High

What's Missing

No waste manifests, hauler invoices, or waste tonnage records received. Questionnaires provide qualitative descriptions:

  • Sterling: 180 tons metal chips, 12K gal spent coolant, 3.5K gal cleaning chemicals — but these are estimates, not manifest data
  • Harvest Ridge: $185K in food waste disposal costs — but no tonnage, no waste composition
  • Clearview: Environmental services company — likely has detailed waste data but it wasn't provided
  • NovaCare: Medical waste generated but no volumes reported

What We Need

For climate purposes: waste tonnage by disposal method (landfill, recycling, incineration, composting). Hauler invoices typically have weight tickets. Waste audits, if they exist, would be ideal.

Fallback

EPA WARM model can estimate waste emissions from industry benchmarks (tons per employee or per sq ft), but this produces Grade C data. Priority portcos should provide actuals; remaining 7 can use estimates.

G-06: No Scope 3 Input Data
All 10 Portfolio Companies
High

What's Missing

The data environment contains zero dedicated Scope 3 data. No business travel records, no employee commuting surveys, no purchased goods spend data, no supply chain emissions factors. The only Scope 3-adjacent data points are:

  • Keystone: 35% of therapy sessions are in-home (implies therapist driving — Cat 7) but no mileage data
  • Ridgeline: Manages 18M sq ft / 285M kWh for clients (Cat 13) but no client energy data provided
  • Sterling: Waste descriptions (Cat 5) but no tonnage
  • Harvest Ridge: Food waste costs but no volumes (Cat 5)

What We Need (Minimum for Screening)

Per the proposal, Step 1 includes Scope 3 screening — not full quantification. For screening, we need:

  • Category 1 (Purchased Goods): Total procurement spend by category — enables spend-based estimation
  • Category 5 (Waste): Waste tonnage by disposal method (see G-05)
  • Category 6 (Business Travel): Total travel spend or miles by mode (air, rail, car)
  • Category 7 (Employee Commuting): Employee count by location (have this) + average commute distance (need survey or ZIP code analysis)
  • Category 13 (Downstream Leased Assets): Ridgeline managed portfolio energy data

Fallback

Scope 3 screening can use industry-average intensity factors from EPA EEIO or Exiobase. This produces directional results — sufficient to identify which categories are material and which are negligible.

3 Priority Portco Gaps

Gaps specific to the three priority portcos that go beyond the portfolio-wide issues above.

Meridian Business Solutions (Fund I — Exit Prep)

G-09: Shared-Meter Allocation Methodology Undocumented
Meridian Business Solutions | MBS-005 (New York), MBS-006 (Boston)
Medium

What's Missing

New York and Boston facilities share building meters. The gas allocation between questionnaire (142K therms) and CSV (130K therms) differs by 9.6%, suggesting different allocation methods were used. No documentation of how the allocation was calculated.

What We Need

Lease documents showing allocation methodology (sq ft pro-rata, sub-metering, or landlord estimate). For exit preparation, a buyer's diligence team will ask how shared-meter allocations were determined. We need the answer documented.

G-10: Fleet Boundary Classification Unresolved
Meridian Business Solutions | 5 facilities with gasoline
Medium

What's Missing

Questionnaire says "no fleet." CSV shows 3,200 gal across 5 facilities. We don't know if these are company-owned vehicles (Scope 1), leased vehicles (Scope 1), or personal vehicles with mileage reimbursement (Scope 3 Cat 7). This is a boundary question, not a data error.

What We Need

Vehicle ownership documentation: company-owned/leased = Scope 1. Personal with mileage reimbursement = Scope 3 Cat 7. One email to the CFO resolves this.

Sterling Precision Manufacturing (Fund I — Mid-Hold)

G-11: No Process-Level Energy Breakdown
Sterling Precision Manufacturing | SPM-001 (Akron), SPM-002 (Elyria)
High

What's Missing

Sterling is the most energy-intensive portco (7.3M kWh electricity, 324K therms gas). We have facility-level totals but no breakdown by process: CNC machining, heat treatment furnaces, compressed air, lighting, HVAC, etc.

Why It Matters

The Sterling value story is cost savings from decarbonization. Without knowing which processes consume the most energy, we can't identify the highest-ROI efficiency measures. Heat treatment furnaces vs. CNC machines vs. compressed air systems have completely different decarbonization economics.

What We Need

Process-level energy allocation — even a rough estimate (e.g., "furnaces ~40%, CNC ~30%, compressed air ~15%, other ~15%"). A facility energy audit report would be ideal. If none exists, a 30-minute call with the plant manager would suffice.

Fallback

Apply DOE Manufacturing Energy Consumption Survey (MECS) profiles for NAICS 3329 (Other Fabricated Metal Product) to estimate process allocation. This is standard practice but less accurate than facility-specific data.

G-12: R-22 Phase-Out Timeline Missing
Sterling Precision Manufacturing | SPM-001 (Akron)
Medium

What's Missing

Akron has 50 lbs of R-22 (GWP 1,810) — a refrigerant banned for new production since Jan 2020. The questionnaire notes an 8 lb recharge in Aug 2024. No information on the replacement plan or timeline.

What We Need

HVAC replacement plan for the R-22 system. This is both a regulatory compliance issue (supply of R-22 is finite and increasingly expensive) and a decarbonization opportunity. Replacement with a modern system reduces both direct emissions (lower GWP refrigerant) and indirect emissions (higher efficiency). This feeds directly into the Sterling decarbonization pathway in Step 3.

Keystone Behavioral Health (Fund II — 100-Day)

G-13: In-Home Therapy Mileage — Major Scope 3 Blind Spot
Keystone Behavioral Health | All facilities
High

What's Missing

35% of Keystone's therapy sessions are in-home — therapists drive personal vehicles to client homes. With ~340 employees across 22 clinics, this likely represents 50-80 therapists driving daily. No mileage data, no reimbursement records, no route information exists.

Why It Matters

This is likely Keystone's single largest emission source after facility electricity. A rough estimate: 65 therapists x 30 miles/day x 250 days = ~487,500 miles/year = ~195 mt CO2e. This could exceed Keystone's entire Scope 1 + 2 footprint.

What We Need

Option A (ideal): Mileage reimbursement records from HR/payroll — total miles reimbursed in 2025 by employee or region.

Option B (good): Number of in-home therapists, average daily visits, and average distance between visits.

Option C (minimum): Total number of in-home visits in 2025 and average ZIP code distance from clinic to visit.

Why Now

Keystone is at 100-day stage — the operational systems are being designed right now. If we build the climate data architecture without a mileage tracking system, we'll have to retrofit it later. Better to embed it in whatever HR/scheduling platform they're implementing.

G-14: HVAC Refrigerant Inventory Missing
Keystone Behavioral Health | All 22 clinics
Medium

What's Missing

No refrigerant data for any Keystone facility. Zero entries in the CSV for refrigerant_type or refrigerant_lbs. The questionnaire doesn't address refrigerants at all (different format, no CFO review).

What We Need

HVAC equipment inventory or property management records showing refrigerant type and installed charge per clinic. Most leased clinic spaces have standard rooftop units — property managers typically maintain these records. Estimate: 22 clinics x ~15 lbs R-410A average = ~330 lbs total installed, ~6.6 lbs leaked/year at 2% rate = ~6 mt CO2e. Small but needed for completeness.

G-15: CFO Financial Review Not Completed
Keystone Behavioral Health
Medium

What's Missing

Questionnaire was submitted by the COO (Jennifer Martinez) with note that CFO (William Park) was unavailable due to billing platform implementation. Energy cost figures and financial data were not reviewed by finance.

What We Need

CFO review of the energy and facility cost sections when the billing platform transition stabilizes. This is a validation gap, not a data gap — the COO's operational data is likely accurate but the financial cross-check is missing.

Key Takeaway for Sarah Mitchell

Northwood gave us a solid foundation for Scope 1 and Scope 2 baseline calculations — energy data exists for 139 facilities across all 10 portcos. After we fix the errors documented in Deliverable 3, we can produce a defensible Scope 1+2 baseline for 8 of 10 companies within the current data environment.

What's missing are the layers that turn a baseline into a strategy: financial data to size the opportunity, value creation plans to align priorities, monthly granularity to design interventions, and Scope 3 inputs to see the full picture.

Deliverable 5 translates these gaps into a specific, prioritized data request — organized so that the most urgent items (which unblock Steps 2 and 3) are separated from the items that improve accuracy but don't block progress.
