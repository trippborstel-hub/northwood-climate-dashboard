Recommendation Priority Portco Selection Review

Sarah, now that we've completed the data quality assessment across all 10 companies, we want to share our thinking on the priority portco selection before we go deeper.

Our proposal selected Meridian, Sterling, and Keystone based on three criteria: status spectrum (near-exit / mid-hold / 100-day), sector diversity, and at least one climate-intensive operation. Having now reviewed the data, here's where each stands:

PortcoData QualityStrategic RationaleOur View
Meridian Business Solutions 4 / 5 Exit prep — strong data, minor issues (fleet boundary, shared meters). Baseline calculation-ready after two quick clarifications. Keep. No concerns.
Sterling Precision Mfg 4 / 5 Climate-intensive mid-hold — highest energy consumption in the portfolio. Facility allocation issue resolvable with one email. Process energy breakdown needed for Step 3 but doesn't block baseline. Keep. Strongest decarbonization story.
Keystone Behavioral Health 2 / 5 100-day stage — worst data quality in the portfolio. 63% Grade C, 6 estimated clinics, no CFO review, no refrigerant inventory, no therapist mileage data. Keep — but the rationale has shifted. See below.

Why We're Keeping Keystone Despite the Data Gap

Keystone's poor data quality is precisely what makes it the most valuable priority portco for this engagement. Here's the logic:

  • The 100-day use case is unique. Keystone is the only portco where the operational systems are being designed right now. We can embed climate data collection into whatever HR, scheduling, and facility management platforms they're implementing — rather than retrofitting it later. No other portco offers this.
  • Bad data proves the need. If we swap Keystone for a portco with clean data (Trident already self-calculated emissions, Clearview's ESG is their business), we produce a nicer baseline but miss the opportunity to demonstrate what your team's data collection infrastructure should look like going forward. The deliverable for Keystone isn't a baseline — it's a blueprint.
  • The Scope 3 finding is differentiated. Keystone's in-home therapy model (35% of sessions, ~490,000 estimated annual miles) is likely its largest emission source — potentially exceeding all facility electricity combined. That's the kind of insight that changes how a portco thinks about its operations. You don't find that in a clean dataset.
  • Three portcos hedges against this risk. We selected three precisely so that if one portco's data is thin, the other two still produce strong results. Meridian and Sterling will carry the quantitative baseline. Keystone carries the architecture and design story.

What Changes for Keystone

The deliverable mix shifts. For Meridian and Sterling, we produce a full GHG Protocol-aligned baseline with decarbonization pathways. For Keystone, the primary deliverable is the data collection architecture — the system design that makes future baselines reliable. We'll still calculate a Keystone baseline from available data, but it will carry more caveats and wider uncertainty bands.

If Keystone's data doesn't materially improve by Week 3 (i.e., R-07 and R-13 don't come back), we'll pivot: deeper analysis on Meridian and Sterling, lighter architecture-focused scope on Keystone. You'll see this coming — we won't surprise you.
