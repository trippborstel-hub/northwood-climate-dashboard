Five Vectors That Move EBITDA why these, and why not carbon price

Standard TCFD transition categories (policy / legal / technology / market / reputation) are too abstract for a mid-market US PE LP. We rescore against the five vectors below — each one ties to a P&L line or to exit DD. A score of 1 = immaterial; 5 = financially material within 7 years if unmitigated.

1 · Customer ESG Pressure
Top customers are F500/public/regulated and have SBTi or Scope 3 commitments that will cascade as procurement requirements (PCFs, CDP, emissions thresholds). Risk = lost contracts or margin compression.
2 · Input / Supply Cost
Exposure to commodities or inputs whose price/availability bends under transition — steel, cement, diesel, refrigerants, ag commodities. Not the abstract carbon price — actual supply economics.
3 · Capex Cycle Cliff
ICE fleets, gas process heat, HFC refrigeration, aging HVAC. 7 years covers at least one major replacement cycle for most equipment classes — is the transition-compliant version budgeted?
4 · Regulatory Exposure
Not federal carbon pricing — the rules actually landing: CA SB 253/261, EU CBAM (if exporters), SEC climate (if exit to public acquirer), state Building Performance Standards, F-gas, methane.
5 · Exit / Refi Readiness
Most underrated. In 7 years every portco hits exit or refi. Strategic acquirers and lenders are pricing climate diligence. A portco that can't answer basic Scope 1/2 + transition questions takes a multiple haircut or loses bidders.

Harvest Ridge Foods · Specialty food co-packing · Fund II · entered Apr 2025
EBITDA $22.8M · top-5 customer concentration 45% · KY plant
Why this is the #1 transition risk in the portfolio. Co-packing customers in specialty/natural CPG are disproportionately F500 brands with the most aggressive Scope 3 commitments in the consumer sector (Unilever, Nestlé, PepsiCo, General Mills all SBTi-validated). With 45% revenue from the top 5, even one anchor customer adopting a supplier emissions threshold or product carbon footprint (PCF) requirement is an existential customer-retention event, not a procurement annoyance. Bowling Green facility also has water-availability flag (physical-risk overlap). Founder/CEO transition compounds execution risk.
ActionWithin 60 days: identify which top-5 customers have published Scope 3 / supplier programs; commission a facility-level Scope 1+2 baseline + a per-SKU cradle-to-gate PCF for the top 3 customers' top SKUs. This is a defensive contract-retention spend, not an ESG project.
Meridian Business Solutions · BPO / managed staffing · Fund I · in active exit prep
EBITDA $38.3M · 8-year hold · sell-side advisor selection on Mar-12 board agenda
Risk is the buyer's diligence questionnaire, not Meridian's operations. Direct transition exposure is low (asset-light services). But the customer base concentrates in financial services — the single highest-SBTi-adoption sector — and any strategic or sponsor-to-sponsor buyer in 2026–27 will run a structured climate DD pass. With nothing to hand them, the realistic outcome is either a 0.5–1.0x multiple haircut (well-modeled in 2024–25 PE secondary data) or a narrower bidder field. On $42M of '26E EBITDA, every 0.5x is ~$21M of enterprise value.
ActionPre-DD pack in weeks, not months: Scope 1/2 baseline, customer-by-customer SBTi/CDP map, transition risk one-pager, climate-section talking points for the CIM. This is the highest-leverage spend in the portfolio because the timing window closes when sell-side launches.
Sterling Precision Manufacturing · A&D precision machining · Fund I

ActionConvert the Sterling decarb planner into a customer-RFP response asset: standardize the Scope 1/2 baseline, build a "supplier sustainability one-pager" that wins ESG-gated RFPs. Treat decarb capex as revenue defense, not ESG.
Ridgeline Property Services · Commercial property mgmt · Fund II · entered Jul 2025

Institutional CRE owners (Ridgeline's core client) have NZAOA-style portfolio commitments and need their PM partner to deliver compliance, not just throw the regulation back at them. The risk is losing accounts to competitors offering bundled sustainability services; the upside is repricing the management contract +5–8% by adding a sustainability-services line. Pinnacle Testing & Inspection · NDT / industrial inspection · Fund II

Customer-mix transition is the story — not Pinnacle's own operations. NDT demand is heavily tied to refineries, pipelines, and aging fossil infrastructure. Monitored, Not Material — Why Carbon Pricing Is Not Modeled

Carbon pricing scores high on TCFD frameworks and low on financial materiality for unregulated US mid-market PE. The portfolio has no exposure to mandatory cap-and-trade or carbon taxes through 2033 under any plausible US federal policy path. We track the following as monitored watchlist items rather than modeled scenarios:

  • Federal carbon pricing — no plausible enactment in 7-year horizon under any current political configuration
  • EU CBAM — only relevant if Sterling materially exports machined components to EU customers; currently negligible
  • RGGI / California cap-and-trade extensions — none of the portcos operate covered facilities
  • SEC climate disclosure (final rule) — relevant only at exit if buyer is an SEC registrant; flagged in Meridian DD prep
  • State-level carbon disclosure (CA SB 253/261) — applies to entities >$1B revenue; only reaches the portfolio indirectly via large customers (which is captured under Customer ESG vector)

If any of these change posture (e.g., a federal pricing proposal advances), this list moves into the modeled risk section above.

Methodology. Vector scores 1–5 are judgment-weighted from portco financial data (portco-financials/tmp_portcos.json), sector-level exposure benchmarks, and customer-mix indicators where disclosed. Overall risk = max(vectors), elevated by exit/refi proximity. Dollar impact estimates are first-order: current EBITDA × exposure share × likelihood-weighted impact, intended for prioritization, not for forecasting. Each portco thesis should be pressure-tested with the deal lead and management team before board use. What's not in this view yet: debt covenant transition triggers (need to read the credit agreements), supplier-side Scope 3 (need procurement data), and customer-by-customer SBTi mapping (the next sprint).
