Scoring Methodology

This scorecard applies the McKinsey ESG risk framework adapted for PE portfolio monitoring. Each portfolio company is assessed across 8 dimensions spanning Environmental, Social, and Governance pillars. Scores are derived from climate questionnaire submissions, facility-level carbon data (139 facilities), financial performance data, and sector-specific risk factors.

Scoring Scale

ScoreRatingDefinition
1MinimalNo material ESG risk. Strong controls. No action required.
2LowMinor ESG considerations. Adequate controls. Monitor on standard cycle.
3ModerateMaterial ESG exposure. Management awareness required. Action plan recommended.
4ElevatedSignificant ESG risk. Active management required. Board-level visibility recommended.
5HighCritical ESG risk requiring immediate attention. Potential value impairment. IC escalation.

Composite Calculation

Composite = Σ(Score × Weight). Risk thresholds: ≤1.5 Minimal, ≤2.5 Low, ≤3.0 Moderate, ≤3.75 Elevated, >3.75 High.
