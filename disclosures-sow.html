Scope

Produce two fund-level climate disclosures for Northwood Capital Partners, covering Fund I and Fund II on a consolidated basis:

  1. TCFD-aligned disclosure — four pillars (Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics & Targets), written to the TCFD Recommendations and IFRS S2 where additive.
  2. CSRD ESRS E1 disclosure — nine E1 disclosure requirements under the voluntary preparatory posture appropriate for a US mid-market PE firm with EU supply-chain exposure through select portcos.

Both disclosures share one underlying evidence base and are produced from one process.

Reporting Parameters

  • Reporting period: Fiscal year ending December 31, 2025 (LTM to January 31, 2026 where calendar-year data unavailable)
  • Reporting date / data cutoff: January 31, 2026
  • Organizational boundary: Operational control approach, fund-level rollup
  • Portcos in scope: 10 active portfolio companies across Fund I and Fund II; realized investments excluded
  • Framework alignment: TCFD Recommendations (2017, 2021 Annex), ESRS E1 (EC Delegated Act June 2023), IPCC AR6 GWPs, GHG Protocol Corporate Standard

Deliverables

  3. 1-Page Board Summary — Northwood IC / LP-facing synthesis
  4. Gap-Fill Synthesis Memo — structured findings from governance interview and portco questionnaires
  5. Methodology Appendix — emission factors, boundary rules, scope 3 methodology, data quality grades
  6. Evidence Log — traceability map from each disclosed figure back to source

Assumptions

This fee assumes:

  • Northwood makes available the existing carbon inventory, physical risk, and transition risk outputs produced in Weeks 2–5 of the broader engagement (already in hand).
  • Sarah Mitchell is available for a 60-minute interview in Week 2.
  • Portco CFOs respond to a 15-minute renewable-energy questionnaire within 5 business days of receipt.
  • Northwood's IC approves any forward-looking target commitments disclosed in the Targets section. Kith does not commit targets on Northwood's behalf.

If any assumption is not met, Kith flags the impact before proceeding. Scope changes are documented as amendments.

What Kith Does Not Do Under This SOW

  • Provide assurance or audit services. We are not an assurance firm.
  • Submit the disclosures to regulators, LPs, or standard-setters. We produce them; Northwood owns submission.
  • Commit Northwood to targets, policies, or positions on its behalf. All commitments require IC sign-off.
  • Modify Northwood's underlying financial or emissions data. We report what Northwood provides.

Why this price (plain terms)

The price reflects (1) Kith's reference-file library and Claude Code-native workflow removing the manual drafting burden, (2) Weeks 2–5 of the broader engagement producing the data infrastructure — the compounding value of the AI-native model, and (3) human hours concentrated where they matter: governance interview, QA review, final narrative shaping.
