# Northwood Capital — Deal Screening Criteria

**Confidential — Internal Use Only**

---

## Purpose

This document defines the screening criteria, decision rules, and memo format used by Northwood Capital's investment team when evaluating new deal opportunities. The screening process is the first gate in our investment pipeline and is designed to quickly and consistently determine whether a deal merits further diligence resources.

**Screening is performed by the assigned deal team (deal lead + support) within 5 business days of CIM receipt.** The output is a 1-page screening memo presented at the weekly Monday pipeline meeting. The Investment Committee (IC Chair: Catherine Zhao) makes the advance/pass/monitor decision.

---

## Auto-Pass Rules

The following criteria result in an automatic pass. No screening memo is required — the deal lead logs the pass reason in the pipeline tracker and communicates the pass to the intermediary.

| Rule | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **EBITDA below minimum** | Adjusted EBITDA < $10M | Below scale threshold for platform investment. Too small for our equity check size and value creation model. May be catalogued as potential add-on for existing portfolio companies. |
| **Enterprise value above range** | EV > $300M | Above our target range. Competitive dynamics at this size favor larger funds with different return profiles and cost of capital. |
| **Turnaround situation** | Declining EBITDA, operational distress, or restructuring required | Not our competency. We buy good businesses and make them better. We do not fix broken ones. |
| **Pure commodity / cyclical** | Revenue primarily from undifferentiated commodity products or services with >30% peak-to-trough revenue cyclicality | Incompatible with our return model. Leverage and hold period assumptions break down with cyclical revenue. |
| **International-only operations** | >75% of revenue from non-U.S. operations | Outside our operating playbook. Our sector expertise, add-on network, and operating partner capabilities are U.S.-focused. |
| **Venture-stage / pre-profitability** | No established EBITDA track record (minimum 2 years) | We require proven unit economics and sustained profitability. We are not growth equity or venture investors. |

### Auto-Pass Logging

When a deal is auto-passed, the deal lead records:
- Date received and source (intermediary or proprietary)
- Company name, sector, EBITDA, and EV (if available)
- Pass rule triggered
- Whether the company could be relevant as a portfolio company add-on (if yes, tag the relevant portco)

## Flag-for-Review Rules

The following criteria do not trigger an automatic pass but require explicit acknowledgment and discussion in the screening memo. The deal lead must address the flagged item in the "Key Risks" section with a preliminary assessment of severity and mitigability.

| Flag | Trigger | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **Small for platform** | Adjusted EBITDA $10M–$15M | Viable as a platform only if (a) clear line of sight to $20M+ EBITDA within 24 months through organic growth or identified add-ons, and (b) entry valuation reflects the smaller scale (target 6–8x vs. 8–10x for larger platforms). |
| **Customer concentration** | Top customer >25% of revenue | Requires diligence on contract terms, relationship depth, switching costs, and customer financial health. Concentration >40% is effectively a discretionary pass absent extraordinary circumstances. |
| **Cyclical end markets** | Revenue materially tied to GDP-sensitive end markets (e.g., construction, automotive, energy capex) | Requires stress testing on -20% revenue scenario. Must demonstrate margin resilience and covenant headroom in downside case. |
| **Regulatory overhang** | Pending legislation, rule-making, or enforcement action that could materially impact the business model | Requires assessment of probability, timeline, and magnitude of regulatory change. Must size the downside impact in the financial model. Does not include ongoing regulatory complexity that creates barriers to entry (which is a positive). |
| **Founder/CEO transition risk** | Founder is primary relationship holder for key customers or has no identified successor | Requires assessment of management bench depth, transition plan feasibility, and customer relationship transferability. |
| **Working capital intensity** | Net working capital >15% of revenue | Requires detailed analysis of cash conversion cycle, seasonal swings, and impact on leverage and free cash flow. Higher working capital compresses returns through larger equity checks and lower distributable cash flow. |
| **Technology disruption exposure** | Business model potentially exposed to AI, automation, or platform disintermediation within hold period | Requires assessment of defensibility, adaptation potential, and timeline. Flag if disruption risk is non-trivial within a 5-year horizon. |

## Scoring Dimensions

When the screening memo recommends ADVANCE, the deal team provides a preliminary score across five dimensions to calibrate the opportunity relative to the firm's historical investments. Scores are 1–5 (1 = weak, 5 = exceptional). These are directional and will be refined during diligence.

| Dimension | Definition | Scoring Guide |
|---|---|---|
| **Market Position** | Strength of the company's competitive position in its target market. | 5 = Clear #1 with durable moat. 4 = Top 3 with identifiable path to #1. 3 = Solid niche position, limited competitive pressure. 2 = Competitive market, position defensible but not dominant. 1 = Undifferentiated, no clear moat. |
| **Management Quality** | Caliber, depth, and retention risk of the management team. | 5 = Proven institutional team, no key-person risk. 4 = Strong CEO/CFO, some bench depth. 3 = Capable team, identifiable gaps that are recruitable. 2 = Founder-dependent, thin bench. 1 = Significant leadership gaps, no clear path to resolution. |
| **Growth Visibility** | Clarity and reliability of the revenue growth trajectory. | 5 = Contracted backlog + identified organic and acquisitive growth. 4 = Strong pipeline with repeatable sales motion. 3 = Positive end-market trends, reasonable growth assumptions. 2 = Growth thesis relies on execution of unproven initiatives. 1 = Flat or declining, growth thesis speculative. |
| **Margin Profile** | Current margin quality and improvement potential. | 5 = Above-sector margins with identified expansion levers. 4 = At-sector margins with clear operational improvement runway. 3 = Adequate margins, some improvement opportunity. 2 = Below-sector margins, improvement requires significant investment. 1 = Structurally low margins, limited path to improvement. |
| **Fragmentation / Consolidation Opportunity** | Availability and attractiveness of add-on acquisition targets. | 5 = 10+ identified targets, proven add-on execution in sector. 4 = 5–10 identified targets, fragmented market. 3 = Some targets identified, moderate fragmentation. 2 = Few targets, market consolidating but limited options. 1 = Consolidated market, no meaningful add-on opportunity. |

**Minimum threshold to advance:** No dimension scored below 2. Average score of 3.0 or higher across all five dimensions.

## Valuation Guardrails

Entry valuation is a critical screening criterion. We maintain sector-specific valuation ranges based on our return model (targeting 2.5–3.0x gross MOIC, 20–25% gross IRR over a 4–6 year hold).

| Sector | Entry EBITDA Multiple Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Business Services** | 7–10x | Higher end for businesses with >80% recurring revenue, low customer concentration, and proven add-on thesis. Lower end for project-based or cyclically exposed services. |
| **Healthcare Services** | 8–11x | Premium reflects non-discretionary demand, payor diversification, and regulatory barriers. Higher end for multi-site platforms with de novo expansion capability. Lower end for single-site or payor-concentrated businesses. |
| **Industrial Technology** | 6–9x | Reflects capital intensity, cyclical exposure, and project-based revenue risk. Higher end for businesses with recurring testing/inspection contracts or defense/aerospace exposure. Lower end for general precision manufacturing. |

### Hard Ceiling

**Walk away above 12x EBITDA** unless all of the following are true:
- Revenue growth >15% CAGR (3-year)
- EBITDA margin >25%
- >85% recurring/contractual revenue
- Clear line of sight to 3 or more accretive add-on acquisitions at 5–7x
- Return model demonstrates >20% gross IRR at 12x+ entry even at exit-at-entry-multiple

In practice, we have never paid above 11x for a platform investment.

### Add-On Valuation

Add-on acquisitions are held to a lower multiple standard than platform investments:
- Target range: 4–7x EBITDA
- Maximum: 8x (requires IC approval and must be justified by strategic value — geographic expansion, capability addition, or customer access that is not achievable organically)
- Add-on transactions below $10M equity check are approved by the deal lead and one Managing Partner (no full IC required)

## Process Notes

### Weekly Pipeline Meeting (Monday, 9:00 AM CT)

- All active deal team members attend
- Format: 10-minute slots per deal
- New deals: Screening memo presented, advance/pass/monitor decision made
- Active deals: Status update, next milestone, resource needs
- Passed deals: Logged with reason and re-engagement trigger (if applicable)

### Screening Timeline

| Milestone | Target |
|---|---|
| CIM received | Day 0 |
| Deal lead assigned | Day 0–1 |
| Screening memo drafted | Day 3–4 |
| Pipeline meeting presentation | Day 5–7 (next Monday) |
| Pass communication to intermediary | Within 24 hours of decision |
| Advance — management presentation request | Within 48 hours of decision |

### Pass Communication

When we pass on a deal, the deal lead communicates the decision to the intermediary within 24 hours. We provide a brief, professional rationale (valuation, sector fit, size, or timing) without disclosing proprietary analysis. Maintaining strong intermediary relationships is a strategic priority — how we pass matters as much as what we pursue.
