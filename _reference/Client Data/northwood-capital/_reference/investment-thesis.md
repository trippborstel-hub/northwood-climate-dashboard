# Northwood Capital — Investment Thesis & Strategy

**Confidential — Internal Use Only**

---

## Mission

Northwood Capital partners with founder-led and entrepreneur-built businesses in the U.S. mid-market to build institutional-quality companies. We invest in sectors where operational improvement, disciplined add-on acquisitions, and management professionalization create durable value. Our objective is to generate superior risk-adjusted returns for our limited partners while building businesses that are stronger, more scalable, and more resilient than when we acquired them.

---

## Investment Philosophy

We believe that the most reliable path to private equity returns in the mid-market is buying good businesses at reasonable prices and making them measurably better. We are not financial engineers. We do not rely on leverage or multiple expansion to generate returns. Our playbook is grounded in operational improvement, strategic acquisitions, and the professionalization of management and systems.

We target businesses that possess an underlying competitive advantage — mission-critical services, recurring revenue, fragmented markets — where the application of capital, operating expertise, and institutional processes can accelerate growth and margin improvement. We take control positions and work as active, engaged partners with management teams.

**Core beliefs that shape our approach:**

- **Operational value creation is more durable than financial engineering.** Leverage and multiple expansion are outcomes, not strategies. EBITDA growth and margin improvement are strategies.
- **Fragmented markets reward disciplined consolidators.** Add-on acquisitions in fragmented sectors create value through scale, cross-sell, geographic density, and procurement leverage — but only when executed with disciplined integration.
- **Management teams are the primary value creation engine.** We succeed when management teams succeed. We invest in leadership — recruiting, developing, and retaining the best operators in our target sectors.
- **Sector specialization produces better outcomes.** Deep sector knowledge improves sourcing, diligence, pattern recognition, and post-close execution. We stay in our lanes.

## Target Sectors

### Business Services

Services businesses with recurring or contractual revenue, low capital intensity, and scalable operating models. Sub-sectors of focus include:

- Business process outsourcing and managed staffing
- IT managed services and cybersecurity
- Professional and technical staffing
- Facilities management
- Regulatory compliance and consulting

**Why we like it:** Mission-critical services with high switching costs, labor-intensive models where operational efficiency drives margin, and fragmented competitive landscapes with abundant add-on acquisition opportunities.

### Healthcare Services

Multi-site healthcare services businesses with predictable payor-driven revenue and clinical defensibility. Sub-sectors of focus include:

- Outpatient rehabilitation and physical therapy
- Behavioral health (ABA therapy, outpatient mental health)
- Dental practice management (DSOs)
- Urgent care and walk-in clinics
- Healthcare staffing and workforce solutions

**Why we like it:** Non-discretionary demand with demographic tailwinds, payor diversification across commercial and government sources, high barriers to entry (licensing, credentialing, clinical expertise), and significant platform consolidation opportunity.

### Industrial Technology

Technology-enabled industrial services and precision manufacturing businesses with technical moats and essential end-market demand. Sub-sectors of focus include:

- Precision machining and engineered components
- Non-destructive testing and industrial inspection
- Environmental remediation and testing services
- Automation and process control services
- Specialty distribution (niche industrial/chemical)

**Why we like it:** Critical infrastructure demand with long replacement cycles, skilled labor shortages that create barriers to entry, reshoring and onshoring tailwinds, and regulatory requirements that drive recurring demand.

## Value Creation Playbook

Every Northwood investment is underwritten to a specific value creation plan developed during diligence and refined during the first 100 days of ownership. Our playbook draws on four core levers:

### 1. Operational Improvement

- Margin expansion through procurement optimization, labor productivity, and SG&A rationalization
- Implementation of KPI-driven management cadences (weekly, monthly, quarterly operating reviews)
- Technology modernization — ERP, CRM, and business intelligence platform implementations
- Working capital optimization (DSO reduction, inventory management, billing efficiency)
- Operating Partner involvement: direct, hands-on engagement from sector-experienced operators

### 2. Add-On Acquisitions

- Systematic identification and execution of tuck-in acquisitions in fragmented markets
- Typical platform company completes 2–4 add-on acquisitions during hold period
- Add-on criteria: geographic expansion, capability adjacency, customer base expansion, or capacity addition
- Dedicated integration playbook covering Day 1, 30/60/90-day, and full integration milestones
- Add-on acquisitions typically acquired at 5–7x EBITDA vs. platform entry multiples of 8–10x, creating immediate arbitrage

### 3. Management Professionalization

- Recruit and install institutional-caliber leadership where gaps exist (CEO, CFO, CRO)
- Formalize board governance — structured board meetings, committee oversight, independent directors where appropriate
- Implement management incentive plans aligned with equity value creation
- Build middle management bench — functional leaders in finance, HR, sales, and operations
- Transition founder-dependent businesses to professionally managed organizations

### 4. Commercial Excellence

- Revenue growth acceleration through sales force effectiveness, pricing optimization, and channel expansion
- Customer segmentation and targeting — focus resources on highest-value customer segments
- Service line expansion and cross-sell within existing customer relationships
- Geographic expansion — new offices, branches, or territories
- Marketing and brand development — invest in the company's go-to-market capabilities

## Return Targets

| Metric | Target Range |
|---|---|
| **Gross MOIC** | 2.5–3.0x |
| **Gross IRR** | 20–25% |
| **Hold Period** | 4–6 years |
| **Entry Multiple** | 7–10x EBITDA (sector-dependent) |

**Return attribution targets (approximate):**
- EBITDA growth (organic + add-on): 50–60% of return
- Debt paydown / deleveraging: 20–30% of return
- Multiple expansion: 10–20% of return (not relied upon in underwriting)

We do not underwrite to multiple expansion. We model exit at entry multiple as the base case and treat any expansion as upside. Our return math must work on EBITDA growth and deleveraging alone.

## What We Look For

**Recurring or contractual revenue.** We prioritize businesses where revenue is predictable — multi-year contracts, subscription models, repeat-purchase dynamics, or payor-driven demand. Minimum 60% recurring/contractual revenue preferred.

**Mission-critical services.** Services that customers cannot easily defer, switch, or bring in-house. Embedded in the customer's operations or compliance requirements.

**Fragmented markets with consolidation opportunity.** We look for industries with hundreds or thousands of small competitors where a well-capitalized platform can consolidate through disciplined add-on acquisitions. The presence of at least 5 identifiable tuck-in targets is a diligence gate.

**Defensible market positions.** Regulatory barriers, technical expertise, customer switching costs, network effects, or geographic density that protect the business from competitive erosion.

**Strong management teams.** We partner with management teams, not replace them. We look for operators who want to build and are motivated by equity upside and professional growth. Founder-CEO transitions are acceptable when a strong successor is identified or recruitable.

**Margin improvement runway.** Businesses with identifiable operational improvement levers — procurement, labor efficiency, SG&A rationalization, pricing optimization — that can be executed within the first 12–24 months.

---

## What We Avoid

**Commodity businesses.** Products or services with no differentiation, where competition is purely on price. Low barriers to entry and margin pressure from undifferentiated competition.

**Cyclical and capital-intensive businesses.** Businesses with significant revenue volatility driven by macroeconomic cycles, or that require heavy ongoing capital expenditure to maintain competitive position. Heavy manufacturing, construction, commodity materials.

**Regulatory cliff risk.** Businesses where a single regulatory change could materially impair the revenue model. We accept regulatory complexity (healthcare, environmental) where regulation creates barriers to entry, but avoid businesses dependent on a regulatory framework that is actively under review or likely to change adversely.

**Turnaround situations.** We do not invest in businesses requiring fundamental restructuring, bankruptcy emergence, or operational triage. We buy good businesses and make them better — we do not fix broken ones.

**Venture-stage businesses.** We require established revenue, proven unit economics, and a track record of profitability. We do not invest in pre-revenue, pre-profitability, or concept-stage companies regardless of growth potential.

**International-only operations.** Our operating playbook, sector expertise, and add-on acquisition capabilities are U.S.-focused. We will consider businesses with incidental international revenue but do not pursue companies with primarily non-U.S. operations.
