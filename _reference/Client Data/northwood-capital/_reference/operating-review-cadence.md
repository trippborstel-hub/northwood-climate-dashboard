# Operating Review Cadence

## Purpose

Northwood's portfolio monitoring framework is built around three objectives: early warning, accountability, and value creation acceleration. These are not equally weighted across a company's hold period.

**Early warning** is the primary function in the first 100 days post-close and during any period of underperformance. The cadence is calibrated to surface deterioration before it compounds — a margin trend that is visible in month-three data should not reach the board as a surprise at month-six.

**Accountability** is the steady-state function. Management teams at Meridian, NovaCare, Sterling, Trident, Clearview, Patriot, Keystone, and Pinnacle all operate against annual budgets and 100-day or value creation plans with specific milestones. The operating review cadence is the mechanism by which commitments made at close and at annual planning are tracked, tested, and enforced.

**Value creation acceleration** is the function that separates sponsor-backed performance from standalone performance. Deal leads and Operating Partners use the review cadence as the primary channel for deploying Northwood's functional expertise — pricing analytics, procurement leverage, M&A pipeline support, talent upgrade, and technology enablement — into the portfolio.

The cadence runs on three tiers. Each tier has a distinct scope, participant set, and output. They are not redundant — the weekly call surfaces operational signal, the monthly review processes financial outcome, and the quarterly board meeting sets strategic direction.

---

## Review Hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Weekly Management Operating Call

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Frequency | Weekly, every Monday or Tuesday |
| Duration | 45–60 minutes |
| Format | Video call; no deck required. Working agenda distributed 24 hours prior |

**Portco participants:** CEO, COO or VP Operations (if distinct), CFO or Controller (joins for financial sections; may be optional in early weeks)

**Northwood participants:** Deal lead (required). Operating Partner joins on a rotating basis — minimum two calls per month per company in their coverage

**Pre-read requirements:** None required for weekly calls. Deal lead reviews prior week's KPI flash (where implemented) before joining.

**Primary output:** Verbal alignment on the week's operational priorities, flagged risks, and near-term decisions requiring Northwood input. Deal lead logs key items in the company's deal file within 24 hours of the call. No formal memo required unless an issue is escalating.

**Scope:** Weekly calls are intentionally narrow. The agenda covers: (1) operational pace against the current month's plan, (2) any emerging issues in sales, operations, HR, or vendor relationships, (3) near-term decisions where management wants sponsor input, and (4) standing items specific to the company's current phase (e.g., integration progress for Keystone and Pinnacle, exit prep workstreams for Meridian).

The weekly call is not a financial review. If a management team is presenting slides or reviewing P&L on the weekly call, the cadence has drifted. Redirect to Tier 2.

---

### Tier 2 — Monthly Financial Review

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Frequency | Monthly, held between the 25th and last business day of each month |
| Duration | 90 minutes |
| Format | Structured agenda; CFO-led presentation of financial package |

**Portco participants:** CEO, CFO (presenter and owner), COO or division heads for relevant business lines

**Northwood participants:** Deal lead (required), Operating Partner (required for companies in active value creation phase or Amber/Red status), Northwood CFO or finance team member (for Fund II companies in first 12 months post-close)

**Pre-read requirements:** Full monthly financial package (see Section 5) delivered by portco CFO no later than the 20th of the following month. Deal lead reviews and annotates before the session. The 90-minute call is a discussion meeting, not a read-through.

**Primary output:** Deal lead memo — one to two pages covering financial performance versus plan, KPI status, flagged risks, and agreed follow-up actions with owners and deadlines. Memo filed in the company's deal file and distributed to the Northwood investment team. RAG rating updated following this review.

**Scope:** The monthly review is the primary financial accountability mechanism. It processes the prior month's P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow against budget, examines KPI trends, and surfaces any variance requiring explanation or intervention. Value creation milestone progress is reviewed here, not on the weekly call.

---

### Tier 3 — Quarterly Board Meeting

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Frequency | Quarterly — typically February, May, August, November |
| Duration | Half-day (3–4 hours) |
| Format | Formal board meeting with pre-distributed board package |

**Portco participants:** CEO, CFO, COO, division/segment heads, General Counsel (if applicable), any functional VP relevant to agenda items (e.g., Chief Revenue Officer for commercial strategy discussion)

**Northwood participants:** Deal lead (board seat), Operating Partner (board observer), one additional Northwood partner (rotating), outside board members where applicable

**Pre-read requirements:** Full board package distributed 5 business days prior. Package includes: quarterly financial review (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow vs. budget and prior year), KPI dashboard with trend data, value creation plan status, risk register update, and management's strategic discussion document for the quarter's agenda item.

**Primary output:** Formal board minutes. Formal resolutions where required. Updated value creation plan with revised milestones if warranted. Strategic decisions documented and assigned to owners.

**Scope:** The quarterly board meeting is the governance mechanism. It sets or resets strategic direction, reviews the value creation plan formally, addresses capital structure or M&A pipeline questions, and handles any governance or consent matters. The board meeting is not the place to surface a financial problem for the first time — problems identified in Tier 1 or Tier 2 should be resolved or on a resolution path before they reach the board.

## KPI Framework by Sector

The KPI framework defines what Northwood tracks at each tier for each sector. Sector-specific metrics are non-negotiable — generic EBITDA tracking without sector-specific leading indicators creates blind spots. A healthcare services company's EBITDA is a lagging indicator; its therapist utilization rate and payor mix shift are the leading indicators.

KPIs are reported in three cadences: Flash (weekly, abbreviated), Monthly (full package), and Quarterly (trended, with narrative).

## Monthly Financial Package Requirements

The monthly financial package is the portco CFO's primary accountability deliverable. It is due to the Northwood deal lead no later than the **20th calendar day of the following month** (i.e., the January package is due February 20th). Late delivery without advance notice is escalated to the deal lead's partner-level supervisor.

The package must include the following components. All financial statements are presented as actual vs. budget vs. prior year, with variances in dollar and percentage terms.

### Required Components

**1. Income Statement (P&L)**
- Monthly actual vs. budget vs. prior year
- Year-to-date actual vs. budget vs. prior year
- Trailing 12-month summary
- Bridge: budget-to-actual EBITDA variance by category (volume/price/cost — not one-line explanations)

**2. Balance Sheet**
- Month-end actual vs. prior month vs. prior year
- Working capital summary: accounts receivable aging, inventory (where applicable), accounts payable aging
- Total debt and cash position
- Net leverage ratio (Net Debt / LTM EBITDA)

**3. Cash Flow Statement**
- Monthly actual vs. budget
- YTD actual vs. budget
- Free cash flow (EBITDA minus capex minus change in working capital)
- Revolver availability (if applicable)

**4. KPI Dashboard**
- Sector-specific KPIs as defined in Section 3
- Current period, prior period, budget, and prior year columns
- Trend arrows or directional flags — no raw numbers only
- Deal lead may request a revised dashboard template if the portco format does not align with the Northwood sector framework

**5. Narrative Commentary**
The narrative is the CFO's analytical accountability, not a description of the table above it. It must cover three questions:

- **What happened?** A one-paragraph explanation of the material drivers of the month's performance — not a restatement of variances, but an explanation of cause.
- **What's at risk?** Forward-looking: risks that are visible but not yet in the numbers, covenant headroom, customer concentration risks, hiring gaps, regulatory calendar items.
- **What changed?** Any change to the company's internal forecast for the remainder of the year, including assumption changes and management actions taken or planned.

Narrative commentary is required even for on-plan months. "We were on plan" is not a narrative. An on-plan month has drivers — favorable utilization, strong bookings, lower-than-expected attrition — and these are the signal that makes the next month's variance interpretable.

**6. Value Creation Plan Milestone Update**
- Status of each active milestone: on track / at risk / delayed / complete
- Owner and next action for any at-risk milestone
- Updated completion date where revised

### Format and Submission

Packages are submitted as a PDF or structured document to the deal lead via the Northwood deal management system. Excel workbooks for financial statements are acceptable as supporting files but do not substitute for the consolidated package. The CFO owns the package — not the analyst or outside accountant.

## RAG Rating Protocol

Every Northwood portfolio company carries a RAG status: **Green**, **Amber**, or **Red**. RAG ratings are updated by the deal lead following each monthly financial review and are visible to the full investment team.

### Rating Definitions

**Green — On Plan or Better**
Financial and operational performance within acceptable variance of budget. Value creation plan milestones on track. No material risks flagged that are not already being managed. Management team stable and executing.

**Amber — Requires Attention**
One or more of the following applies: financial performance below budget by more than 10.0% on revenue or 15.0% on EBITDA for two or more consecutive months; a value creation milestone that is delayed by more than 30 days without a credible recovery plan; an emerging operational or market risk (margin compression, key person departure, customer loss) that has not been fully mitigated; or deal lead judgment that the company requires elevated monitoring even if the numbers are within tolerance.

**Red — Intervention Required**
One or more of the following applies: financial performance materially below budget with no credible recovery within the current fiscal year; covenant breach or breach risk within 90 days; management confidence issue; material legal, regulatory, or reputational risk; or an Amber situation that has not improved within 60 days of escalation.

### RAG Escalation Triggers

The following events trigger an automatic RAG review outside the monthly cycle:

- Any month where EBITDA is more than 20.0% below budget
- CFO or CEO departure
- Customer accounting for more than 10.0% of revenue giving notice of non-renewal or reduction
- Covenant waiver request
- Any OSHA recordable fatality or serious injury (Industrial Technology companies)
- Any payor audit, CMS investigation, or material claim denial (Healthcare companies)
- Any data breach or significant cybersecurity incident (Trident, Meridian)

RAG review triggered by these events is completed within five business days of the triggering event. Deal lead memo distributed to full investment team within 48 hours of completion.

### Amber Protocol

When a company is rated Amber:

1. Deal lead prepares a written Amber memo within five business days of the rating change. Memo covers: basis for rating, management's explanation, deal lead assessment of credibility of management's plan, and proposed monitoring enhancements.
2. Reporting frequency increases from monthly to bi-weekly for financial KPIs. The full monthly package remains due on the 20th; a mid-month KPI flash is added by the 5th.
3. Operating Partner engagement increases — minimum four calls per month vs. standard two. Operating Partner is present at all monthly reviews while Amber status is active.
4. Deal lead presents Amber company at each investment team meeting until status resolves.

**Patriot current Amber actions:** Andrew Walsh is providing bi-weekly margin recovery updates. Operating Partner coverage for Patriot is informal (no dedicated Operating Partner coverage per current model — Andrew Walsh is carrying the operational engagement directly with Patriot CFO and COO). This is noted as a resource gap for Amber situations in Business Services.

### Red Protocol

When a company is rated Red:

1. Remediation committee convened within 10 business days: deal lead, investment team partner, Northwood CFO, and relevant Operating Partner.
2. Full management review — deal lead and Operating Partner conduct on-site visits to assess management team, operations, and financial position firsthand.
3. Management is required to present a formal remediation plan to the remediation committee within 30 days of Red rating. Plan must include specific actions, owners, and measurable milestones with dates.
4. Reporting moves to weekly: weekly KPI flash, bi-weekly call with management and deal lead, monthly formal review with full investment team.
5. Northwood evaluates whether additional resources are required: interim CFO support, operating consultant engagement, or management replacement.
6. Red status triggers a formal LP notification review — General Counsel and Fund CFO determine whether LPA requires notification.

## Value Creation Plan Tracking

Every Northwood portfolio company operates against two planning documents: (1) the **100-day plan** covering the immediate post-close period, and (2) the **Value Creation Plan (VCP)** covering the full hold period.

The operating review cadence is the mechanism by which both are tracked. They are not separate from the financial review — they are integrated into it.

### 100-Day Plan Tracking

100-day plans are live for Keystone (closed recently) and Pinnacle (closed recently). For companies beyond the 100-day period, the 100-day plan is closed and archived.

The 100-day plan milestone tracker is a standing agenda item on every monthly review for any company in the 100-day period. The tracker covers:

- Milestone name and owner (management, not Northwood)
- Original target completion date
- Current status: on track / at risk / complete / delayed
- For at-risk or delayed items: specific blocker and revised completion date

Northwood does not own 100-day plan milestones. Management owns them. Northwood's role is to track, apply pressure on delays, and remove blockers that require sponsor access (banking relationships, vendor introductions, HR support).

### VCP Tracking

The Value Creation Plan is a multi-year document that maps the investment thesis to specific operational initiatives, financial targets, and milestones. It is reviewed formally at each quarterly board meeting.

Monthly reviews include a VCP milestone update as a required component of the financial package (see Section 5, Item 6). The monthly update is abbreviated — status flags and variance commentary, not a full VCP re-presentation.

VCP milestones fall into categories: revenue growth initiatives (pricing, new products, new customers, geographic expansion), margin initiatives (cost reduction, procurement, labor efficiency), and platform initiatives (M&A, technology, organizational design). Each milestone has a financial impact estimate from underwriting — variance from that estimate is tracked as an input to valuation reassessment at each annual planning cycle.

### Annual Budget Cycle Integration

The operating review cadence connects to the annual budget process in October and November. Management teams present annual budgets at the November or December board meeting. Northwood reviews the budget against the VCP — the budget must be traceable to VCP initiatives, not constructed bottom-up from prior year actuals alone.

Following budget approval, KPI targets for the following year are updated in all reporting templates. RAG rating thresholds are reset against the approved budget. The deal lead is responsible for ensuring that the budget and VCP are aligned and that the monthly review cadence for the following year reflects any changes in KPI mix or reporting emphasis.

## Escalation and Intervention Protocol

Performance deterioration requires a staged response. The response is calibrated to severity and trajectory — not every miss triggers the same intervention. The protocol below defines the stages.

### Stage 1 — Increased Reporting Frequency

**Trigger:** One month with EBITDA more than 10.0% below budget, or any month with book-to-bill below 0.9x (Industrial), therapist turnover above 30% annualized (Healthcare), or bill/pay spread compression of more than 200 basis points in a single month (Staffing).

**Response:**
- Deal lead contacts CEO within 48 hours of package receipt to discuss the variance.
- Mid-month KPI flash added to the reporting cadence.
- Deal lead memo distributed to investment team flagging the miss.
- RAG rating reviewed — deal lead determines whether Amber criteria are met.
- No formal escalation to Operating Partner unless deal lead judgment warrants.

### Stage 2 — Operating Partner Escalation

**Trigger:** Two consecutive months at Stage 1 conditions, or any month meeting Amber criteria as defined in Section 6.

**Response:**
- Amber rating assigned.
- Operating Partner engagement elevated per Amber protocol (Section 6).
- Written Amber memo to investment team.
- Bi-weekly KPI flash and bi-weekly check-in call with management.
- Operating Partner conducts an on-site visit within 30 days of Amber designation (or video-based diagnostic session for companies without an assigned Operating Partner).
- Deal lead and Operating Partner jointly evaluate whether the issue is a management execution problem, an external market problem, or an investment thesis problem. This distinction drives the intervention design.
