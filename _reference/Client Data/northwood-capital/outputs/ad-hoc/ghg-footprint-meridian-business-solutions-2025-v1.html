Methodology & Standards

This footprint follows the GHG Protocol Corporate Accounting and Reporting Standard (Revised Edition) and the GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance (2015). Organizational boundary is set using the operational control approach. Scope 2 is reported using the location-based method; market-based reporting is noted as a data gap (no utility-specific emission factors, RECs, or contractual instruments on file).

Emission factors: Stationary combustion — EPA MRR 40 CFR 98 Subpart C. Mobile combustion — EPA Emission Factors Hub (2024). Electricity — EPA eGRID 2022 subregional emission rates (total output, CO2e). GWP values — IPCC AR5.

Reporting year: Calendar year 2025 (Jan 1 – Dec 31, 2025).

Data Quality Note: Electricity and natural gas data for 11 of 16 facilities sourced from utility bills (Grade A). Five facilities (Philadelphia, Charlotte, Denver, Seattle, Phoenix) sourced from accounting exports (Grade B). Fleet gasoline reported for 5 facilities only — see discrepancy note below.
Discrepancy — Fleet Vehicles: The climate questionnaire submitted by CFO Patricia Wells states "no fleet vehicles." However, the carbon data collection template reports fleet gasoline consumption at 5 facilities (3,200 gallons total). This analysis includes the fleet data as reported. Recommend reconciling with management before finalizing.
Data Gap — Refrigerants: The questionnaire confirms all HVAC systems use R-410A refrigerant (GWP = 2,088). No refrigerant charge or recharge data was submitted. Fugitive emissions from refrigerant leakage are excluded from this footprint. At a typical commercial leak rate of 5%, even modest charges could add 10–50+ tCO2e. Recommend requesting HVAC service records for next iteration.
Data Gap — Scope 2 Market-Based: No utility-specific emission factors, green tariff contracts, or renewable energy certificates (RECs) on file. Market-based Scope 2 cannot be calculated. Location-based method is the sole Scope 2 figure reported here.

Recommended Actions

  1. Reconcile fleet gasoline discrepancy — Clarify with Patricia Wells (CFO) whether the 3,200 gallons reported in the carbon template represent company-owned fleet or employee reimbursements (which would be Scope 3).
  2. Obtain refrigerant data — Request HVAC service records and R-410A recharge logs from facilities management. Even at a conservative 5% annual leak rate, fugitive emissions from 16 facilities with R-410A could be material.
  3. Investigate market-based Scope 2 options — Request utility supplier names and any existing green power or REC agreements. Relevant for exit buyers with net-zero commitments.
  4. Validate shared-building allocations — New York (MBS-005) and Boston (MBS-006) have shared building meters. Confirm allocation methodology is defensible.
  5. Establish baseline year — If Meridian intends to set reduction targets, CY2025 is the logical baseline. Document methodology choices now for continuity.
