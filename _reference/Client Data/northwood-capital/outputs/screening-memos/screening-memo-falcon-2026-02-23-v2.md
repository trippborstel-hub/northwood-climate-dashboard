### Company Overview

NETL is a Houston-based environmental analytical testing and remediation support services company founded in 1998 by Tom Bradley. The Company operates six NELAP/ISO 17025-accredited laboratories across Texas, Louisiana, and Oklahoma, employing approximately 450 people including 120+ credentialed chemists and laboratory scientists. NETL serves industrial, energy, and municipal clients with compliance-driven testing that is non-discretionary and regulated — approximately 80% of revenue is recurring under multi-year MSAs.

---

### Market

The U.S. environmental testing market is approximately $12B, growing 6–8% annually through 2030 [CIM; consistent with IBISWorld sector estimates]. Growth is driven by EPA PFAS drinking water standards (finalized 2024, 5-year compliance window), state-level PFAS legislation (30+ states), and infrastructure spending ($10B allocated for PFAS remediation under the IIJA). The market is highly fragmented — top 10 players hold <25% share — with hundreds of regional and local laboratories. Several PE-backed platforms (Eurofins, Montrose Environmental) have executed successful consolidation strategies, validating the buy-and-build thesis.

### Investment Highlights

1. **Regulatory-driven recurring revenue with multi-year demand visibility.** 80% recurring revenue under MSAs. EPA PFAS standards create a compliance mandate that public water systems must meet within 5 years — this is not discretionary spending. PFAS testing (~15% of current revenue) has grown >40% annually and management projects $25M+ within 3 years [CIM, Mgmt Estimate].

2. **Highly fragmented market with proven consolidation playbook.** $12B market, <25% held by top 10. Management has identified 10+ regional laboratory targets at $3M–$10M revenue [CIM]. Environmental testing consolidation is well-precedented — add-on acquisitions bring accreditations, customer relationships, and geographic density. Target add-on multiples of 4–6x vs. platform entry of 8–10x.

3. **PFAS testing first-mover advantage.** $4.5M investment (2020–2024) in LC-MS/MS analytical capability. Capacity for 50,000+ PFAS samples annually [CIM]. Many smaller regional labs lack this capability — NETL can capture share organically and acquire labs that need PFAS testing capacity.

### Key Risks

1. **Hidden customer concentration — Gulf Petrochemical Corp subsidiaries (22% combined revenue)** — *High*

   The CIM states "no single customer represents more than 8% of revenue." However, three of the top 10 customers — Gulf Petrochemical Testing (7.5%), GPC Environmental Services (5.8%), and Gulf Coast Industrial Inspection (5.1%) — are subsidiaries of Gulf Petrochemical Corp, representing approximately 22% combined concentration [Spine, corporate family analysis].

   **This is the critical diligence item.** The misrepresentation itself is a concern — it signals either a deliberate omission by the seller/advisor or a lack of rigor in CIM preparation. Either raises questions about what else may be misstated.

   At 22%, the concentration is below the 25% trigger in our flag-for-review criteria and well below the 40% effective pass threshold. The risk is manageable if GPC's contracts are long-tenured, diversified across business units, and not tied to a single decision-maker. But we need to verify: (a) contract terms and renewal history across all three GPC entities, (b) whether GPC's business is itself concentrated in upstream oil & gas (which would compound the geographic concentration risk), and (c) whether there are other hidden related-party concentrations in the remaining customer base.

2. **Geographic concentration — TX, LA, OK = 75% of revenue** — *Medium*

   Gulf Coast concentration creates exposure to regional energy cycles, hurricane disruption, and state regulatory changes. Mitigated by: (a) compliance-driven demand that persists through economic cycles, (b) add-on acquisition strategy explicitly targets geographic diversification, and (c) NELAP accreditations in 3 additional states (AL, MS, AR) provide organic expansion runway [CIM]. Diligence should stress-test a Gulf Coast downturn scenario with 15–20% revenue impairment to energy-sector customers.

3. **Founder/CEO transition risk** — *Medium*

   Tom Bradley (age 62, 28 years as CEO) is the primary relationship holder for key industrial and energy clients [CIM]. CFO Lisa Nakamura (7 years, joined from Pace Analytical) has professionalized finance. VP Laboratory Operations (Dr. James Wu, 12 years) and VP Business Development (Sarah Hernandez, 9 years) provide bench depth. CEO available for 12–18 month transition [CIM]. Risk is manageable if client relationships are institution-facing (MSAs, procurement-driven) rather than personal-relationship-driven. Diligence must map which customer relationships are CEO-dependent vs. operationally embedded.

**Flagged items:**

- **Small for platform** ($14.2M EBITDA, within $10–15M flag range): Path to $20M+ within 24 months is credible. Projected FY 2026 EBITDA of $15.0M [CIM, Mgmt Estimate]. PFAS segment growing >40% annually. One add-on at $3–5M EBITDA would clear the threshold. Valuation should reflect smaller scale — target 7–8x per screening criteria guidance for sub-$15M EBITDA platforms.

- **Valuation at top of range**: Implied multiple of 8.1x–9.9x. Industrial Technology sector guardrail is 6–9x. The 9.9x top end exceeds our ceiling. Environmental testing with 80% recurring revenue arguably warrants a premium within the industrial range, but we should not bid above 9x absent exceptional diligence findings. At 8.5x on $14.2M = $120.7M EV, within the indicated range.
