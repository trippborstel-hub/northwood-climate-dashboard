## Pass Summary

**Pass reason:** Valuation. The seller's indicated enterprise value of $196.0M represents a 14.0x multiple of LTM adjusted EBITDA ($14.0M). This exceeds our target entry range of 8–10x for business services / logistics and is incompatible with our return model.

---

## What We Liked

- **Asset-light model:** Freight brokerage with no fleet ownership generates attractive free cash flow characteristics. Low capex, high cash conversion.
- **Proprietary technology:** In-house TMS platform built over 8 years provides competitive advantage in load matching and rate optimization. Technology differentiates NFC from traditional brokerage competitors.
- **Growing end market:** U.S. freight brokerage market is ~$90B and growing 5–7% annually, driven by shipper outsourcing trends and supply chain complexity.
- **Strong management team:** Experienced CEO and CRO with deep carrier and shipper relationships. Institutional-quality operations.

---

## What Killed It

14.0x is financial sponsor pricing for a business with 11.2% EBITDA margins. Our return math requires 8–10x entry for this risk profile.

**Return analysis at seller's ask:**

| Scenario | Entry Multiple | Exit Multiple | EBITDA CAGR | Gross MOIC | Gross IRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base case at seller ask | 14.0x | 14.0x | 10% | 1.6x | ~10% |
| Our target return | 14.0x | 14.0x | 10% | *needs 2.5x* | *needs ~20%* |

At 14.0x entry and exit-at-entry-multiple (our standard assumption), we cannot achieve our target return even with aggressive EBITDA growth assumptions. The return only works with significant multiple expansion at exit — and we do not underwrite to expansion.

**Additional concerns at this valuation:**

- EBITDA margins of 11.2% are thin for a logistics business. Freight market cyclicality creates margin compression risk in downturns.
- Revenue is correlated with freight market volumes and pricing. The freight market is currently in a recovery cycle — peak-cycle earnings may be embedded in the current EBITDA.
- Asset-light businesses command higher multiples, but 14.0x reflects growth equity pricing, not control buyout pricing.
