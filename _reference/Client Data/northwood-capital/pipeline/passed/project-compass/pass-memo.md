## Pass Summary

**Pass reason:** Regulatory risk. CMS has proposed a rule change to its physician fee schedule methodology that could reduce per-claim reimbursement for RCM service providers by 15–20%. The final rule is expected in Q3 2026. At a 13.0x entry multiple, the downside scenario under the proposed rule change effectively eliminates our return thesis.

## What We Liked

- **High recurring revenue (95%):** Multi-year contracts with healthcare providers create exceptional revenue visibility. Revenue is predictable and growing.
- **Exceptional client retention (97%):** Five-year retention rate of 97% demonstrates deep client integration and high switching costs. Clients rarely change RCM providers due to implementation complexity and data migration risk.
- **Technology platform with automation capabilities:** Proprietary software with AI-assisted coding and automated denial management. Technology differentiation drives operational efficiency and margin expansion.
- **Scalable financial model:** 22.1% EBITDA margins with identified improvement runway. Technology leverage allows claim volume growth without proportional headcount increase.

---

## What Killed It

CMS proposed rule could reduce per-claim reimbursement by 15–20%. In the downside scenario, this impact eliminates the return thesis at 13.0x entry.

**Regulatory analysis:**

CMS published a proposed rule in November 2025 that would modify the physician fee schedule methodology for billing and claims management services. The proposed changes would:

1. **Reduce allowed charges for certain billing service codes** — Estimated 15–20% reduction in per-claim reimbursement for RCM service providers
2. **Modify the incident-to billing rules** — Could reduce the volume of claims processed through RCM platforms
3. **Increase documentation requirements** — Would increase the operational cost per claim for RCM providers

**Impact analysis at proposed rule severity:**

| Scenario | Revenue Impact | EBITDA Impact | MOIC at 13.0x Entry |
|---|---|---|---|
| No rule change (base) | — | — | 2.3x |
| Moderate impact (-10% per-claim) | -$5.2M | -$3.8M | 1.6x |
| Full proposed impact (-20% per-claim) | -$10.4M | -$7.5M | 1.1x |

At full proposed impact, EBITDA would decline from $11.5M to approximately $4.0M, making a 13.0x entry virtually unrecoverable regardless of hold-period growth. Even the moderate scenario compresses returns below our target.

**Additional valuation concern:**

13.0x is aggressive pricing for healthcare services RCM, even without the regulatory overhang. Our healthcare services entry range is 8–11x. At 13.0x, the seller is pricing in growth and technology premium that is at risk from the regulatory change.
