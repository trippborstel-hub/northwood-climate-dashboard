## Relationship History

**Initial Contact — Q2 2024 (Industry Conference)**

James Harrison met the founder of Apex Automation Technologies at a manufacturing technology conference in Q2 2024. The founder (age 58 at that time) expressed interest in identifying a private equity partner who understood industrial technology and could support the business through its next growth phase. He was not actively running a process but indicated a general timeline of 2–3 years for a formal sale.

James established a personal rapport based on shared background in industrial operations and manufacturing. The conversation covered the founder's vision for the business, the growth in robotics integration demand, and what the founder was looking for in a partner (operational expertise, not just financial engineering).

**Follow-Up Contact — Q4 2024**

James followed up with a phone call in Q4 2024. Key updates:

- Business continued to perform well. Founder indicated revenue growth of ~10% YoY.
- The founder was exploring expansion into robotics integration for automotive manufacturing clients — a new end market for the business.
- No advisor retained. The founder expressed preference for identifying the right partner through personal relationships rather than a formal auction process.
- Timeline remained H2 2027 for a formal transaction, but the founder noted he was "open to an earlier conversation if the right partner comes along."

**Most Recent Contact — December 2025**

James had an informal check-in with the founder in December 2025. Key updates:

- Robotics integration expansion proceeding well. The founder described initial contracts with two Tier 1 automotive suppliers for production line automation.
- EBITDA margins stable at approximately 17%, consistent with prior observations.
- The founder remains committed to H2 2027 timeline. He has not retained a sell-side advisor. He mentioned having exploratory conversations with "a couple of firms" but no formal process.
- James reiterated Northwood's interest and our approach to industrial technology investments. The founder was receptive and described Northwood's profile as "the kind of partner I'm looking for."

## Business Observations

### Company Overview

Apex Automation Technologies provides industrial automation solutions, including system design, integration, programming, and ongoing support for manufacturing facilities. The Company specializes in robotic welding, material handling automation, and production line optimization for industrial clients.

### Performance Assessment

| Metric | Observation |
|---|---|
| **Revenue trajectory** | ~10% YoY growth, consistent over past 2 years |
| **EBITDA margin** | ~17%, stable |
| **End markets** | Manufacturing, automotive, food & beverage processing |
| **Growth drivers** | Reshoring, labor shortages, Industry 4.0 adoption, automotive EV transitions |
| **Competitive position** | Regional leader with strong reputation for execution and reliability |

### Strategic Observations

- **Robotics integration expansion:** The move into automotive robotics integration is strategically significant. Automotive is a large addressable market ($5B+ in automation integration), and Tier 1 supplier contracts provide reference-ability and recurring revenue potential from aftermarket support and upgrades.

- **Labor shortage tailwind:** Manufacturing labor shortages are structural, not cyclical. Companies are increasingly investing in automation as an alternative to hiring — a secular demand driver for Apex's services. This is the same thesis that underpins our Sterling Precision Manufacturing investment.

- **Fragmented market:** Industrial automation integration is fragmented, with hundreds of regional system integrators. A well-capitalized platform could consolidate through geographic and capability-adjacent add-on acquisitions.

- **Founder transition:** The founder is 58 and planning ahead. He has no identified internal successor, which creates both an opportunity (founder motivated to find the right partner) and a risk (CEO transition execution). Northwood's track record of managing founder-led transitions (Sterling Precision, Clearview Environmental) would be a differentiator.

## Preliminary Thesis

If and when the founder initiates a process, the Northwood thesis would be:

1. **Platform acquisition** at 8–10x EBITDA ($120.0M–$150.0M EV)
2. **Add-on consolidation** of 2–3 regional system integrators at 5–7x, expanding geographic coverage and end-market exposure
3. **Operational improvement** through centralized project management, procurement leverage, and recurring service contract development
4. **Management professionalization** — recruit institutional CEO/COO during transition period, retain and incentivize technical leadership
5. **Portfolio synergies** — potential cross-referral and knowledge sharing with Sterling Precision and Pinnacle Testing
