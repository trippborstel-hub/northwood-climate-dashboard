## Key Model Risks

### 1. Founder/CEO Transition

The model assumes a successful leadership transition from James Whitfield (CEO/Founder, age 55) with no material revenue disruption. Whitfield is the primary relationship holder for the top 10 clients (28% of revenue concentrated in top 3). The 20% rollover and consulting agreement mitigate but do not eliminate transition risk. Downside case models a scenario where transition friction triggers customer churn and flat revenue.

**Mitigation:** Structured 18-month co-management period. CTO (Michael Okafor) and CFO (Rebecca Santos) are strong internal succession candidates. Whitfield's 20% rollover provides alignment through exit.

### 2. Cybersecurity Talent Shortage

EBITDA margin expansion from 16.4% to 18.6% depends on scaling the cybersecurity MDR practice, which requires hiring 15–20 additional security engineers over the hold period. Atlanta's cybersecurity talent market is competitive (unemployment <1% for certified security professionals). Failure to hire compresses the margin improvement to 17.0–17.5% and reduces exit EBITDA by $1–2M.

**Mitigation:** Remote-first hiring model for Tier 1 SOC analysts. Apprenticeship program for career-changers. Retention through equity incentive participation and competitive compensation.

### 3. Customer Concentration

Top 3 clients represent 28% of revenue ($16.2M). Loss of the largest client (~$6M ARR) would reduce EBITDA by approximately $1.5–2.0M at current contribution margins. Customer concentration is partially mitigated by the nature of managed services contracts — 3-year terms with 12-month notice requirements create revenue visibility and time to replace.

### 4. Technology Platform Risk

CloudFirst operates on a legacy PSA/RMM technology stack (ConnectWise Manage + Datto RMM). Migration to a modern platform (e.g., Kaseya, Ninja) is budgeted in growth capex ($1.5M Years 1–2) but carries execution risk. Failed or delayed migration could disrupt service delivery and compress margins temporarily.

### 5. Interest Rate Sensitivity

At 3.5x entry leverage and SOFR + 425bps, a 100bps increase in base rates would increase annual interest expense by ~$0.3M in Year 1, reducing levered FCF by ~10%. The model assumes a stable rate environment. In a rising rate scenario, deleveraging accelerates the reduction of interest rate exposure — by Year 3, interest expense is <$2.5M regardless of rate movements.
