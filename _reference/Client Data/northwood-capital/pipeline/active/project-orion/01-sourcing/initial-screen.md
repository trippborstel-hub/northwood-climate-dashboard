## Preliminary Assessment

**Sector fit:** Strong. IT managed services and cybersecurity align directly with Northwood's Business Services / Technology Services sector focus. The Company is a direct analog to the Trident IT Solutions thesis from Fund I — same sector, similar dynamics, strong recurring revenue, add-on opportunity.

**Strategic overlap with portfolio:** Potential synergy knowledge-sharing with Trident IT Solutions (Fund I). Mark Sullivan leads both relationships, providing deep sector pattern recognition. CloudFirst is not a direct competitor to Trident — different geography (Atlanta vs. Dallas), complementary capabilities.

**Return potential:** Base case model indicates ~22% IRR / 2.8x MOIC over a 5-year hold, consistent with Fund II return targets. Upside is driven by cybersecurity revenue acceleration and add-on acquisition arbitrage (platform at 9-10x, add-ons at 5-7x).

**Process dynamics:** Targeted process with 3-4 bidders. Raymond James is a relationship intermediary — Northwood has completed one prior transaction with their mid-market technology practice. Competitive dynamics are manageable given the smaller deal size and the founder's preference for a partner willing to support continued growth rather than maximizing upfront valuation.

## Screening Decision

**ADVANCE** — Proceed to preliminary due diligence. Request management presentation and data room access.

**Rationale:** CloudFirst Technologies fits Northwood's core investment thesis — highly recurring IT services business in a fragmented market with strong add-on consolidation opportunity and cybersecurity growth tailwind. The adjusted EBITDA is below the $10M platform minimum but passes on an adjusted basis with clear line of sight to scale. The deal team's sector expertise (Trident IT Solutions, ShieldPoint Cyber) provides differentiated diligence capability and value creation playbook.
