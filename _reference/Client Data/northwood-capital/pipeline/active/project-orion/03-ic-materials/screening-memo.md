### Company Overview

CloudFirst Technologies is a provider of IT managed services and cybersecurity solutions to mid-market enterprises, headquartered in Atlanta, GA. Founded in 2008 by James Whitfield, the Company serves 118 clients with approximately 225 employees across the Southeastern U.S. CloudFirst operates an in-house Security Operations Center and delivers integrated managed IT and cybersecurity from a single platform — a differentiator vs. most sub-scale regional MSPs.

---

### Market

The U.S. IT managed services market exceeds $150 billion and is growing at 8-10% annually, driven by IT complexity, cybersecurity threat escalation, talent scarcity, and regulatory compliance requirements. The market is highly fragmented — over 40,000 MSPs in the U.S., the vast majority with fewer than 50 employees. Consolidation is accelerating, with PE-backed platforms actively acquiring sub-scale providers at 5-7x EBITDA.

### Investment Highlights

1. **85% recurring revenue with 112% NRR** — highly visible, expanding revenue base on multi-year managed services contracts with strong upsell dynamics through cybersecurity cross-sell.
2. **Cybersecurity growth tailwind** — fastest-growing segment (25% of revenue), in-house SOC capability differentiates CloudFirst from sub-scale MSPs, and cybersecurity demand is structurally increasing.
3. **Fragmented market with identified add-on targets** — 3 LOI-ready add-on targets at 5-7x would accelerate geographic expansion and create meaningful multiple arbitrage against 9-10x platform entry.

---

### Key Risks

1. **Customer concentration (top 3 = 28% of revenue)** — No single customer exceeds 12%, but the top 3 collectively represent meaningful concentration. Multi-year contracts provide near-term protection. — *Medium*
2. **Founder transition risk** — CEO/Founder James Whitfield is the primary relationship holder for top clients. Willing to roll 20% and continue, but no formal succession plan exists. — *High*
3. **Cybersecurity talent scarcity** — Atlanta market is competitive for cybersecurity professionals. CTO Michael Okafor is a recruiting magnet but is himself a key-person risk. — *Medium*

**Flagged items:** (1) Small for platform — $9.5M Adj. EBITDA is below $10M minimum. Passes on adjusted basis with projected $10.5M and clear path to $20M+ through organic growth + add-ons. Entry valuation at higher end for size — target entry below 10x. (2) Founder/CEO transition risk — flagged per criteria. Requires structured transition plan as LOI condition.
