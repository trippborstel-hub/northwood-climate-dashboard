## Section 2: Company Overview

### Business Description

CloudFirst Technologies is a provider of IT managed services and cybersecurity solutions to mid-market enterprises (100-2,000 employees) in the Southeastern United States. The Company operates through two integrated business lines: IT Managed Services (75% of revenue) and Managed Cybersecurity (25% of revenue).

Founded in 2008 by James Whitfield in Atlanta, GA, CloudFirst has grown from a two-person IT consulting shop into a full-service technology partner serving 118 clients with approximately 225 employees. The Company operates from its Atlanta headquarters with satellite offices in Charlotte, NC and Nashville, TN.

### Revenue Model

CloudFirst's revenue model is contract-based with high recurring characteristics:

- **Managed Services Contracts (75% of revenue):** Multi-year agreements (average 3-year term) priced per-user or per-device. Monthly recurring revenue with annual escalators of 3-5%. Services include 24/7 infrastructure monitoring, cloud management, help desk support, and network operations.
- **Cybersecurity Subscriptions (25% of revenue):** Annual subscription contracts with auto-renewal. Tiered packaging (Essential, Professional, Enterprise) priced per-user. Services include managed detection and response (MDR), endpoint protection, vulnerability management, and compliance-as-a-service.
- **Project Revenue (15% of total):** Non-recurring consulting, migration, and implementation projects. While lower margin, project work often converts to recurring managed services contracts.

Total recurring revenue is 85% of the base. Net revenue retention of 112% reflects strong upsell and expansion dynamics — primarily cybersecurity cross-sell into existing managed services clients, user growth within existing accounts, and contract tier upgrades.

## Section 3: Market & Competitive Landscape

### Market Size and Growth

The U.S. IT managed services market exceeds $150 billion in annual spend. The addressable market for CloudFirst — mid-market enterprises (100-2,000 employees) in the Southeastern U.S. requiring integrated managed IT and cybersecurity — is estimated at $8-10 billion (SAM).

Market growth is projected at 8-10% annually through 2030, driven by:

- **IT complexity acceleration:** Multi-cloud environments, remote/hybrid workforce infrastructure, and expanding SaaS application portfolios are exceeding the capacity of in-house IT teams at mid-market companies.
- **Cybersecurity threat escalation:** Ransomware attacks increased 68% YoY in 2025, with mid-market companies increasingly targeted due to less mature security programs. Average cost of a breach for a mid-market company now exceeds $3.5 million.
- **Talent scarcity:** The U.S. has approximately 750,000 unfilled cybersecurity positions. Mid-market companies cannot compete for this talent against large enterprises and technology companies. Outsourcing is the only viable option for most.
- **Regulatory compliance:** HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOC 2, CMMC, and state-level privacy regulations require specialized compliance capabilities that most mid-market IT departments lack.

### Market Structure

The IT managed services sector is one of the most fragmented in all of business services. Over 40,000 MSPs operate in the United States, with the vast majority below $10M in revenue. The market is consolidating rapidly — PE-backed platforms are acquiring sub-scale MSPs to build regional and national platforms.

Recent consolidation activity:

| Acquirer | Target Profile | Multiple | Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| ECI-backed Ntiva | Regional MSP ($15M rev) | ~10x EBITDA | 2025 |
| TA Associates / Dataprise | East Coast MSP ($12M rev) | ~9x EBITDA | 2024 |
| Madison Dearborn / Pax8 | Cloud marketplace + MSP enablement | 12x+ EBITDA | 2024 |
| Various PE platforms | Sub-scale MSPs ($3-8M rev) | 5-7x EBITDA | 2024-2025 |

The pattern is clear: platform-level MSPs (>$50M revenue, PE-backed) are acquiring at 9-11x, while sub-scale MSPs ($3-15M revenue) transact at 5-7x. This creates a structural multiple arbitrage opportunity for buy-and-build strategies.

### Competitive Positioning

CloudFirst occupies a defensible position in the mid-market MSP tier. Its integrated managed services + cybersecurity capability with an in-house SOC differentiates it from the thousands of sub-scale MSPs that offer managed services only. The Company is smaller than national platforms (Dataprise, Logically) but offers deeper local relationships and comparable technical capability.

Key barriers to entry in CloudFirst's segment:
- SOC buildout requires $3-5M investment and 12-18 months to operationalize
- Cybersecurity talent is scarce — Okafor's team and reputation are not replicable quickly
- Multi-year client contracts create 3-year switching cost barriers
- Compliance certifications (SOC 2 Type II audited) take 12+ months to achieve

### Disruption Assessment

AI and automation are augmenting, not displacing, managed services providers. AI-powered monitoring, automated remediation, and predictive analytics improve MSP economics by increasing technician leverage. CloudFirst's CTO has a roadmap to integrate AI-augmented threat detection into the SOC — this is an opportunity, not a threat. Risk: Low within hold period.

## Section 5: Investment Thesis

### Thesis Pillar 1: Recurring Revenue Platform with Structural Growth

**What we believe:** CloudFirst's 85% recurring revenue base with 112% NRR provides a predictable, compounding growth engine that is undervalued at current scale.

**Evidence:**
- 85% of revenue is contracted MRR on multi-year agreements (avg 3-year term)
- NRR of 112% — clients are expanding spend annually through user growth, service upgrades, and cybersecurity adoption
- 94% gross retention — client losses are attributable to M&A and business closure, not competitive displacement
- Revenue has compounded at ~15% annually for 3 years with improving margins

**Why we're right:** The combination of high recurring revenue, strong NRR, and low churn creates a compounding flywheel that the current owner cannot fully capitalize on due to limited capital and institutional infrastructure. A PE-backed platform can accelerate the flywheel through sales force investment, geographic expansion, and marketing — levers Whitfield has not deployed.

**Value creation mechanism:** Sustained 10-12% organic revenue growth flows to EBITDA at incrementally higher margins due to operating leverage on the recurring base. $5-7M in incremental EBITDA from organic growth alone over 5 years.

**Key assumption to monitor:** NRR must remain above 105%. If cross-sell and upsell dynamics deteriorate, the organic growth engine weakens.

### Thesis Pillar 2: Cybersecurity Growth Accelerant

**What we believe:** CloudFirst's cybersecurity practice is an undermonetized asset that will become the primary growth and margin driver during our hold period.

**Evidence:**
- Cybersecurity revenue has grown from $0 to $14.5M in 6 years — fastest-growing segment
- Cybersecurity gross margins (~55%) are 1,500bps above managed services (~40%)
- Only 40% of existing managed services clients have adopted cybersecurity — significant whitespace
- U.S. cybersecurity services market growing 12-15% annually, driven by threat escalation
- In-house SOC capability is a meaningful differentiator vs. sub-scale MSPs

**Why we're right:** Most mid-market MSPs rely on third-party SOC providers (outsourced security monitoring), which compresses margins and limits differentiation. CloudFirst's in-house SOC, built by a former Fortune 500 CISO, is a genuine moat. We have seen this pattern at Trident IT Solutions — the cybersecurity capability commands premium pricing and deepens client stickiness.

**Value creation mechanism:** Increasing cybersecurity penetration from 40% to 70% of the client base generates $8-10M in incremental recurring revenue at 55%+ gross margins. Blended EBITDA margin expansion of 200-300bps from mix shift alone.

**Key assumption to monitor:** Michael Okafor (CTO) must be retained. The cybersecurity practice is built around his credibility, technical vision, and recruiting capability.

### Thesis Pillar 3: Add-On Driven Geographic Expansion

**What we believe:** The highly fragmented IT managed services market offers substantial consolidation opportunity at favorable valuations, and CloudFirst's platform capabilities create meaningful integration value.

**Evidence:**
- Over 40,000 MSPs in the U.S., vast majority below $10M revenue
- 3 add-on targets identified with LOI-ready profiles ($5-8M revenue, $0.7-1.2M EBITDA)
- Add-on acquisition multiples: 5-7x EBITDA vs. 9-10x platform entry — immediate arbitrage
- PE-backed MSP platforms are actively consolidating, validating the playbook
- Northwood has executed this exact strategy at Trident IT Solutions (2 successful add-ons)

**Why we're right:** Northwood's sector expertise — having built Trident IT Solutions through the same buy-and-build playbook — gives us a differentiated execution edge. We know the integration challenges (technical stack migration, client communication, technician retention), the timeline, and the value creation drivers. We are not learning on the job.

**Value creation mechanism:** 2-3 add-on acquisitions at 5-7x contribute $2-4M of incremental EBITDA. At a blended platform exit multiple of 10x+, this creates $20-40M of value from multiple arbitrage alone.

**Key assumption to monitor:** Availability and pricing of sub-scale MSP add-on targets. If PE competition drives add-on multiples above 8x, the arbitrage diminishes.
