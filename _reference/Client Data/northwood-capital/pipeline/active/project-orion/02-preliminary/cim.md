## 1. Executive Summary

CloudFirst Technologies ("CloudFirst" or the "Company") is a leading provider of IT managed services and cybersecurity solutions to mid-market enterprises in the Southeastern United States. Founded in 2008 by James Whitfield in Atlanta, Georgia, the Company has grown from a two-person IT consulting firm into a full-service technology partner serving 118 clients with approximately 225 employees.

CloudFirst operates at the intersection of two of the fastest-growing segments in technology services: managed IT infrastructure and cybersecurity. The Company's dual capability set enables it to serve as a single-source technology partner for mid-market organizations that lack the internal resources to manage increasingly complex IT environments and the escalating threat landscape.

The Company generates $58.0 million in LTM revenue with $9.5 million in LTM Adjusted EBITDA (16.4% margin). Approximately 85% of revenue is recurring, derived from multi-year managed services contracts with an average term of 3 years. Net revenue retention of 112% demonstrates the Company's ability to expand within its existing customer base through service upgrades, user growth, and cybersecurity cross-sell.

The founder and majority shareholder is seeking a capital partner to support the Company's next growth phase — including organic expansion, cybersecurity capability buildout, and strategic acquisitions — while providing partial liquidity. Mr. Whitfield intends to roll approximately 20% of his equity and continue in a leadership role post-transaction.

## 2. Company History

CloudFirst Technologies was founded in 2008 by James Whitfield, a former IT director at a regional financial services firm. Recognizing that mid-market companies in Atlanta's growing business community lacked access to enterprise-grade IT management, Mr. Whitfield launched CloudFirst as an IT consulting and break/fix services provider.

**Key milestones:**

- **2008–2012 — Foundation:** Established core managed services offering. Secured first major client (a regional accounting firm with 250 employees). Built initial team of 12 engineers. Revenue reached $5M by 2012.

- **2013–2016 — Managed Services Pivot:** Transitioned from break/fix to managed services contract model. Introduced 24/7 network monitoring and help desk services. Revenue grew to $18M with improving margins as recurring revenue base scaled.

- **2017–2019 — Regional Expansion:** Opened satellite offices in Charlotte, NC and Nashville, TN. Client base expanded from local Atlanta businesses to regional mid-market enterprises. Revenue reached $32M. Hired Rebecca Santos as CFO to professionalize financial operations.

- **2019–2021 — Cybersecurity Launch:** Recruited Michael Okafor as CTO to lead the buildout of a managed cybersecurity practice. Launched Security Operations Center (SOC) in Atlanta. Cybersecurity became the fastest-growing service line, growing from $0 to approximately $10M in annual revenue by 2021.

- **2022–Present — Platform Maturity:** Revenue surpassed $50M. Completed buildout of cybersecurity offerings including managed detection and response (MDR), endpoint protection, vulnerability management, and compliance-as-a-service. Client base grew to 118 active accounts. Company is now positioned as a comprehensive IT and security partner for mid-market enterprises.

## 3. Services Overview

CloudFirst delivers its services through two integrated business lines:

### 3.1 IT Managed Services (~75% of revenue)

| Service | Description |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Management | 24/7 monitoring and management of servers, networks, endpoints, and cloud environments |
| Cloud Services | Microsoft 365 / Azure / AWS migration, management, and optimization |
| Help Desk & End User Support | Tier 1-3 technical support, onboarding/offboarding, device management |
| Network Operations | WAN/LAN design, SD-WAN deployment, network performance monitoring |
| Business Continuity | Disaster recovery planning, backup management, business continuity testing |
| IT Consulting & vCIO | Virtual CIO services, technology roadmapping, vendor management |

**Revenue model:** Multi-year contracts (average 3-year term) priced on a per-user or per-device basis. Contracts include annual escalators (typically 3-5%). Services are delivered through a combination of remote engineers (70%) and on-site resources (30%).

### 3.2 Managed Cybersecurity (~25% of revenue)

| Service | Description |
|---|---|
| Managed Detection & Response (MDR) | 24/7 threat monitoring, investigation, and incident response from Atlanta SOC |
| Endpoint Protection | Next-gen antivirus, EDR deployment and management across client environments |
| Vulnerability Management | Continuous scanning, penetration testing, remediation tracking |
| Compliance-as-a-Service | SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI-DSS compliance assessment, gap analysis, audit support |
| Security Awareness Training | Phishing simulations, employee security training programs |
| Incident Response | Emergency incident response retainer and breach remediation |

**Revenue model:** Recurring subscription contracts with annual terms and auto-renewal. Priced per-user with tiered packaging (Essential, Professional, Enterprise). Strong cross-sell from existing managed services clients — approximately 40% of managed services clients have adopted at least one cybersecurity service.

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### 6.1 Market Overview

The U.S. IT managed services market exceeds $150 billion in annual spend and is projected to grow at 8-10% annually through 2030. Key growth drivers include:

- **IT complexity:** Multi-cloud environments, remote workforce infrastructure, and expanding application ecosystems drive demand for outsourced management
- **Cybersecurity threat escalation:** Ransomware, phishing, and state-sponsored attacks are growing in frequency and sophistication, particularly targeting mid-market companies with less mature security programs
- **Talent scarcity:** IT and cybersecurity talent shortage makes outsourced management the only viable option for most mid-market enterprises
- **Regulatory compliance:** HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOC 2, and emerging state-level privacy regulations require specialized compliance capabilities that most mid-market companies cannot build internally

### 6.2 Competitive Landscape

The IT managed services market is highly fragmented with over 40,000 MSPs in the United States. The vast majority are small, local providers with fewer than 50 employees and limited service breadth.

CloudFirst competes in the "mid-market MSP" tier — providers large enough to deliver enterprise-grade services but focused on clients in the 100-2,000 employee range. Key competitors in the Southeast include:

| Competitor | Est. Revenue | Geography | Differentiation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Logically | $50M+ | Southeast / National | Broader national footprint, less cybersecurity depth |
| Dataprise | $80M+ | Mid-Atlantic / National | Larger scale, PE-backed, acquisitive growth |
| Ntiva | $60M+ | East Coast | Similar profile, recent PE investment |
| Local MSPs (20+) | $2-15M each | Local / Regional | Smaller, less capable, potential add-on targets |

CloudFirst's competitive advantages include:
- Integrated managed services + cybersecurity from a single provider
- In-house SOC capability (vs. outsourced SOC used by most mid-market MSPs)
- Deep regulatory compliance expertise (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOC 2)
- 17-year client relationships and local market reputation
