### Company Overview

Specialty Chemical Solutions is a regional distributor of specialty and performance chemicals headquartered in Cincinnati, OH. Founded in 1985 by Richard Hoffman, the Company serves approximately 450 industrial and commercial customers with ~340 employees across the Midwest and Mid-Atlantic. SCS differentiates through technical application expertise, custom blending capabilities (20% of revenue at 400-600bps premium margins), and 40-year customer relationships with an average tenure exceeding 15 years.

---

### Market

The U.S. specialty chemical distribution market is estimated at $35-40 billion, growing at 4-6% annually. The market is highly fragmented — the top 10 distributors hold less than 25% market share. Hundreds of regional independents ($10-100M revenue) operate in niche product categories and geographies. Consolidation is active, with PE-backed platforms acquiring regional distributors at 5-7x EBITDA. Key demand drivers include industrial onshoring, water treatment infrastructure, food safety regulation, and increasing chemical management complexity.

### Investment Highlights

1. **Essential products with inelastic demand** — specialty chemicals are consumed in customers' daily manufacturing and facility operations, representing <3% of customer COGS but critical to production. 63% of revenue from low-cyclicality end markets (water treatment, food processing, institutional).
2. **40-year customer relationships with 85% retention** — deeply embedded customer relationships with average tenure exceeding 15 years. Custom blending creates formulation lock-in. No customer lost to a competitor in 5 years.
3. **Fragmented market with 5 identified add-on targets** — highly fragmented $35-40B market with hundreds of founder-owned regional distributors. 5 targets identified ($10-25M revenue) at 5-7x EBITDA, creating meaningful multiple arbitrage and geographic expansion.

---

### Key Risks

1. **Founder/CEO transition — no succession plan** — Richard Hoffman (age 63) is exiting. No internal successor identified. Top customer relationships are concentrated in Hoffman personally. External CEO hire required. — *High*
2. **Working capital intensity ($35M+ inventory)** — NWC exceeds 15% of revenue at ~22%. Driven by safety stock, hazmat storage, and same-day delivery commitments. Compresses returns through larger equity check and lower cash conversion (~60%). — *Medium*
3. **Customer concentration (top 5 = 32%)** — No single customer exceeds 9%, but collective concentration in industrial end markets creates GDP sensitivity for ~35% of revenue. — *Medium*

**Flagged items:** (1) Working capital intensity — NWC at ~22% of revenue exceeds 15% flag. Requires detailed cash conversion cycle analysis. Optimization opportunity of $3-5M identified. (2) Founder/CEO transition — flagged per criteria. External CEO recruitment is required. Hoffman transition advisory agreement needed. (3) Mild cyclicality — metalworking (22%) and agriculture (15%) have moderate GDP sensitivity, but 63% of revenue is from non-cyclical end markets.
