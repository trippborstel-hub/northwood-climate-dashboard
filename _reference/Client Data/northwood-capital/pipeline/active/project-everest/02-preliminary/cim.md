## 1. Executive Summary

Specialty Chemical Solutions Inc. ("SCS" or the "Company") is a leading regional distributor of specialty and performance chemicals headquartered in Cincinnati, Ohio. Founded in 1985 by Richard Hoffman, SCS has grown from a single-product water treatment chemical distributor into a diversified specialty chemical platform serving approximately 450 industrial and commercial customers across the Midwest and Mid-Atlantic regions with approximately 340 employees.

The Company generates $185.0 million in LTM revenue with $18.0 million in LTM Adjusted EBITDA (9.7% margin). SCS differentiates itself through technical application expertise, custom blending capabilities, and deeply embedded customer relationships — the average customer tenure exceeds 15 years. The Company distributes essential process chemicals that are consumed in customers' daily manufacturing and facility operations, creating inelastic demand and predictable repeat purchasing patterns.

Mr. Hoffman, age 63, is pursuing a full exit as part of a planned retirement. The Company's CFO, David Schmidt, and VP of Sales, Karen Walsh, are expected to continue post-transaction.

## 2. Company History

Specialty Chemical Solutions was founded in 1985 by Richard Hoffman, a former technical sales engineer for a major chemical manufacturer. Hoffman recognized an opportunity to build a specialty distributor focused on technical service and custom solutions — an approach that differentiated SCS from commodity chemical distributors competing on price alone.

**Key milestones:**

- **1985–1992 — Foundation:** Launched in a 5,000 sq ft warehouse in Cincinnati, Ohio. Initial focus on water treatment chemicals for industrial cooling systems. Secured first major account (a regional steel mill) through superior technical service. Revenue reached $8M by 1992 with 15 employees.

- **1993–2000 — Product Diversification:** Expanded into metalworking fluids, industrial cleaners, and food-grade processing chemicals. Built custom blending facility in Cincinnati. Revenue grew to $45M. Established relationships with major chemical manufacturers (BASF, Dow, Ashland) as an authorized distributor.

- **2001–2008 — Regional Expansion:** Opened distribution warehouses in Indianapolis, IN, Columbus, OH, and Pittsburgh, PA. Hired David Schmidt as Controller (later promoted to CFO). Revenue reached $95M. Weathered the 2008-2009 recession with only 12% revenue decline — demonstrating demand resilience.

- **2009–2015 — Market Recovery and Growth:** Revenue recovered to pre-recession levels by 2012 and continued growing. Added agricultural chemical distribution and institutional cleaning product lines. Revenue reached $135M. Headcount grew to 280.

- **2016–Present — Platform Maturity:** Hired Karen Walsh as VP of Sales to professionalize the commercial organization. Invested in ERP system (SAP Business One) to improve inventory management and customer service. Added fourth distribution warehouse in Louisville, KY. Revenue surpassed $185M. The Company is now the largest independent specialty chemical distributor in the Ohio Valley region.

### 3.2 Custom Blending & Technical Services

SCS operates a 25,000 sq ft custom blending facility at its Cincinnati headquarters. Custom and private-label blends represent approximately 20% of revenue and carry margins 400-600bps above distributed products. The Company's 12 technical application specialists provide on-site technical support, chemical management programs, and regulatory compliance assistance.

Custom blending is a key differentiator — it creates formulation lock-in with customers (switching requires re-qualification and testing), provides a barrier to commodity price competition, and generates higher margins.

### 5.3 Customer Retention

The Company's customer retention rate is 85% measured on a trailing 3-year basis. Churn is concentrated in smaller accounts (<$100K annual revenue). Among the top 50 customers, retention exceeds 98%. No customer has been lost to a direct competitor in the past 5 years — all churn is attributable to customer business closure, facility relocation, or in-sourcing by very large manufacturers.

Retention drivers include:
- Custom blend formulations that require re-qualification to switch suppliers
- Technical application support embedded in customer operations
- Just-in-time delivery reliability (same-day/next-day) — supply interruption halts production
- Regulatory compliance documentation (SDS, COA) integrated into customer quality systems

## 6. Competitive Landscape

### 6.1 Market Overview

The U.S. specialty chemical distribution market is estimated at $35-40 billion. The market is highly fragmented — the top 10 distributors collectively hold less than 25% market share. Hundreds of regional and local distributors operate in niche product categories and geographies.

The market is growing at 4-6% annually, driven by:
- Industrial production recovery and onshoring trends
- Increasing regulatory complexity (customers outsourcing chemical management)
- Water treatment demand growth (aging infrastructure, stricter EPA standards)
- Food safety requirements driving demand for specialty sanitizers and processing aids

### 6.2 Competitive Positioning

| Competitor | Est. Revenue | Geography | Differentiation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Univar Solutions | $9.5B | National/Global | Broadline commodity + specialty. Largest U.S. chemical distributor. |
| Brenntag | $14.5B | National/Global | #2 globally. Broad product portfolio. |
| Azelis | $4.0B | National/Global | Specialty-focused. PE-backed growth platform. |
| Hawkins Inc. | $900M | Upper Midwest | Water treatment and industrial chemicals. Public company. |
| Regional independents (50+) | $10-100M | Local/Regional | Product niche or geographic focus. Potential acquisition targets. |

SCS competes primarily against regional independent distributors, not against national broadline players. The Company's competitive advantages include:

- **Technical expertise:** 12 application specialists provide on-site support that commodity distributors do not offer
- **Custom blending:** 20% of revenue from formulations that create switching costs
- **Delivery speed:** Same-day/next-day delivery within 400-mile radius — national distributors typically offer 3-5 day lead times
- **Relationship depth:** 40 years of local market knowledge and customer intimacy

### 6.3 Consolidation Activity

Specialty chemical distribution is actively consolidating:

| Transaction | Year | Multiple | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| Azelis (EQT Partners) | 2021 | ~12x | Large specialty platform, global scale |
| Nexeo / Univar (merger) | 2019 | ~9x | Broadline consolidation |
| Regional specialty distributors (avg) | 2023-2025 | 6-8x | PE-backed roll-ups |
| Small independent distributors | 2024-2025 | 4-6x | Founder exits, tuck-in acquisitions |

Platform-level specialty distributors ($100M+ revenue) transact at 8-10x. Sub-scale regional distributors ($10-50M revenue) are acquired at 5-7x, creating meaningful arbitrage for consolidation strategies.
