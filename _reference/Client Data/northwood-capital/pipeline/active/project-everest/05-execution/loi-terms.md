## Key Terms

### Exclusivity

- **Exclusivity period:** 60 days from execution of the LOI
- **Exclusivity commencement:** Upon countersignature by seller
- **Exclusivity expiration:** 60 calendar days from execution, extendable by mutual agreement
- **Exclusivity decision expected:** February 25, 2026

### Due Diligence

Full confirmatory due diligence to include:

| Workstream | Scope |
|---|---|
| Quality of Earnings | Financial DD — historical and projected EBITDA, revenue quality, working capital normalization, adjustments validation |
| Legal | Corporate structure, contracts, litigation, IP, regulatory compliance |
| Environmental | Phase I environmental site assessments for all 5 facilities (Cincinnati HQ, Indianapolis, Columbus, Pittsburgh, Louisville); chemical storage/handling compliance review |
| Insurance | Adequacy of property, liability, environmental, fleet, and workers compensation programs |
| IT / Systems | SAP Business One ERP assessment, Salesforce CRM, data security, systems integration readiness |
| HR / Benefits | Employee census, benefits programs, retention risk, key person dependencies, labor compliance |
| Tax | Federal and state tax compliance, structural optimization |
| Commercial | Customer relationship stability, competitive dynamics, pricing sustainability |

### Working Capital

- **Working capital target:** Normalized net working capital to be agreed between buyer and seller prior to signing
- **Estimated NWC range:** $32.0M–$35.0M (approximately 17–19% of LTM revenue)
- **Mechanism:** Dollar-for-dollar adjustment at close based on closing balance sheet versus agreed NWC target
- **True-up:** Post-close true-up within 90 days of closing based on final closing balance sheet

### Key Employee Provisions

| Provision | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Founder consulting agreement** | 12–18 month post-close transition advisory role for Richard Hoffman. Part-time (2–3 days per month). Focused on customer relationship introductions and institutional knowledge transfer. Compensated separately from transaction proceeds. |
| **CFO retention** | David Schmidt — retention package including equity participation in management pool (20%), competitive base and bonus, and PE mentorship/development support |
| **VP Sales retention** | Karen Walsh — retention package including equity participation, expanded role trajectory (Chief Commercial Officer), and sales team operational autonomy |
| **Non-compete (founder)** | 3-year non-compete and non-solicitation agreement covering the Company's geographic territory, customer base, and employees |

### Environmental

- Environmental site assessments (Phase I, and Phase II if triggered) required for all facilities handling or storing chemicals
- Review of EPA, DOT, and OSHA compliance history across all 5 locations
- Assessment of hazardous material storage, secondary containment, waste disposal, and spill response programs
- Confirmation of no pending regulatory actions, outstanding violations, or known environmental liabilities
- Environmental compliance cost review (currently $0.8M annually)

### Material Adverse Change

- Standard MAC clause — buyer may terminate if a material adverse change occurs between signing and closing
- Carve-outs for general economic conditions, industry-wide developments, and changes in applicable law (standard)

### Closing Conditions

- Satisfactory completion of confirmatory due diligence (all workstreams)
- Receipt of committed debt financing on terms acceptable to buyer
- All required regulatory approvals and clearances (Hart-Scott-Rodino if applicable)
- No material adverse change between signing and closing
- Execution of key employee agreements (Hoffman consulting, Schmidt and Walsh retention)
- Delivery of audited financial statements and closing balance sheet
- Third-party consents as required (material contracts, leases, supplier agreements)

## Competitive Dynamics

- **Process type:** Structured auction — 6–8 initial bidders, believed narrowed to 3–4 in final round
- **Competitive position:** 2–3 other bidders believed to remain active
- **Strategic bidders:** At least one strategic chemical distributor may bid at a premium to financial sponsors
- **Northwood differentiation:** Alignment with founder's stated preferences — management equity rollover, employee retention commitment, brand continuity, no platform integration (standalone investment)
- **Price positioning:** LOI at $155.0M (8.6x) represents the midpoint of the $145–$165M indicated range; believed competitive with other financial sponsor bids
- **Transition plan advantage:** Northwood's articulated CEO succession plan, Operating Partner assignment (David Okafor), and structured founder transition distinguishes the bid from sponsors without a specific execution plan
