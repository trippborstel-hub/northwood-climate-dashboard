## Preliminary Assessment

**Sector fit:** Good. Specialty chemical distribution falls within Northwood's Industrial Technology sector (sub-sector: specialty distribution). The Company distributes essential, non-discretionary products to industrial end markets with long customer relationships and technical switching costs. This aligns with Northwood's thesis of critical infrastructure services with barriers to entry.

**Size and valuation:** The Company is appropriately sized for a Fund II platform investment. At $18.0M Adjusted EBITDA, the equity check of $58-66M is within Fund II's range. The indicated valuation range of 8.1x-9.2x is consistent with Northwood's Industrial Technology guardrails (6-9x), though the high end approaches the ceiling.

**Return potential:** Preliminary model indicates ~25% IRR / 2.5x MOIC over a 5-year hold. Value creation is driven by (a) essential product demand with pricing power, (b) add-on consolidation in a fragmented market (5 identified targets), and (c) operational improvement through procurement optimization and working capital management.

**Key concern — founder transition:** Richard Hoffman has run this business for 40 years. He is the business in the eyes of customers, suppliers, and employees. There is no CEO successor, no management committee, and limited formal governance. This is a manageable risk but it is the most important execution challenge in the deal. An external CEO hire is required pre- or immediately post-close.

**Key concern — working capital:** $35M+ in inventory is meaningful. Working capital as a percentage of revenue is likely 18-20%, above the 15% flag threshold. This needs detailed analysis in QofE — seasonal patterns, slow-moving inventory, regulatory requirements for hazardous materials, and opportunities for optimization.

## Screening Decision

**ADVANCE** — Proceed to preliminary due diligence. Request management presentation and data room access.

**Rationale:** Specialty Chemical Solutions is a well-run, 40-year-old specialty distribution business with essential products, deep customer relationships, and a fragmented market ripe for consolidation. The business fits Northwood's Industrial Technology thesis — critical products, technical barriers to entry, long customer lifecycles, and add-on driven value creation. The two key risks — founder transition and working capital intensity — are addressable through careful execution. James Harrison's industrial sector expertise and Northwood's operating partner capability (David Okafor) provide the right team for this deal.
