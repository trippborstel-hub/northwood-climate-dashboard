### Company Overview

Smile Partners Management ("SPM") is a Nashville, TN-based dental support organization (DSO) operating 15 dental practice locations across Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia. Founded in 2012, the Company employs ~380 people and supports 42 dentists, generating approximately 190,000 patient visits annually. SPM provides non-clinical management services (billing, marketing, procurement, HR, facilities) to affiliated practices, enabling dentists to focus on clinical care.

---

### Market

The U.S. dental services market is approximately $160B. DSOs currently manage ~10–12% of U.S. dental practices, a share that has grown from ~5% in 2015 and is projected to reach 20–25% by 2030. The DSO model benefits from dentist preference for employment over ownership (particularly among younger graduates), patient demand for integrated services, and payor preference for dealing with organized delivery systems. The Southeastern U.S. market is underpenetrated relative to coastal markets, creating expansion opportunity.

### Investment Highlights

1. **Proven DSO platform with scalable model** — Grown from 7 to 15 locations in 5 years with demonstrated de novo playbook. Revenue per mature location of ~$5.5M–$6.5M. Unit economics well-understood and repeatable.

2. **Favorable macro and demographic tailwinds** — DSO penetration growing from ~10% to projected 20–25% by 2030. Younger dentists increasingly prefer employment model. Southeastern U.S. underpenetrated relative to national average.

3. **Experienced management team with rollover commitment** — CEO, CFO, and COO intend to roll equity. Dr. Reeves has prior DSO scaling experience. Andrew Chen (CFO) brings institutional financial discipline.

---

### Key Risks

1. **Dentist recruitment and retention** — *High.* The DSO model is critically dependent on attracting and retaining dentists. Compensation competition from competing DSOs and private practice. Diligence must assess compensation model competitiveness, turnover rates, and recruitment pipeline.

2. **De novo ramp-up execution** — *Medium.* New clinics historically take 18 months to reach breakeven. Capital intensity during ramp period compresses near-term returns. Growth plan depends on successful execution of 3+ de novo locations. Must stress-test ramp timing assumptions.

3. **Insurance reimbursement mix** — *Medium.* Current mix is 65% commercial, 25% Medicaid, 10% self-pay. Medicaid rates are state-dependent and subject to legislative change. Must assess payor contract renewal trends, rate trajectory, and commercial-to-Medicaid ratio by location.

**Flagged items:** Small for platform (addressed above — path to $20M+ credible but execution-dependent). Valuation sensitivity (will target 9–10x range).
