## Preliminary Assessment

### What We Like

- DSO model proven at 15 locations with demonstrated unit economics
- Patient volume growth of 12% YoY reflects strong demand and execution
- Management team has DSO-specific experience (Dr. Reeves previously scaled a 6-location group)
- Three adjacent MSAs identified for de novo expansion (Memphis, Knoxville, Chattanooga)
- Healthcare services sector aligns with Northwood thesis; complements existing portfolio (NovaCare, Keystone)

### Key Concerns

- EBITDA of $11.5M is borderline for platform — "small for platform" flag triggered
- Healthcare services valuation range of 8–11x means the high end of seller expectation ($125.0M / 10.9x) is aggressive
- De novo clinic ramp-up historically takes 18 months to breakeven — capital-intensive growth model
- Insurance reimbursement mix (65% commercial, 25% Medicaid, 10% self-pay) creates exposure to payor negotiation risk
- Dentist recruitment and retention is the single biggest operational risk in the DSO model

### Flagged Items

- **Small for platform:** $11.5M adjusted EBITDA. Path to $20M+ requires successful de novo expansion and/or add-on acquisitions within 24 months. Projected FY 2026 EBITDA of $12.8M. Credible but execution-dependent.
- **Valuation range:** 8.7x–10.9x implied multiple. Low end is within healthcare services range (8–11x); high end is at the ceiling. Will need to target 9–10x in IOI.
