Margins on Medicaid patients are lower than commercial but still positive.

### Maria Gonzalez — COO

**Overall impression: Strong. 4/5.**

- Operational leader with impressive scale experience — came from Aspen Dental where she oversaw 35 locations as Regional VP Operations. She brings a playbook that has been tested at 5x SPM's current scale.

Post-COVID hygienist supply is still below pre-pandemic levels, and compensation has increased 15–20%. SPM is managing this through signing bonuses, flexible scheduling, and a hygiene apprenticeship program partnering with local community colleges.
- **Positive signal:** Maria is the operational backbone of the growth thesis. Her Aspen Dental experience is directly applicable at 2–3x SPM's current scale.

## Key Discussion Topics

### Clinic-Level Economics

Andrew provided a summary of clinic-level performance by vintage (detailed clinic P&Ls requested for data room):

- **Mature clinics (pre-2022):** 9 locations, average revenue $5.8M, average EBITDA margin 24%
- **2023–2024 openings:** 4 locations, average revenue $4.2M, average EBITDA margin 14% (improving)
- **2025 openings:** 2 locations, average revenue $3.4M, average EBITDA margin 5% (early ramp)

The vintage analysis is critical for modeling — it shows that the de novo model works but requires 18–24 months to reach mature economics.

### Dentist Compensation and Retention

Katherine described the compensation model:
- Base salary + production-based bonus (typical: $180K base + 25% of production above threshold)
- Benefits package (health, dental, retirement, CE funding)
- Partnership track for top-performing dentists (equity-like participation in their practice economics)
- 15% annual turnover vs. ~20% industry average

Retention is strong relative to industry but still a key risk. The partnership track concept is interesting — need to understand the mechanics and financial implications in more detail.

### Add-On Acquisition Opportunity

Katherine and Maria discussed the add-on opportunity:
- Primary target: solo practitioner retirements (2,000+ dentists age 55+ in TN/AL/GA)
- Typical deal: 1–2 location practice, $1.5M–$3.0M revenue, acquired at 4–6x practice-level EBITDA
- Integration: Convert to SPM systems and brand within 90 days, retain existing patient base, add SPM services
- Pipeline: 3 practices in active conversation, 8+ in relationship-building stage
- No add-on acquisitions completed to date — the current sponsor's strategy was organic-first

The add-on opportunity is real but unproven within SPM. This is both risk and opportunity — if we acquire the platform and successfully execute 2–3 add-ons at 4–6x, the multiple arbitrage is significant.
