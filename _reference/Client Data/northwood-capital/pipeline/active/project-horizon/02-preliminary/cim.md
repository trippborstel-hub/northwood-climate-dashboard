## 1. Executive Summary

Smile Partners Management, Inc. ("SPM" or the "Company") is a dental support organization headquartered in Nashville, TN, providing non-clinical management services to 15 affiliated dental practice locations across Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia. The Company supports 42 dentists and generates approximately 190,000 patient visits annually.

SPM was founded in 2012 by Dr. Katherine Reeves, a practicing dentist who recognized the opportunity to build a professionally managed dental platform that would allow dentists to focus on patient care while benefiting from the operational scale and infrastructure of an institutional management organization.

In 2021, the Company's current private equity sponsor acquired a majority interest, providing capital and operational support to accelerate the Company's growth from 7 to 15 locations. Under the current partnership, SPM has more than doubled revenue, professionalized its management infrastructure, and developed a repeatable de novo expansion playbook.

The Company generated LTM revenue of $72.0M and adjusted EBITDA of $11.5M (16.0% margin) as of January 31, 2026. Revenue has grown at a compound annual rate of approximately 18% over the past three years, driven by a combination of de novo location openings, same-store patient volume growth, and operational improvements.

The current sponsor is seeking to complete a successful exit, and SPM's management team — led by Dr. Reeves (CEO), Andrew Chen (CFO), and Maria Gonzalez (COO) — intends to roll a meaningful portion of their equity into the next partnership.

## 3. The DSO Model

### Structure

SPM operates under a management services organization (MSO) / DSO model that is the standard structure for multi-site dental platform companies. Under this model:

- **SPM (the MSO)** provides non-clinical management services: practice management, billing and collections, marketing, procurement, HR, IT, compliance, and facility management
- **Affiliated dental practices** employ the dentists and deliver clinical care
- A **management services agreement (MSA)** governs the relationship, with SPM receiving a management fee based on a percentage of practice revenue

This structure preserves the "corporate practice of dentistry" compliance framework required in most states, while enabling the operational and financial benefits of a scaled platform.

### Value Proposition to Dentists

SPM's model addresses the key pain points that drive dentist preference for DSO-affiliated employment:

- **Administrative burden reduction** — Dentists focus on patient care; SPM handles billing, scheduling, marketing, HR, and compliance
- **Practice economics improvement** — Centralized procurement (25–30% savings on supplies), negotiated payor contracts, and marketing leverage
- **Career development** — Structured mentorship, specialty training pathways, and partnership tracks
- **Work-life balance** — Predictable schedules, no ownership overhead, benefits and retirement plans
- **Capital access** — SPM funds facility buildouts, equipment, and technology investments

## 5. Market Opportunity

### U.S. Dental Services Market

The U.S. dental services market is approximately **$160 billion**, encompassing general dentistry, specialty care, orthodontics, and cosmetic services. Key market characteristics:

- **Steady growth:** 4–5% annual growth driven by population growth, aging demographics, and increasing access through insurance coverage expansion
- **Non-discretionary demand:** Regular dental care is clinically necessary and largely covered by insurance
- **Favorable demographics:** Growing pediatric population and aging baby boomers both drive demand — children for preventive care, aging adults for restorative and implant services

### DSO Penetration

DSO-managed practices represent approximately **10–12% of U.S. dental practices** currently, a share that has grown from ~5% in 2015. Industry projections indicate DSO penetration will reach **20–25% by 2030**, driven by:

- **Generational shift:** Over 75% of new dental school graduates prefer employment over practice ownership. Student debt loads (~$300K average for dental school) make practice acquisition challenging.
- **Operational complexity:** Regulatory compliance, insurance credentialing, HR management, and technology requirements increasingly favor institutional management.
- **Patient expectations:** Patients increasingly expect coordinated care, modern facilities, online scheduling, and seamless insurance processing — capabilities that scaled DSOs deliver more effectively.

### Southeastern U.S. Opportunity

The Southeast is **underpenetrated by DSOs** relative to coastal and urban markets. Tennessee, Alabama, and Georgia have DSO penetration rates of 6–8%, below the national average. This underpenetration, combined with strong population growth and favorable practice economics in these markets, creates a compelling expansion opportunity.

SPM has identified **three adjacent MSAs** for near-term de novo expansion:
- **Memphis, TN** — 1.3M metro population, limited DSO presence
- **Knoxville, TN** — 880K metro population, one competing DSO
- **Chattanooga, TN** — 560K metro population, no institutional DSO presence

## 7. Growth Strategy

### De Novo Expansion

SPM has developed a repeatable de novo expansion playbook refined over 8 location openings:

**Site Selection:** Data-driven analysis of demographics, insurance coverage, competition density, and real estate availability. Target: 50,000+ population within 5-mile radius, median household income >$55K, limited DSO competition.

**Buildout:** Standardized 4,000–5,000 sq ft clinic design, 8–10 operatories. Lease negotiation, permit, and buildout timeline of 6–9 months.

**Staffing:** Lead dentist recruited 3–4 months before opening. Hygienists and support staff recruited 1–2 months before opening. Centralized recruiting team manages pipeline.

**Patient Ramp:** Targeted marketing campaign (digital, local, referral) beginning 60 days before opening. Ramp to breakeven in 12–18 months, mature economics in 24–30 months.

**Near-term pipeline:** 3 locations planned for 2026 (Memphis, Knoxville, Chattanooga).

### Add-On Acquisitions

The dental market is highly fragmented, with ~120,000 solo and small-group practices in the U.S. SPM has identified acquisition opportunities in its target markets:

- Solo practitioner retirements (target: 1–2 location practices at 4–6x EBITDA)
- Small multi-location groups seeking institutional partnership
- Conversion of acquired practices to SPM operating model and brand

### Same-Store Growth

- Marketing investment to increase new patient acquisition
- Expanded specialty services (orthodontics, implants) at additional locations
- Extended hours and weekend availability at high-demand locations
- Insurance network expansion to capture additional covered lives

### Operational Improvement

- Procurement leverage from increased scale (target: 5% additional savings on supplies)
- Centralized scheduling optimization to improve dentist utilization
- Revenue cycle management enhancement (reduce DSO from 42 days toward 35 days)
- Hygiene department optimization to increase hygiene revenue per visit
