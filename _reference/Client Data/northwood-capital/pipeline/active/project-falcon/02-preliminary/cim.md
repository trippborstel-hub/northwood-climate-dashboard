## 1. Executive Summary

National Environmental Testing Labs, Inc. ("NETL" or the "Company") is a leading provider of environmental analytical testing, remediation support services, and emerging contaminant testing to industrial, energy, and municipal clients across the Gulf Coast and Southern United States.

Founded in 1998 by Tom Bradley, NETL has grown from a single laboratory in Houston to a network of six accredited facilities across Texas, Louisiana, and Oklahoma. The Company employs approximately 450 professionals, including over 120 credentialed chemists and laboratory scientists, and maintains accreditations under NELAP, ISO 17025, and state-specific environmental laboratory programs in eight states.

NETL generated LTM revenue of $95.0M and adjusted EBITDA of $14.2M (14.9% margin) as of January 31, 2026. Revenue has grown at a compound annual rate of approximately 12% over the past three years, driven by organic volume growth, expansion into emerging contaminant testing, and the Company's strong reputation for analytical accuracy and client responsiveness.

The Company's shareholders are exploring strategic alternatives to identify a growth-oriented partner capable of accelerating NETL's expansion, both organically and through strategic acquisitions. The Company's founder, Tom Bradley (age 62), is pursuing a succession plan that provides for an orderly leadership transition while preserving the Company's culture and market position.

## 2. Company History & Overview

### Founding and Early Growth (1998–2008)

NETL was founded in 1998 by Tom Bradley, a chemist and environmental engineer who previously served as laboratory director at a national environmental consulting firm. Bradley identified an opportunity to build a Gulf Coast-focused environmental testing laboratory that combined the analytical rigor of the national players with the responsiveness and client intimacy of a regional firm.

The Company opened its first laboratory in Houston, TX, initially focusing on soil and groundwater testing for petroleum storage tank compliance — a critical need in the energy-rich Gulf Coast region. Early growth was driven by the region's dense concentration of oil and gas operators, refineries, and petrochemical facilities requiring ongoing environmental compliance testing.

By 2005, NETL had expanded to three laboratory facilities (Houston, Baton Rouge, and Beaumont) and had established itself as the preferred testing partner for several major energy and industrial clients in the region.

### Regional Expansion (2008–2018)

The Company's second decade saw expansion into adjacent service lines and geographic markets:

- **2008:** Opened Oklahoma City laboratory to serve the Midcontinent energy market
- **2011:** Added remediation support services — sampling, monitoring, and analytical support for remediation projects, extending the Company's presence in the value chain
- **2014:** Opened Lake Charles, LA laboratory to serve the growing petrochemical and LNG construction corridor
- **2016:** Achieved ISO 17025 accreditation across all facilities, enabling the Company to compete for government and military environmental testing contracts
- **2017:** Opened Dallas laboratory to serve the North Texas municipal and commercial real estate markets

Throughout this period, the Company maintained a focus on analytical quality, regulatory expertise, and client service responsiveness — differentiators that have driven industry-leading client retention rates.

### Current Platform (2018–Present)

Over the past eight years, NETL has evolved from a traditional environmental testing laboratory to a comprehensive analytical services platform:

- **PFAS Testing Capabilities (2020):** NETL was among the first regional laboratories to invest in the analytical equipment and methodology development required for PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) testing. This early investment has positioned the Company as a leader in an emerging contaminant testing segment that has grown from zero to approximately 15% of revenue.

- **Technology Modernization (2021–2023):** Implementation of a cloud-based Laboratory Information Management System (LIMS) across all six facilities, enabling centralized quality control, unified reporting, and real-time sample tracking. The LIMS investment has improved turnaround times by approximately 20% and reduced analytical errors.

- **Quality and Compliance (2024):** Achieved NELAP accreditation in three additional states (Alabama, Mississippi, and Arkansas), expanding the Company's addressable market and positioning for geographic growth.

## 3. Products & Services

NETL provides three core service lines, each serving distinct but complementary market needs:

### Environmental Analytical Testing (65% of revenue)

The Company's core business. NETL performs laboratory analysis of soil, water, air, and waste samples for regulatory compliance purposes. Testing is performed under strict chain-of-custody protocols and in accordance with EPA-approved analytical methods.

**Key testing categories:**

- **Water quality testing** — Municipal drinking water compliance, industrial wastewater discharge monitoring, stormwater runoff analysis. Testing parameters include metals, organics, nutrients, bacteria, and radionuclides.
- **Soil and sediment analysis** — Contaminated site assessment, brownfield redevelopment support, agricultural soil testing. Parameters include petroleum hydrocarbons, heavy metals, pesticides, and PCBs.
- **Air quality testing** — Stack emissions testing, ambient air monitoring, indoor air quality assessment. Compliance with Clean Air Act and state air quality standards.
- **Waste characterization** — RCRA hazardous waste determination, waste classification for disposal, treatment feasibility studies.

The Company processes approximately 400,000 samples annually across its six laboratories, with an average turnaround time of 5–7 business days for standard analyses and 24–48 hours for rush/emergency work.

### Remediation Support Services (20% of revenue)

NETL provides field sampling, environmental monitoring, and analytical support for environmental remediation projects. This service line extends the Company's relationship with clients from the analytical testing laboratory into the field, capturing additional revenue per project and deepening client relationships.

**Key services:**

- Groundwater monitoring well installation and sampling
- Remediation system performance monitoring
- Post-remediation confirmation sampling
- Long-term monitoring programs for closed sites
- Regulatory agency coordination and reporting support

Remediation support contracts are typically multi-year engagements, with active monitoring programs at over 150 sites. This service line provides significant recurring revenue visibility.

### Emerging Contaminant Testing (15% of revenue)

The Company's fastest-growing segment, driven by the regulatory and public health response to PFAS and other emerging contaminants.

**Key services:**

- PFAS analysis using EPA Methods 533, 537.1, and draft Method 1633
- Microplastics identification and quantification
- 1,4-Dioxane testing
- Pharmaceutical and personal care product (PPCP) screening

NETL invested approximately $4.5M between 2020 and 2024 in PFAS analytical equipment (LC-MS/MS systems), method development, and staff training. The Company is now one of the leading regional PFAS testing providers, with the capacity to process over 50,000 PFAS samples annually.

## 4. Market Opportunity

### Market Size and Growth

The U.S. environmental testing services market is approximately **$12 billion** and is projected to grow at **6–8% annually** through 2030, driven by regulatory expansion, infrastructure spending, and increasing awareness of emerging contaminants.

**Key growth drivers:**

- **EPA PFAS drinking water standards:** The EPA finalized national drinking water standards for six PFAS compounds in 2024, requiring all U.S. public water systems to test and comply within five years. This single regulation is expected to generate billions of dollars in testing demand.

- **State-level PFAS regulation:** Over 30 states have enacted or proposed PFAS-related legislation covering drinking water, groundwater, soil, wastewater, and biosolids. State standards frequently exceed federal requirements, creating additional testing demand.

- **Infrastructure spending:** The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (2021) allocated approximately $55 billion for clean water infrastructure, including $10 billion specifically for PFAS remediation. Environmental site assessments required for infrastructure projects generate testing demand.

- **Industrial compliance expansion:** Tightening industrial discharge permits, increasing enforcement of RCRA and Clean Water Act requirements, and expanding corporate ESG reporting requirements all drive additional testing demand.

### Market Structure

The environmental testing market is **highly fragmented**, with hundreds of regional and local laboratories competing alongside a handful of national and international platforms:

- **National / international platforms** (Eurofins, SGS, Bureau Veritas): ~20% market share collectively
- **Large regional players** (Pace Analytical, TestAmerica, NETL and peers): ~15% market share
- **Mid-size regional laboratories**: ~25% market share
- **Small and local laboratories**: ~40% market share

This fragmentation creates a compelling **platform consolidation opportunity**. Several private equity-backed environmental testing platforms have successfully executed consolidation strategies in recent years, demonstrating the viability of a buy-and-build approach.

## 7. Growth Strategy

NETL's growth strategy is built on four pillars:

### Organic Revenue Growth

- **PFAS testing expansion:** Continue to build PFAS testing capacity as EPA and state regulations drive sustained demand growth. Management projects PFAS testing revenue to grow from ~$14M (current) to $25M+ within three years.
- **Geographic expansion:** Leverage existing NELAP accreditations in Alabama, Mississippi, and Arkansas to grow customer base in adjacent states. Establish satellite sampling locations to extend the Company's collection footprint.
- **Service line expansion:** Add specialized testing capabilities in emerging areas (microplastics, 1,4-dioxane, PPCP) to capture additional share of existing client spending.

### Acquisitive Growth

- **Regional laboratory acquisitions:** Management has identified 10+ regional environmental testing laboratories as potential acquisition targets. Target profile: $3M–$10M revenue, accredited laboratory operations, customer base in states adjacent to NETL's current footprint.
- **Capability acquisitions:** Specialty testing capabilities (radiochemistry, asbestos, industrial hygiene) that would broaden the Company's service offering and increase revenue per customer.

### Operational Improvement

- **Laboratory network optimization:** Centralize high-volume testing at the Houston and Baton Rouge facilities while positioning smaller laboratories for client-facing sample collection and specialized testing.
- **Automation investment:** Automated sample preparation and analysis systems to increase throughput, reduce turnaround times, and improve labor productivity.
- **Procurement leverage:** Consolidated purchasing for laboratory consumables, reagents, and equipment across all facilities.

### Workforce Development

- **Chemist and scientist pipeline:** Partnerships with regional universities (University of Houston, Louisiana State University, Oklahoma State University) for recruitment. Structured career development pathway from laboratory technician to senior scientist.
- **Retention programs:** Competitive compensation, professional development funding, and laboratory accreditation certification support. Current annual voluntary turnover of ~12%, below the industry average of ~18%.
