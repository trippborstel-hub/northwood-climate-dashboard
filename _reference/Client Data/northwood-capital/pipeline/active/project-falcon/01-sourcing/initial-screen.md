### Company Overview

National Environmental Testing Labs ("NETL") is a Houston, TX-based provider of environmental analytical testing, remediation support, and emerging contaminant testing services. Founded in 1998 with ~450 employees, the Company operates six accredited laboratories across Texas, Louisiana, and Oklahoma. NETL serves industrial, energy, and municipal clients with compliance-driven testing that is non-discretionary and regulated.

---

### Market

The U.S. environmental testing market is approximately $12B and growing at 6–8% annually, driven by EPA PFAS standards, state-level environmental regulations, and infrastructure-related environmental assessments. The market is highly fragmented — the top 10 players account for less than 25% of the market — creating a compelling consolidation opportunity for a well-capitalized platform. Key competitors include Eurofins, Pace Analytical, and TestAmerica, alongside hundreds of regional laboratories.

### Investment Highlights

1. **Regulatory-driven recurring revenue** — 80% of revenue from recurring testing contracts. Environmental compliance is non-discretionary. EPA PFAS standards and state regulations provide multi-year demand visibility.

2. **Fragmented market / consolidation opportunity** — $12B market with top 10 players at <25% share. 10+ regional laboratories identified as potential add-on targets at 4–6x EBITDA. Consolidation thesis is well-proven in environmental testing (Eurofins, Montrose Environmental have executed this strategy at larger scale).

3. **PFAS testing tailwind** — Emerging contaminant testing is the Company's fastest-growing segment (~15% of revenue, growing >40% annually). NETL has invested in PFAS analytical capabilities that many smaller regional labs lack, creating a competitive advantage in a multi-year growth cycle.

---

### Key Risks

1. **Geographic concentration (TX/LA/OK = 75% of revenue)** — *Medium.* Gulf Coast concentration creates regional economic and weather exposure. Mitigated by regulatory demand resilience and add-on geographic expansion strategy. Diligence should stress-test a Gulf Coast downturn scenario.

2. **Regulatory certification timeline** — *Medium.* Laboratory accreditations (NELAP, ISO 17025, state certifications) take 12–18 months to obtain in new states. Limits speed of organic geographic expansion but can be accelerated through acquisitions of already-accredited labs.

3. **Lab consolidation execution risk** — *Medium.* Post-acquisition lab integration requires maintaining accreditation, retaining technical staff, and migrating LIMS systems without service disruption. Execution failure could damage client relationships and accreditation status.

**Flagged items:** Small for platform (addressed above — path to $20M+ EBITDA credible). Geographic concentration (addressed above — mitigated by regulatory demand resilience and add-on expansion strategy).
