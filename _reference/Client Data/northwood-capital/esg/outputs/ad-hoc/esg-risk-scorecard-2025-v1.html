Scoring Methodology

This scorecard applies the McKinsey ESG risk framework adapted for PE portfolio monitoring. Each portfolio company is assessed across 8 dimensions spanning Environmental, Social, and Governance pillars. Scores are derived from climate questionnaire submissions, facility-level carbon data (139 facilities), financial performance data, and sector-specific risk factors.

Scoring Scale

ScoreRatingDefinition
1MinimalNo material ESG risk identified. Strong controls in place. No action required.
2LowMinor ESG considerations. Adequate controls. Monitor on standard cycle.
3ModerateMaterial ESG exposure exists. Management awareness required. Action plan recommended.
4ElevatedSignificant ESG risk. Active management required. Board-level visibility recommended.
5HighCritical ESG risk requiring immediate attention. Potential value impairment. IC escalation required.

Composite Calculation

Composite Score = Σ(Dimension Score × Dimension Weight). Risk level thresholds: ≤1.5 Minimal, ≤2.5 Low, ≤3.0 Moderate, ≤3.75 Elevated, >3.75 High.

Limitations

This is the first execution of the framework. No historical baselines exist for trend analysis. Scope 1/2 emissions have not yet been calculated from raw consumption data. Physical risk scores rely on self-reported questionnaire data, not independent geospatial assessment. Social dimension scores (S1-S3) are informed by questionnaire responses and public sector data rather than independent audit.
