## Thesis Overview

NovaCare Health Partners is an 18-location outpatient rehabilitation and physical therapy platform operating across the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast United States. The company provides physical therapy, occupational therapy, sports medicine rehabilitation, and post-surgical recovery services through approximately 210 employees, including 85 licensed physical therapists and physical therapy assistants.

**Investment Thesis:** Acquire a proven multi-site PT platform and build a regional market leader through a combination of de novo clinic openings and accretive add-on acquisitions, while driving margin improvement through centralized billing, procurement, and operational standardization.

---

## Why This Asset

### 1. Favorable Industry Dynamics
- US outpatient rehabilitation market estimated at $42B, growing 5-6% annually
- Aging population (65+ growing 3% annually) drives sustained demand for PT services
- Shift from inpatient to outpatient rehab settings (lower cost, comparable outcomes) supported by CMS and commercial payers
- Direct access laws expanding in most states -- patients can see a PT without physician referral, increasing addressable market
- Highly fragmented: top 10 providers hold <15% market share; thousands of independent practices ripe for consolidation

### 2. Proven Clinic Operating Model
- 18 clinics with consistent performance: average revenue per clinic of $5.8M, average EBITDA margin of 17.0%
- Standardized clinic layout, patient intake, and treatment protocols developed over 10 years of operation
- Strong physician referral network: relationships with 200+ referring physicians across 4 states
- Patient satisfaction scores above 90th percentile (Net Promoter Score of 72)
- Average patient visits per clinic of 12,500 annually with 85% therapist utilization

### 3. Attractive Entry Valuation
- $160.0M EV at 9.0x LTM EBITDA ($17.8M)
- Comparable platform transactions: 10-12x for multi-site PT businesses with similar scale
- Discount reflects: (a) regional concentration, (b) PE-to-PE secondary with limited control premium, (c) smaller scale vs. national platforms
- Entry at 9.0x with exit at 10.0x provides 100+ bps of multiple expansion contribution to returns

### 4. Management Team
- Dr. Sandra Reeves (CEO): Doctor of Physical Therapy, 15 years in outpatient rehab operations, former Regional VP at ATI Physical Therapy. Combines clinical expertise with operational leadership.
- Michael Park (CFO): Former controller at a multi-site specialty healthcare platform; strong PE reporting discipline
- Amanda Torres (COO): Built the clinic operations playbook under prior ownership; manages all 18 location directors
- Full team rolling $19.2M (20%), demonstrating conviction in the growth plan

## Value Creation Plan

### Lever 1 -- Platform Expansion (De Novo + Add-On)
- **De novo clinics:** Open 4-6 new clinics over Years 2-5 in adjacent markets. Target ramp: breakeven within 9-12 months, $5-6M revenue at maturity. Estimated cost: $300-400K per clinic (leasehold improvements, equipment, initial operating losses).
- **Add-on acquisitions:** Acquire 2-3 smaller PT practices (3-8 locations each) at 6-8x EBITDA. Fragmented market with 5,000+ independent practices nationally. Multiple arbitrage: acquire at 6-8x, value at platform multiple of 9-10x.
- **Target:** 28-32 clinics by Year 5, generating $160-180M in revenue and $28-32M in EBITDA.
- **Expected contribution:** $8-12M incremental EBITDA from expansion.

### Lever 2 -- Centralized Billing and Revenue Cycle Management
- Current state: each clinic manages its own billing and collections, resulting in inconsistent processes and elevated DSO (52 days)
- Centralize billing operations into a shared services center, implement unified billing software (WebPT or similar)
- Expected impact: reduce DSO from 52 to 38-40 days, improve collections yield by 3-5%, reduce billing FTEs by 20%
- Estimated annual savings: $1.5-2.0M

### Lever 3 -- Procurement Centralization
- Consolidate purchasing of medical supplies, equipment, and therapy materials across all clinics
- Leverage platform scale for volume discounts with top 5 suppliers
- Estimated annual savings: $0.8-1.2M (5-8% of addressable spend)

### Lever 4 -- Margin Improvement
- Combined levers (billing + procurement + G&A rationalization) expected to add 100-200 bps to EBITDA margin
- Target EBITDA margin: 18-19% by Year 4 (from 17.0% at entry)
- G&A leverage as platform scales: corporate overhead does not scale linearly with clinic count

## Return Analysis

| Scenario | Exit Multiple | MOIC | IRR | Key Assumption |
|----------|--------------|------|-----|----------------|
| **Bull** | 11.0x | 3.8x | 25% | Full expansion (32 clinics), margin to 19%, 3 add-ons |
| **Base** | 10.0x | 2.7x | 18% | 28-30 clinics, margin stable at 16-17%, 2 add-ons |
| **Bear** | 9.0x | 1.8x | 10% | Limited expansion, margin flat, 1 add-on |
| **Downside** | 8.0x | 1.2x | 3% | Reimbursement cuts, therapist shortage, no expansion |

**Recommendation:** Base case 2.7x / 18% IRR is achievable with moderate execution. Platform has demonstrated consistent cash flow generation, and the fragmented market provides a deep pipeline of add-on opportunities. Downside is protected by the recurring nature of patient visits and essential healthcare demand.

## Key Risks Identified at Underwriting

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement cuts | Low | High | Diversify payer mix toward commercial; direct access growth reduces physician referral dependency |
| Physical therapist recruitment and retention | Medium | High | Competitive compensation, clinical development programs, mentorship model |
| De novo clinic ramp slower than plan | Medium | Medium | Conservative underwriting (12-month breakeven); proven playbook from prior ownership |
| Add-on integration execution | Medium | Medium | Standardized integration checklist; centralized billing enables rapid onboarding |
| Regulatory changes (scope of practice) | Low | Medium | Monitor state-level; trend toward expanded PT scope is favorable |
| Key person (Dr. Reeves) | Low | Medium | Employment agreement, equity rollover, depth of clinical leadership |

## Sector Due Diligence Highlights

- Physical therapy is a needs-based, non-deferrable service -- patients require rehabilitation post-surgery, post-injury, and for chronic conditions
- Low technology disruption risk -- PT requires in-person, hands-on clinical delivery
- Recession resilience: outpatient rehab volumes declined only 3-5% in 2008-2009 downturn, recovering within 12 months
- Favorable regulatory trajectory: direct access expanding, value-based care models incentivize outpatient over inpatient settings
- Acquirer universe for exit is deep: large PE-backed platforms (ATI, US Physical Therapy, Upstream Rehabilitation), health systems, and strategic acquirers
