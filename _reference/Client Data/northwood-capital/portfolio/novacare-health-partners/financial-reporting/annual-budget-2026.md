## Key Budget Assumptions

### Revenue
- **Organic growth (existing 28 clinics):** 7.0% through volume growth and mid-year rate increases (~3% commercial, 2% Medicare)
- **De novo clinic contribution:** 2 new clinics opening Q3 2026; combined $4.0M in partial-year revenue
- **Patient volume:** Average 29,500 visits per month across existing clinics (28,700 LTM)
- **Revenue per visit:** Gradual increase from $510 to $525 through payer mix and rate improvements
- **Net patient retention:** 92% of active patients continue treatment through prescribed plan

### Cost of Goods Sold (Clinical Delivery)
- **Therapist wage inflation:** 5.0% average increase effective March 1
- **Therapist headcount:** Net add of 12 licensed PTs/PTAs (8 for de novo clinics, 4 replacement/growth)
- **Clinic operating costs:** 3% inflation on rent, utilities, supplies
- **Gross margin:** Flat to +10 bps; wage inflation offset by volume leverage and rate increases

### SG&A
- **De novo startup costs:** $350K per clinic (leasehold improvements, equipment, initial operating losses) -- $700K total
- **Corporate headcount adds:** 2 FTEs (1 clinical recruiter, 1 billing specialist)
- **EHR standardization completion:** $180K to migrate remaining 4 clinics
- **Marketing:** $1.2M (physician referral programs, community outreach for de novo markets)

### Capital Expenditures
| Category | Amount ($M) | Notes |
|----------|------------|-------|
| De novo clinic buildout | $0.7 | 2 clinics at $350K each |
| Existing clinic refresh | $0.5 | Equipment replacement, leasehold updates |
| EHR/technology | $0.3 | EHR migration completion, scheduling tool |
| Corporate | $0.1 | Office equipment |
| **Total Capex** | **$1.6** | |

### Working Capital
- **DSO target:** 38-40 days (stable from FY 2025)
- **DPO target:** 28-30 days (stable)
- **Net working capital as % of revenue:** 4.5% (stable)

## Risks to Budget

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|
| Therapist wage inflation above 5% | 30 bps margin compression per incremental 2% | Medium | Annual competitive wage survey; retention programs reduce replacement cost |
| De novo clinic ramp slower than plan | $1-2M revenue shortfall | Medium | Conservative 12-month breakeven assumption; proven playbook |
| Medicare reimbursement rate changes | $1-2M revenue impact if adverse | Low | CMS rule monitoring; commercial payer diversification |
| Therapist recruitment for de novo clinics | Delayed opening, reduced capacity | Medium | Recruiting pipeline seeded 6 months pre-opening; signing bonuses budgeted |
| Patient volume softness from economic cycle | 3-5% volume decline | Low | PT is needs-based; limited deferral for post-surgical and injury patients |
