## Executive Summary

January revenues of $14.1M were below budget by $0.2M (-1.4%) and reflect the typical Q1 seasonal dip from insurance deductible resets. Patient visits of 26,800 were 2.5% below budget as patients deferred elective PT appointments early in the year. EBITDA of $2.16M (15.3% margin) was 80 bps below budget margin, driven by the volume shortfall and fixed cost deleveraging. Year-over-year revenue growth of 6.0% remained healthy. Net leverage declined to 1.4x. All covenants fully compliant with substantial headroom.

## 2. Variance Commentary

### Revenue (-$200K vs. Budget)
- **Patient visits** were 2.5% below budget (26,800 actual vs. 27,500 budget), consistent with the annual deductible reset pattern. Patients with high-deductible health plans defer elective PT visits in January until deductibles are met. This effect is well-documented in NovaCare's historical data (January is the lowest-volume month every year).
- **Revenue per visit** of $526 was in line with budget ($520), reflecting continued success in payer rate negotiations and favorable mix shift toward commercial payers.
- **No structural concerns.** February appointment bookings are tracking ahead of January by 8%, consistent with historical recovery pattern.

### Gross Profit (-$150K vs. Budget)
- **Volume shortfall** was the primary driver. Clinical delivery costs are ~60% fixed (therapist salaries, clinic rent) and do not flex proportionally with patient volume declines.
- **Therapist wages** were on budget. No unplanned overtime or agency staffing required.
- **Clinic operating costs** (rent, utilities, supplies) were $50K favorable to budget from timing of supply orders.

### SG&A (+$10K vs. Budget)
- Essentially on plan. Minor favorable variance from delayed timing of a marketing campaign launch (shifted to February).

## 7. Outlook & Risks

**Positive Indicators:**
- February appointment bookings tracking 8% above January, consistent with deductible reset recovery pattern
- Two commercial payer rate increases effective April 1 (BlueCross +2.5%, Aetna +3.0%)
- De novo clinic site selection advancing; two markets shortlisted
- Therapist recruiting pipeline has 6 candidates in final stages for Q2 hires

**Watch Items:**
- CMS proposed rule on outpatient rehabilitation payment rates expected Q2 2026; monitoring for potential impact to Medicare reimbursement (currently 28% of revenue)
- Therapist turnover stable at 18% but still 300 bps above the 15% target; retention initiatives continuing
- One clinic (Clinic #22, acquired via RehabWorks) underperforming on utilization (74% vs. 85% target); COO investigating staffing and referral pipeline
