## Critical Observations

1. **January revenue dip is seasonal, not structural.** Revenue of $14.1M in January reflects the typical Q1 deductible reset effect in outpatient rehabilitation. Patients defer elective PT visits early in the year when out-of-pocket costs reset. Historical pattern shows January as the lowest revenue month, with recovery beginning in March and full normalization by April.

2. **Therapist retention improving but not yet at target.** Annual retention has improved from 78% at acquisition to 82% currently, driven by competitive wage adjustments and clinical mentorship programs. The 85% target remains 300 bps away. Management has identified scheduling flexibility as the next lever and is piloting a new scheduling tool at 4 clinics.

3. **Margin slightly below plan but stable.** LTM EBITDA margin of 16.0% is 50 bps below budget, primarily due to therapist wage inflation running at ~5% YoY (vs. 3% budgeted). Volume leverage and centralized billing savings are partially offsetting. Management expects margin stabilization at 16.0-16.5% in 2026.

4. **Leverage profile is excellent.** At 1.4x, NovaCare has significant balance sheet capacity for the 2026 de novo expansion and potential third add-on. The company could fund 2 de novo clinics (~$700K total investment) and a $20-30M add-on entirely from cash flow and revolver capacity.

5. **De novo pipeline advancing.** Two clinic locations are in site selection for 2026 openings. If the historical 9-12 month ramp-to-breakeven playbook holds, these clinics will contribute positively to 2027 EBITDA.
