December patient visits were soft (-3% vs. plan) due to holiday scheduling, partially offset by strong October and November volumes.

## Therapist Retention Update

| Metric | Q4 2025 | Q3 2025 | Q4 2024 | Target |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| Annualized Turnover | 18% | 18% | 20% | 15% |
| Open PT Positions | 8 | 10 | 14 | <8 |
| Avg Days to Fill | 52 | 58 | 65 | 45 |
| Retention Rate (ann.) | 82% | 82% | 80% | 85% |

**Progress:**
- Turnover declined from 22% at entry (2019) to 18% in Q4 2025 through competitive wage adjustments, clinical mentorship program, and CE reimbursement
- Cost per hire stabilized at $4,200 (down from $4,800 peak in Q2 2025)
- New initiative: piloting flexible scheduling tool at 4 clinics; early data shows improved therapist satisfaction scores (+6 pts NPS)

**Remaining Gap:** 300 bps from the 15% turnover target. Management believes the flexible scheduling pilot, if successful, can close 100-150 bps. The remaining gap may reflect structural market conditions (national PT turnover is ~20%).
