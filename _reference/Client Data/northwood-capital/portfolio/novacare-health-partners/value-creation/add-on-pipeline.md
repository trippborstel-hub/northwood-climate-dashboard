## Active Pipeline

### Target 1: Bayside Physical Therapy

**Stage:** Early Conversations
**Priority:** HIGH
**Northwood Lead:** Priya Kapoor, Partner
**NovaCare Lead:** Dr. Sandra Reeves, CEO

#### Company Overview
Bayside Physical Therapy operates 5 outpatient PT clinics in the Virginia Beach / Hampton Roads metropolitan area. Founded in 2010 by a licensed PT who has built a strong reputation for sports medicine and orthopedic rehabilitation. The practice has relationships with 3 major health systems and serves a significant military/veteran population (proximity to Naval Station Norfolk). The founder (age 58) has expressed interest in a liquidity event within 12-18 months but has not retained an advisor.

#### Financial Profile (Estimated)
| Metric | 2024E | 2025E |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Revenue | $13.0M | $14.0M |
| EBITDA | $1.9M | $2.1M |
| EBITDA Margin | 14.6% | 15.0% |
| Clinics | 5 | 5 |
| Licensed PTs | 22 | 24 |
| Patient Visits/Year | 28,000 | 30,000 |

#### Strategic Rationale
1. Virginia Beach is one of the two markets selected for 2026 de novo expansion -- acquiring Bayside would accelerate market entry and eliminate the need for a greenfield launch
2. Military/veteran patient base is attractive: steady referral volume, favorable reimbursement, less cyclical than commercial
3. 5 clinics at established performance levels vs. 12-month ramp risk of a de novo clinic
4. Founder's referral network and health system relationships would transfer with the practice
5. $0.6-0.8M identified synergies from centralized billing, procurement, and EHR integration

#### Estimated Valuation Range
- 7.0-8.0x EBITDA (~$14.7-16.8M EV)
- Higher multiple justified by established market position, military referral base, and strategic fit with de novo plan

#### Next Steps
- Dr. Reeves to schedule introductory meeting with founder (target March 2026)
- Request preliminary financial data under NDA
- Assess clinical quality, therapist retention, and payer mix
- If attractive, could replace Virginia Beach de novo with acquisition (higher initial cost but faster and lower execution risk)

### Target 2: Keystone Rehab Associates

**Stage:** Monitoring
**Priority:** MEDIUM
**Northwood Lead:** Thomas Chen, Senior Associate
**NovaCare Lead:** Amanda Torres, COO

#### Company Overview
Keystone Rehab Associates operates 3 outpatient PT clinics in the Harrisburg and Lancaster, Pennsylvania markets. The practice was founded in 2005 by two partners (one clinical, one business) who are considering retirement options over the next 2-3 years. Revenue is approximately $9.0M with an estimated EBITDA of $1.3M (14.4% margin). The practice has strong physician referral networks and serves a mix of commercial, Medicare, and workers' compensation patients.

#### Preliminary Assessment
- **Pros:** Harrisburg is a target de novo market -- acquisition would provide immediate scale; established referral relationships; complementary payer mix (higher workers' comp exposure); two experienced clinic directors could lead expansion
- **Cons:** Two-partner ownership may complicate negotiation timeline; owners not urgently motivated; practice management systems are outdated
- **Estimated multiple:** 6.5-7.5x EBITDA ($8.5-9.8M EV)

#### Monitoring Plan
- Quarterly check-in calls maintained by NovaCare COO
- Track Harrisburg market dynamics (competitor openings, physician group changes)
- Re-evaluate in Q3 2026 or if seller motivation accelerates
- If de novo clinic in Harrisburg opens first, acquisition rationale shifts from market entry to market consolidation

### Target 3: Chesapeake PT Group

**Stage:** Monitoring
**Priority:** MEDIUM
**Northwood Lead:** Thomas Chen, Senior Associate

#### Company Overview
Chesapeake PT Group operates 3 clinics in the Baltimore metropolitan area, specializing in orthopedic and post-surgical rehabilitation. Revenue is approximately $7.0M with an estimated EBITDA of $1.0M (14.3% margin). The founder/owner is a licensed PT who has expressed general interest in a partner or acquirer. The practice has been identified through NovaCare's physician referral network -- several referring physicians work across NovaCare's Maryland locations and the Chesapeake clinics.

#### Preliminary Assessment
- **Pros:** Baltimore is a natural geographic extension of NovaCare's existing MD footprint (RehabWorks clinics in eastern MD); strong clinical reputation; physician referral overlap creates warm relationship
- **Cons:** Baltimore is a competitive PT market with several large players; practice is small ($7M revenue); integration would require dedicated attention
- **Estimated multiple:** 6.0-7.0x EBITDA ($6.0-7.0M EV)

#### Monitoring Plan
- Maintain relationship through shared physician network
- Monitor for change in seller motivation
- Consider as a Year 7-8 opportunity, or earlier if an opportunistic situation arises

### Target 4: Summit Sports Medicine

**Stage:** Monitoring
**Priority:** LOW
**Northwood Lead:** Thomas Chen, Senior Associate

#### Company Overview
Summit Sports Medicine is a single-location PT practice in Raleigh, NC, with a specialty in sports rehabilitation and return-to-play programming. Revenue is approximately $5.0M with an estimated EBITDA of $0.7M (14.0% margin). The practice has a strong brand in the local sports medicine community and relationships with several college and high school athletic programs.

#### Preliminary Assessment
- **Pros:** Raleigh is a high-growth MSA; sports medicine specialty is differentiated and higher-margin; potential to expand Summit's brand into a multi-clinic model
- **Cons:** Single location; small scale ($5M) may not justify standalone transaction costs; geographic distance from core Mid-Atlantic footprint; owner motivation uncertain
- **Estimated multiple:** 5.5-6.5x EBITDA ($3.9-4.6M EV)

#### Monitoring Plan
- Track Raleigh market developments
- Re-evaluate if NovaCare establishes a presence in NC through other means
- Consider as a potential bolt-on only if a larger NC acquisition creates a geographic anchor

## Pipeline Screening Criteria

All targets are evaluated against the following criteria established by Northwood and NovaCare management:

| Criterion | Requirement | Flexibility |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Revenue | $5-25M | Higher considered for strategic value |
| EBITDA Margin | >12% | Lower acceptable with clear synergy path |
| Valuation | <8.0x EBITDA | Up to 8.5x for highly strategic targets |
| Geography | Mid-Atlantic / Southeast US | Adjacent markets considered |
| Clinic count | 3-8 locations | Single locations considered as bolt-ons |
| Clinical quality | Above-average patient outcomes | Non-negotiable |
| Therapist retention | <25% annual turnover | Diligence focus area |
| Referral network | Established physician relationships | Critical for revenue stability |
| Owner motivation | Sale within 18 months | Monitoring status for longer timelines |
| Integration complexity | Low-medium | Dedicated integration team available |
