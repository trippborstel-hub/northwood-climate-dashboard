### Description
NovaCare's platform build is the primary value creation driver. The two add-on acquisitions (PhysioFirst at 7.5x, RehabWorks at 8.0x) added 10 clinics at multiples below the platform's implied value (10.0x), generating immediate multiple arbitrage. Integration has been successful -- PhysioFirst clinics are now performing at platform-average margins, and RehabWorks clinics are improving (with 2 watch-list clinics receiving targeted attention). The 2026 plan calls for 2 de novo clinics (Harrisburg, PA and Virginia Beach, VA) at $350K investment each, with 9-12 month ramp to breakeven and $2M Year 1 revenue target per clinic.

### Risks & Mitigants
- **Risk: De novo ramp execution.** Historical playbook shows 9-12 month breakeven, but each market is unique. Mitigation: conservative budget assumptions, proven site selection criteria, dedicated opening team.
- **Risk: Add-on pipeline quality.** Quality targets in the Mid-Atlantic PT market are increasingly competitive. Mitigation: proprietary relationships through physician network, willingness to look at smaller practices (3-5 locations).
- **Risk: Integration of remaining RehabWorks clinics.** Two clinics (22, 25) underperforming on utilization. Mitigation: mentor clinic directors assigned, targeted recruiting.

The $2.5M annual run-rate savings exceeds the $2.0M target from the original IC memo.

## Initiative 3: Therapist Recruitment Pipeline

**Objective:** Reduce annualized therapist turnover from 22% to 15%, stabilize clinical workforce, and build a recruitment pipeline capable of supporting platform expansion.

**Sponsor:** Amanda Torres, COO
**RAG Status: YELLOW**

### KPIs
| Metric | Entry (Sep 2019) | Current (Jan 2026) | Target | Status |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|--------|--------|
| Therapist Turnover (annualized) | 22% | 18% | 15% | **YELLOW** -- 300 bps gap |
| Avg Days to Fill PT Position | 72 | 52 | 45 | **YELLOW** |
| Open PT Positions | 8 | 11 | <8 | **YELLOW** |
| Cost per PT Hire | $5,200 | $4,200 | <$4,000 | **YELLOW** |
| Therapist NPS | 42 | 58 | 65 | **YELLOW** |
| Annual Investment (retention programs) | -- | $1.8M | -- | Ongoing |

### Description
Therapist retention has improved materially from 22% turnover at entry to 18% currently -- a 400 bps improvement driven by wage adjustments, mentorship programming, and CE reimbursement. However, the 15% target remains 300 bps away. The national PT turnover rate is approximately 20%, meaning NovaCare is already performing above the industry benchmark. The flexible scheduling pilot (launched Q4 2025 at 4 clinics) is showing early promise, with a 6-point improvement in therapist NPS at pilot locations. Management plans to expand the pilot to 8 additional clinics in Q2 2026.

### Risks & Mitigants
- **Risk: 15% target may be structurally unachievable.** Industry average is ~20%, and NovaCare is already outperforming at 18%. Mitigation: recalibrate target to 16% if flexible scheduling pilot does not close the gap.
- **Risk: Competitive poaching.** Large hospital systems and national PT chains continue to recruit actively with signing bonuses. Mitigation: NovaCare's clinical culture and career development programs are differentiators; focus on non-monetary retention levers.
- **Risk: De novo expansion strains recruiting.** Each new clinic requires 6-8 PTs, pulling from the same talent pool. Mitigation: dedicated recruiter assigned 6 months pre-opening; relocation assistance offered.
