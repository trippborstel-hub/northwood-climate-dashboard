## Investment Thesis & Value Creation

### Original Thesis

Regulatory compliance consulting is a non-cyclical, recurring-revenue business driven by increasing regulatory complexity across industries. Atlas was a well-regarded regional firm with deep expertise in healthcare and environmental compliance but had reached a scale ceiling under founder management. The thesis centered on three pillars:

1. **Buy-and-build:** Consolidate a fragmented market through targeted add-on acquisitions to achieve national coverage and cross-sell capability
2. **Vertical expansion:** Enter financial services compliance, a higher-margin vertical with stronger secular growth
3. **Operational professionalization:** Upgrade management, implement enterprise CRM and project management systems, centralize back-office functions

### Execution

**Add-on Acquisitions (4 completed):**

| # | Target | Date | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | RegTech Associates | Q4 2017 | Mid-Atlantic presence, healthcare compliance depth |
| 2 | Compliance Partners LLC | Q3 2018 | Southeastern footprint, environmental compliance |
| 3 | FinReg Advisors | Q1 2019 | Entry into financial services compliance vertical |
| 4 | DataGuard Compliance | Q2 2020 | Data privacy / GDPR / CCPA specialist capability |

All four add-ons were acquired at 5.0x-7.0x EBITDA, well below the platform entry multiple. Cumulative add-on consideration was approximately $45M, funded through a combination of revolver draws, cash from operations, and incremental term loan.

**Organic Initiatives:**

- Recruited Diane Chow (ex-Deloitte) to lead the newly created Financial Services Practice in 2019. The practice grew to ~25% of revenue by exit.
- Implemented Salesforce-based CRM across all offices, enabling cross-sell tracking and pipeline management for the first time.
- Centralized HR, finance, and IT functions across acquired entities, eliminating ~$3M in redundant overhead.
- Invested in proprietary compliance monitoring software tool, increasing client retention and enabling recurring subscription revenue.

### Outcome

Revenue grew from $78.0M to ~$160.0M over the hold period. Adjusted EBITDA grew from $14.4M to $25.9M (80% growth). EBITDA margin expanded from 18.5% to approximately 16.2% on a revenue-weighted basis; margin compression at the topline reflected the addition of lower-margin add-on businesses that were still being integrated. On a like-for-like basis, legacy Atlas operations expanded margins by approximately 300bps.

## Exit Process

Northwood retained Harris Williams as sell-side advisor in Q2 2021. The process was structured as a targeted auction with outreach to 12 strategic buyers and 8 financial sponsors.

The company attracted strong strategic interest due to its national footprint, financial services compliance capability, and proprietary technology tools. Three strategic buyers and two financial sponsors submitted final bids.

The winning bidder was a large global professional services firm seeking to bolster its North American regulatory compliance advisory practice. The strategic acquirer valued Atlas at 11.0x LTM EBITDA, reflecting a 2.0x turn of multiple expansion from entry.

Key drivers of exit premium:
- National platform with 6 offices across major regulatory hubs
- Financial services compliance vertical (fastest-growing segment)
- Proprietary compliance monitoring technology
- High client retention (93%+) and recurring engagement model

**Closing:** Q4 2021

The investment benefited from favorable timing: entry was completed before the post-2018 wave of regulatory complexity (GDPR, CCPA, SOX enforcement tightening), which drove sustained demand growth. The add-on strategy was the primary EBITDA growth engine, contributing roughly half of the incremental EBITDA at exit.

---

## Key Lessons

1. **Founder transition executed well.** Recruiting a strong replacement CEO pre-close de-risked the transition and enabled immediate execution on the buy-and-build thesis.
2. **Financial services compliance was the unlock.** The FinReg Advisors add-on opened a higher-margin vertical that became the fastest-growing segment and a key driver of strategic exit interest.
3. **Integration speed matters.** Back-office consolidation within 6 months of each add-on close allowed the team to maintain margins despite rapid growth.
4. **Strategic exits require narrative.** The combination of national footprint + vertical specialization + proprietary technology created a compelling acquisition narrative for strategic buyers that financial sponsors could not match on price.
