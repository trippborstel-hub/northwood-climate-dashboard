# Atlas Compliance Group — Exit Memo

**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund I, L.P.
**Sector:** Business Services / Regulatory Compliance Consulting
**Deal Lead:** Mark Sullivan
**Date:** Q4 2021

---

## Purpose

This memo documents the exit of Atlas Compliance Group, the first realization in Fund I. Atlas was sold to a strategic acquirer in Q4 2021, generating gross proceeds of $187.2M on invested equity of $62.4M (Fund I share), representing a 3.0x gross MOIC and ~35% gross IRR over an approximately 4.5-year hold period.

## Exit Process Summary

**Sell-Side Advisor:** Harris Williams
**Process Launch:** Q2 2021
**Process Type:** Targeted auction
**Contacted:** 12 strategic buyers, 8 financial sponsors
**Final Round Bidders:** 3 strategic, 2 financial sponsors

The strategic acquirer's final bid of $285.0M (11.0x LTM EBITDA) represented a meaningful premium over the next-highest financial sponsor bid (~$250M, ~9.7x). The spread reflected the acquirer's ability to extract revenue synergies by cross-selling Atlas capabilities into its existing client base, as well as cost synergies from shared infrastructure.

**Key Process Observations:**
- Strategic buyers uniformly valued the financial services compliance vertical and proprietary monitoring technology more highly than financial sponsors
- Financial sponsor bids clustered in the 9.0x-10.0x range, suggesting limited multiple arbitrage opportunity in a sponsor-to-sponsor exit
- The acquirer completed confirmatory diligence in 45 days and closed within 30 days of signing

## Value Creation Achieved

### Revenue Growth

| Component | Revenue |
|---|---|
| Entry Revenue | $78.0M |
| Organic Growth | ~$37.0M |
| Add-on Revenue (at acquisition) | ~$45.0M |
| Exit Revenue | ~$160.0M |

Organic revenue CAGR of approximately 8% over the hold period, driven by client expansion, new client wins, and the secular tailwind of regulatory complexity. Add-on acquisitions contributed ~$45M in incremental revenue at acquisition, with additional organic growth post-integration.

### EBITDA Growth

| Component | EBITDA |
|---|---|
| Entry EBITDA | $14.4M |
| Exit EBITDA | $25.9M |
| Growth | 80% |

EBITDA growth was driven approximately equally by organic expansion and add-on contribution. The four add-ons contributed approximately $6M in run-rate EBITDA at the time of their respective acquisitions. Organic EBITDA growth reflected improved utilization, selective pricing actions, and the natural margin accretion of the financial services compliance practice.

### Operational Improvements

- **National footprint built:** 2 offices at entry expanded to 6 offices at exit, covering major regulatory hubs (DC, New York, Chicago, Charlotte, Houston, San Francisco)
- **Financial services vertical created:** New practice launched via FinReg Advisors acquisition (Q1 2019) and organic build. Grew to ~25% of revenue by exit.
- **Client retention improved:** From ~88% at entry to 93%+ at exit, driven by deeper service integration and proprietary monitoring tools
- **Technology investment:** Proprietary compliance monitoring platform launched in 2020, creating recurring subscription revenue stream and increasing client switching costs

The original base case assumed a financial sponsor exit at 9.0x-10.0x. The strategic acquirer's willingness to pay 11.0x reflected the differentiated platform that was built during the hold period.

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked

1. **Pre-close CEO recruitment was critical.** The founder was ready to exit, and recruiting Richard Langley before close ensured continuity and immediate thesis execution. This should be the standard playbook for founder-exit transactions.

2. **Add-on strategy was disciplined.** All four acquisitions were sourced proactively, priced at 5.0x-7.0x, and integrated within 6 months. The team resisted the temptation to pursue larger, more expensive tuck-ins that would have diluted returns.

3. **Financial services compliance was a thesis-changing add-on.** The FinReg Advisors acquisition opened a vertical that drove both organic growth and strategic exit interest. This is a model for how a single well-chosen add-on can transform the exit narrative.

4. **Technology investment created exit optionality.** The proprietary compliance monitoring platform was a $2M investment that increased client retention, created recurring revenue, and was a key differentiator in the exit process.

### What We Would Do Differently

1. **Earlier financial services entry.** FinReg Advisors was acquired in Q1 2019, nearly two years into the hold. The vertical was so successful that an earlier move (or building it organically in parallel from day one) could have created even more value.

2. **Margin management during rapid growth.** Revenue-weighted EBITDA margin declined from 18.5% to ~16.2% as lower-margin add-ons were integrated. More aggressive cost rationalization in the first 90 days post-acquisition of each add-on would have improved exit EBITDA.

3. **Earlier engagement of sell-side advisor.** The strategic interest at exit was strong. Starting the process 6 months earlier would have provided more optionality and potentially attracted even more bidders in a less competitive M&A environment.

### Implications for Future Investments

- **Regulatory compliance is a strong sector.** Non-cyclical demand, high switching costs, and recurring revenue create favorable return dynamics. The team should continue to screen compliance-adjacent opportunities.
- **Buy-and-build at scale works when integration is systematized.** The Atlas playbook (centralize back-office within 6 months, retain client-facing teams, cross-sell immediately) should be templated for future platform investments.
- **Strategic exit premiums are earnable, not assumed.** Atlas earned its strategic premium through deliberate platform construction. This outcome should inform how we build other portfolio companies, but should never be underwritten.
