# Clearview Environmental Services — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** March 1, 2023
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Clearview Environmental Services, an environmental remediation, testing, and compliance platform, in a platform acquisition. The founder exited fully, and a professional CEO (Michelle Vaughn, formerly COO of a national environmental services firm) was installed at close. Management received a 20% equity rollover through a new incentive pool.

**Sector:** Environmental Services — Environmental Remediation / Testing & Compliance
**Headquarters:** Midwest
**Transaction Type:** Platform acquisition (founder exit, professional CEO installed)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

Environmental regulatory tailwinds — particularly PFAS enforcement and federal infrastructure spending — are driving sustained, multi-year demand for remediation and compliance testing. Clearview is well-positioned to build a national platform in a fragmented market. Key thesis pillars:

1. **PFAS / infrastructure tailwinds** — EPA finalized PFAS maximum contaminant levels (MCLs), creating mandatory testing and remediation obligations across thousands of sites. Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act funding is flowing to states for environmental cleanup. Both create durable, non-discretionary demand.
2. **Build national platform** — Environmental services is highly fragmented regionally. Clearview's geographic footprint (Midwest, Mid-Atlantic) can be expanded through targeted add-ons in the Southeast, Texas, and West Coast corridors.
3. **Recurring testing contracts** — Regulatory-driven testing contracts provide revenue visibility (35%+ of revenue at entry). Growing PFAS testing requirements will increase the recurring revenue share.
4. **Operational leverage** — Centralized project management, fleet optimization, and field technician scheduling at platform scale should drive margin improvement over the hold period.

## Seller / Founder Details

The founder built Clearview from a small environmental testing lab in 1996 to a $160M revenue platform over 27 years. The exit was driven by retirement planning and the founder's recognition that the next phase of growth — building a national platform through acquisitions — required a different management profile. Full founder exit at close with no transition period beyond a 90-day consulting agreement for client introductions.
