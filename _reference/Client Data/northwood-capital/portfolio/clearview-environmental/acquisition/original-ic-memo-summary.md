## Opportunity Summary

Clearview Environmental Services is a Midwest-based environmental remediation, testing, and compliance platform with $160M in LTM revenue and $25.6M in adjusted EBITDA (16.0% margin). The company provides site assessment, soil and groundwater remediation, environmental testing, and regulatory compliance services across 12 states. The founder is seeking a full exit after 27 years, creating an opportunity to acquire a well-established platform and install professional management to execute a buy-and-build strategy in a market experiencing generational regulatory tailwinds.

## Thesis Pillars

### 1. PFAS and Infrastructure Regulatory Tailwinds

The EPA's finalization of PFAS maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) creates a multi-decade remediation and testing obligation across thousands of contaminated sites nationwide. Estimates of total PFAS remediation costs range from $400B to $1T+. Simultaneously, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act has allocated $21B specifically for environmental cleanup, with funds flowing to states through 2026 and beyond.

These are not cyclical demand drivers — they are structural regulatory mandates that create non-discretionary spending requirements for both public and private site owners. Clearview is positioned directly in the path of this spending as a full-service environmental remediation and testing provider.

### 2. Build National Platform Through Add-on Acquisitions

The $50B+ environmental services market is highly fragmented, with thousands of regional firms operating at $10M-$50M in revenue. There is no dominant mid-market platform with national coverage. The opportunity is to build one through disciplined add-on acquisitions at 5-7x EBITDA:

- **Identified targets:** 25+ regional environmental services and testing firms across the Southeast, Texas, and West Coast
- **Integration playbook:** Clearview's centralized project management and EHS compliance systems provide a repeatable integration template
- **Geographic expansion:** Priority markets include Houston/Gulf Coast (petrochemical remediation), Southeast (military base PFAS), California (aggressive state-level environmental regulation)

### 3. Recurring Testing Contracts Provide Revenue Visibility

Approximately 35% of Clearview's revenue comes from recurring environmental testing contracts — typically 1-3 year agreements for ongoing site monitoring, water quality testing, and compliance reporting. As PFAS testing mandates expand, this recurring share is expected to grow to 45%+ over the hold period. Recurring contracts carry higher margins (20%+ vs. 14% for project-based remediation) and provide revenue predictability that supports both valuation and debt capacity.

### 4. Margin Expansion Through Operational Scale

The business is operationally intensive but has clear paths to margin improvement at platform scale:

- Fleet optimization across a larger geographic footprint (currently operating at 72% utilization)
- Centralized procurement of testing supplies, safety equipment, and subcontractor services
- Project management system implementation to improve field technician utilization from 73% to 80%+ target
- Laboratory consolidation (currently operating 3 labs; add-on acquisitions may bring overlapping lab capacity)

## Key Risks Discussed

1. **Founder exit / CEO transition risk** — The founder is the face of the business with 27 years of client relationships. Mitigant: Michelle Vaughn identified as CEO candidate pre-close. 90-day consulting transition for key client introductions. Top 10 client retention letters secured.

2. **EPA regulatory changes** — While current regulatory momentum is strongly favorable, a change in administration or EPA priorities could slow PFAS enforcement timelines. Mitigant: Multiple state-level PFAS regulations already enacted provide a floor on testing demand independent of federal action. 15+ states have adopted PFAS MCLs or are in rulemaking.

3. **Project-based revenue lumpiness** — Approximately 65% of revenue is project-based remediation, which can create quarterly variability. Mitigant: Growing recurring testing contracts (35% and increasing), diversified customer base (185+ customers, top customer 7%), and strong backlog provide smoothing.

4. **Field technician labor availability** — Environmental remediation requires certified technicians in a competitive labor market. Mitigant: Clearview has invested in training programs, certification sponsorship, and field technician career paths. Turnover is below industry average.

---

## IC Discussion Notes

The committee focused on the CEO transition as the primary execution risk. Vaughn's background (22 years, COO of a $300M competitor) was viewed as strong, but the founder's relationship-driven sales model requires careful transition. The 90-day consulting agreement was considered short; the deal team committed to extending to 6 months if client conversations warranted.

The regulatory thesis was viewed as highly compelling and differentiated from typical services deals. The committee noted that PFAS remediation is a generational spending cycle comparable to asbestos abatement — the demand is regulatory-mandated, non-discretionary, and multi-decade in duration.

Entry multiple of 9.0x was discussed relative to the 8-10x range for environmental services platforms. The committee was comfortable given the quality of the recurring revenue base and the growth outlook.

**Vote:** Approved unanimously.
