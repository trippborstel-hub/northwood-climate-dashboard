## 3. PFAS Market & Regulatory Update

### Regulatory Landscape

**Federal:**
- EPA finalized PFAS MCLs in April 2024 — now in enforcement phase
- Federal funding for PFAS remediation continues to flow through IIJA allocations
- EPA proposed expanded PFAS reporting requirements under TSCA — comment period closed, final rule expected Q2 2026

**State Level:**
- 28 states now have enacted or proposed PFAS regulations (up from 22 at acquisition)
- Illinois, Michigan, and Pennsylvania have implemented aggressive testing mandates creating new contract opportunities
- California PFAS testing requirements expanded to include agricultural water sources

### Clearview PFAS Revenue Trajectory

| Period | PFAS Revenue | % of Total | Growth |
|--------|-------------|------------|--------|
| 2023 (pro forma) | $5.0M | 3.1% | — |
| 2024 | $25.5M | 15.0% | +410% |
| 2025 | $35.1M | 18.5% | +38% |
| 2026 Budget | $38.0M | 17.8% | +8% |

*Note: 2026 budget is conservative. Management believes upside to $42-45M is achievable based on current contract pipeline.*

### Competitive Position
- Clearview is now one of the top 5 regional PFAS testing providers in the Midwest and Mid-Atlantic
- GreenTech acquisition brought EPA-certified PFAS analytical methods and proprietary sample preparation technology
- Competitive advantage: combined remediation + testing capability allows Clearview to be a single-source provider
