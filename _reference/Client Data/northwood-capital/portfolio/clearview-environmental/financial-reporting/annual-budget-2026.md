## Key Assumptions

### Revenue Assumptions
- Organic revenue growth of 10-12% driven by PFAS testing demand and remediation backlog conversion
- No add-on acquisition revenue included in base budget (incremental acquisitions would be additive)
- PFAS testing revenue budget: $38.0M (18% of total), representing 25%+ growth from 2025
- Customer count expected to grow from 185 to 200 through new geographic penetration
- Backlog conversion rate of 85% on existing $42M backlog
- New project win rate maintained at 35%

### Cost Assumptions
- Field technician wages: +4% average increase (market-competitive adjustment)
- Headcount additions: 25 net new hires (15 field technicians, 5 lab staff, 5 G&A)
- Fleet costs: +6% reflecting equipment additions for Southeast expansion
- Insurance premiums: +5% renewal increase
- Lab supply costs: +3% inflation adjustment
- Subcontractor costs: maintained at 12% of remediation revenue

### Margin Assumptions
- Gross margin improvement of 60 bps driven by higher-margin PFAS testing mix shift
- SG&A leverage as revenue grows faster than overhead additions
- EBITDA margin expansion of 60 bps to 16.7%

## Key Risks to Budget

1. **PFAS testing demand timing** — Budget assumes continued acceleration of state-level PFAS testing mandates. Delays in state rulemaking could push testing revenue to H2 2026.
2. **Seasonal weather impact** — Extended winter or severe weather events could compress the Q2-Q3 field season, impacting revenue and utilization.
3. **Field technician recruitment** — Budget assumes 15 net new field hires. Tight labor market could delay ramp-up and constrain capacity.
4. **Subcontractor availability** — Peak season demand may require higher subcontractor utilization at lower margins.
