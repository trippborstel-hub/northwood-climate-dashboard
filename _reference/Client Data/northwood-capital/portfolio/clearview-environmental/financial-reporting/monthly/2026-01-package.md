# Clearview Environmental Services — Monthly Financial Package

**Period:** January 2026
**Prepared:** February 12, 2026
**Distribution:** Northwood Capital deal team, Board of Directors

---

## Executive Summary

January 2026 was in line with expectations for a seasonally slow period. Revenue of $14.8M came in slightly above the January budget of $14.5M, driven by stronger-than-expected PFAS testing activity. EBITDA of $2.2M was on plan. Field technician utilization of 71% reflects the winter seasonal pattern and is consistent with prior year January (69%). The $42M project backlog provides confidence in a strong Q2-Q3 ramp as field season begins.

**Month RAG: Green** — on plan for seasonal period.

## Key Items for Northwood Attention

1. **Backlog at $42M** — strongest level in company history, providing approximately five months of revenue visibility. Supports confidence in Q2-Q3 seasonal ramp.
2. **PFAS momentum continuing** — new contracts landing earlier than budget assumptions. Management recommends accelerating PFAS lab capacity investment from Q3 to Q2.
3. **Two Midwest remediation projects delayed** — weather-related (not client or scope issues). Revenue deferred to February/March with no margin impact expected.
4. **Add-on pipeline** — management has identified two targets for deeper evaluation. Preliminary materials expected for March board meeting.

---

## Outlook

February and March are expected to remain seasonally soft, with revenue in the $15-16M monthly range. The field season ramp begins in April, with Q2 revenue projected at $56.8M per the annual budget. Current backlog and PFAS contract momentum provide upside to Q2 budget. No material concerns or deviations from the annual plan are expected at this time.
