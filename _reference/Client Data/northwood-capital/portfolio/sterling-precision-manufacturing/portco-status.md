## Summary

Sterling Precision Manufacturing is a strong performer in Fund I. Acquired in June 2020 at the onset of the COVID downturn, the company has capitalized on reshoring tailwinds and sustained aerospace and defense demand to deliver 53% revenue growth and 61% EBITDA growth through the LTM period. One add-on acquisition (MicroTech Tooling, Q1 2022) has been completed, adding precision tooling capability. The company has significantly deleveraged from 3.2x to 1.1x and returned $20.0M in distributions. At a 9.0x exit multiple on current EBITDA, the implied gross MOIC is 2.9x.

## Performance Summary

Sterling has outperformed the original underwrite across all key dimensions. Revenue growth of 53% has been driven by three factors: (1) reshoring-driven demand from domestic OEMs bringing supply chains back to the U.S., (2) increased defense spending flowing through to Tier 2/Tier 3 suppliers, and (3) the MicroTech Tooling add-on (Q1 2022) which added precision tooling capability and approximately $18M in annual revenue at close. EBITDA margin has expanded 90 bps from 17.1% to 18.0% through shop floor automation, lean manufacturing initiatives, and procurement consolidation post-MicroTech integration. The company has paid down $28.0M of debt and returned $20.0M in distributions ($15.0M dividend recap in Q4 2023, $5.0M cumulative tax distributions).

Defense revenue now accounts for approximately 38% of total revenue, up from 25% at entry, reflecting the company's successful pivot toward higher-margin, longer-cycle defense programs. On-time delivery performance remains above 96%, supporting customer retention and new program wins.
