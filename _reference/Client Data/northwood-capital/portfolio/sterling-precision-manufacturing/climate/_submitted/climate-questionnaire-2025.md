## Section 5: Emissions Tracking & Targets

**Do you currently track greenhouse gas emissions?** No. We track energy consumption (utility bills) and waste volumes (manifests), but we have not calculated a carbon footprint or emissions inventory.

**Do you have emissions reduction targets?** No.

**Do you report to any sustainability frameworks?** No. (No CDP, GRI, SASB, TCFD, or EcoVadis reporting.)

**Is there a designated sustainability or ESG role?** No. Environmental compliance (waste manifesting, air permits) is handled by our plant managers and contracted through Clean Harbors. There is no sustainability officer, ESG coordinator, or climate-related role.

David Okafor (Northwood Operating Partner) raised the topic in Q3 2025 in the context of this questionnaire request. ## Section 6: Customer & Supply Chain Requirements

**Have any customers requested emissions or sustainability data?**

Yes. Three RFPs received in 2025 included supply chain sustainability questionnaires or requests for carbon footprint data:

1. **Raytheon Technologies** (Q1 2025) — Supplier sustainability assessment form included in RFP for missile defense component program. We submitted the RFP but left the sustainability section blank with a note that we were "in the process of establishing a carbon reporting capability." Won the contract anyway, but the procurement contact indicated sustainability scoring would be weighted more heavily in future competitions.

2. **L3Harris Technologies** (Q2 2025) — Included an environmental management questionnaire as a required attachment. We completed what we could (waste handling, compliance status) but could not provide emissions data. RFP is still pending award decision.

3. **Boeing Defense** (Q4 2025) — New supplier qualification process now includes a carbon intensity metric request. We flagged to the program manager that we did not have this data. They said it was "informational for now, mandatory starting 2027."

**Our view:** This is a competitive concern that is growing. Our peers — especially larger machining companies and companies with European parent companies — can provide this data. We are at a disadvantage in new program competitions when we cannot respond to sustainability sections. Revenue at stake is difficult to quantify precisely, but these three programs alone represent approximately $25-42M in potential annual revenue.

**Do your suppliers provide sustainability data?** No. Our supply chain is primarily domestic metals and tooling suppliers. None have proactively shared emissions data with us.

## Section 7: Physical Risk & Business Continuity

**Have you experienced any weather-related operational disruptions?** Minor. The most significant event was the February 2024 ice storm that shut down the Akron plant for 2 days due to power outage and road closures. Estimated production loss: ~$350K in delayed shipments (recovered within the month). Otherwise, weather has not been a material operational factor.

**Flood zone status:** We have not conducted a formal flood zone assessment. The Akron and Elyria facilities are not in obvious flood areas. Canton is near an industrial park drainage area but we have not experienced flooding.

**Water dependency:** Precision machining uses industrial water primarily for coolant systems (closed-loop recirculating at both Akron and Elyria). Water consumption is moderate (~2.5M gallons/year total). Ohio is not a water-stressed region.

## Section 8: Energy Efficiency & Improvement Opportunities

**Have you implemented any energy efficiency measures?**

Partially. We have done the following:

- LED lighting retrofit at Canton warehouse (2022) — reduced lighting energy ~40% at that facility
- Variable frequency drives on 3 of the larger CNC machining centers at Akron (installed 2023 with CNC automation Phase I)
- Building envelope improvements at Elyria (new dock doors and insulation, 2021)

**What efficiency improvements have been identified but not implemented?**

1. **Complete LED lighting retrofit at Akron and Elyria** — Estimated cost: $120K. Our facilities manager estimates 18-month payback based on Canton results. Not yet budgeted.
2. **Compressed air system optimization** — A vendor audit in 2024 found 15-20% energy waste from leaks, pressure drops, and oversized compressors. Estimated repair/optimization cost: $80K. Estimated annual savings: $60-70K. Not yet budgeted.
3. **Heat recovery from furnaces** — An engineering assessment in 2023 identified potential to recover waste heat from the heat treatment furnaces for building heating (Akron only). Estimated cost: $250K. Estimated annual savings: $60-80K. Payback ~3 years. Not pursued due to capital priorities.
4. **Solar PPA evaluation** — Robert Kline has mentioned evaluating a solar PPA for the Akron roof but we have not conducted a feasibility study. The Akron building has a large, flat roof (~45,000 sq. ft. available) that could potentially support a solar installation.

## Section 9: Workforce & Community

**Apprenticeship program:** Sterling operates a registered apprenticeship program in partnership with Stark State College (Akron). Currently 35 active apprentices in a 3-year machinist training track. The program was launched in 2023 to address skilled machinist shortages. Retention rate of apprenticeship graduates: ~85%. The program has been recognized by the Ohio Governor's Office of Workforce Transformation as a model for manufacturing workforce development.

**Community engagement:** Sterling sponsors local STEM education programs at 4 Akron-area high schools and participates in Manufacturing Day events. The company has a good reputation in the Akron manufacturing community.

**Workforce demographics:** Average employee tenure: 8.2 years. Average age: 42. Voluntary turnover: ~8% (below manufacturing industry average of ~12%).

**Connection to sustainability:** The apprenticeship program and workforce development efforts have not been framed as part of an ESG or sustainability narrative. David Okafor (Northwood) has suggested this could be positioned as a strength in sustainability reporting — particularly around the "S" (social) dimension — but we have not taken any steps to formalize this.

## Section 10: Additional Comments

Energy cost is a significant and growing concern for us. At $1.8M/year, it is our third-largest operating expense. The demand charges in particular are painful and unpredictable month-to-month. Any support from Northwood on energy management strategy would be welcome.

On the customer ESG requirements — this is new territory for us. We are a precision machining company, not a sustainability consultancy. We understand the strategic importance based on the RFP experiences in 2025, but we do not have the internal expertise or bandwidth to set up carbon reporting on our own. We would appreciate guidance on what the minimum viable approach looks like and what it would cost.

The R-22 system needs to be replaced. We know this. It has been deferred twice. I am requesting that it be included as a priority in the 2026 capital plan. Every summer is a gamble with that unit.
