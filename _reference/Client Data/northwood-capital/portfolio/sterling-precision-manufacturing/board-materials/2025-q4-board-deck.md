**Full Year Commentary:**

These gains partially offset by continued machinist wage inflation (+6% YoY).

## 4. CNC Automation Phase II — Capital Approval

**Request:** Board approval of $5.5M capital investment for Phase II CNC automation program.

### Investment Summary

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Equipment | 8 new 5-axis CNC machining centers (5 DMG Mori, 3 Mazak) |
| Ancillary | 4 robotic loading/unloading cells for high-volume defense parts |
| Total Investment | $5.5M |
| Delivery | March-April 2026 |
| Full Commissioning | May 2026 |
| Full Run-Rate Impact | H2 2026 |

### Expected Benefits

| Benefit | Annual Impact |
|---------|---------------|
| Labor savings (12 older machines replaced, net reduction of 8 operator positions through attrition) | $0.6M |
| Throughput improvement (20% faster cycle times on 5-axis vs. multi-setup 3-axis) | $0.5M |
| Scrap reduction (single-setup machining reduces fixture-related defects) | $0.2M |
| **Total Annual Savings** | **$1.3M** |
| **Payback Period** | **~4.2 years** |

### Phase I Results (for reference)

Phase I (2022-2024, $4.5M invested) delivered:
- 15% throughput improvement on targeted work cells
- 80 bps scrap rate reduction (3.0% to 2.2%)
- $0.9M in annualized labor and yield savings
- Payback achieved in 3.5 years (ahead of 4-year plan)

**Management Recommendation:** Approve. Phase II builds on proven Phase I playbook. Equipment has been pre-ordered; this approval formalizes the commitment per board governance.

## 6. Second Add-On Evaluation

### Target Overview

Management has identified and screened two potential add-on targets:

| | Target A: Surface Treatment Co. | Target B: Sub-Assembly Services Co. |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Ohio | Indiana |
| Revenue | ~$20M | ~$25M |
| Est. EBITDA | ~$3.5M | ~$3.8M |
| Est. Multiple | 5.5-6.0x | 6.0-6.5x |
| Employees | ~60 | ~85 |
| Strategic Rationale | Vertical integration — in-house coating/surface treatment for defense parts (currently outsourced at $2.5M/yr) | Adjacent capability — sub-assembly services for complex defense modules |
| Status | Proprietary conversation | Broker-represented, early process |

### Management Recommendation

Advance diligence on Target A (surface treatment). Rationale:

1. **Vertical integration:** Sterling currently outsources $2.5M/year of surface treatment work. In-housing this creates immediate margin uplift.
2. **Defense program value:** Several defense programs require integrated machining + coating capability. Offering both increases win probability on new RFPs.
3. **Proprietary opportunity:** No competitive process — opportunity to negotiate favorable pricing.
4. **Risk:** Lower. Smaller transaction, adjacent capability, geographic proximity enables operational integration.

**Board Action Requested:** Approval to proceed with preliminary diligence on Target A, including management meetings and financial data review. Estimated diligence costs: $100-150K.
