## 1. CNC Automation Program

|------|---------|

| Scope | 6 new 5-axis CNC centers, shop floor data collection, process optimization |

| Item | Details |

## 3. Second Add-On Acquisition

### Strategy

Sterling's add-on strategy targets adjacent capabilities that enhance the company's value proposition to OEMs — enabling multi-process, vertically integrated manufacturing that reduces the customer's supply chain complexity. The successful MicroTech Tooling acquisition (Q1 2022) demonstrated the playbook.

### Target Criteria

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Revenue | $15-30M |
| EBITDA | $2.5-5.0M |
| Multiple | 5.0-7.0x |
| Capability | Complementary to precision machining (surface treatment, assembly, NDT, etc.) |
| Geography | Midwest preferred (operational integration) |
| Certifications | AS9100, ITAR, or Nadcap preferred |

### Current Pipeline

| Target | Revenue | Est. Multiple | Status | Strategic Fit |
|--------|---------|--------------|--------|--------------|
| Target A: Surface Treatment Co. (OH) | ~$20M | 5.5-6.0x | Proprietary conversation | High — vertical integration, $2.5M/yr currently outsourced |
| Target B: Sub-Assembly Services Co. (IN) | ~$25M | 6.0-6.5x | Broker process, early stage | Medium — adjacent capability, enables module-level delivery |

### Integration Approach

Based on MicroTech playbook:
- 90-day integration plan (back-office consolidation, ERP migration, procurement integration)
- Retain all production staff; combine G&A functions over 6-12 months
- Cross-sell to existing customer base immediately
- Target: full integration within 12 months, G&A synergies of 15-20% of acquired SG&A

## 4. Lean Manufacturing / Operational Excellence

### Program Overview

Ongoing lean manufacturing program led by COO Derek Williams and David Okafor (Operating Partner). Key elements:

1. **Value Stream Mapping:** Completed for all major product families. Identified $1.2M in waste reduction opportunities across material handling, setup time, and WIP inventory.

2. **Setup Time Reduction (SMED):** Reduced average setup time from 4.2 hours to 2.8 hours since 2022 (33% improvement). Target: 2.0 hours by Q4 2026.

3. **Inventory Optimization:** Reduced raw material inventory from 45 days to 35 days of supply. Work-in-process reduced by 20% through flow manufacturing implementation on high-volume parts.

4. **Quality System Enhancement:** Implemented real-time SPC on 60% of defense programs. Target 100% by Q4 2026 (aligns with Phase III automation vision).

### Results to Date

| Metric | At Entry (2020) | Current | Improvement |
|--------|----------------|---------|-------------|
| OTD | 91% | 96.2% | +520 bps |
| First-Pass Yield | 90% | 94.8% | +480 bps |
| Scrap Rate | 3.5% | 2.1% | -140 bps |
| Setup Time (avg.) | 4.2 hrs | 2.8 hrs | -33% |
| WIP Inventory | $8.5M | $6.2M | -27% |

## 5. Workforce Development (Apprenticeship Program)

### Challenge

Skilled machinist labor is the single largest operational constraint in precision manufacturing. The average age of a CNC machinist in the U.S. is 48, and the industry faces a projected shortage of 35,000+ machinists by 2028. Sterling competes for talent against automotive, medical device, and other precision manufacturing employers in the Midwest.

### Program Design

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Launched | 2023 |
| Structure | 2-year program combining community college coursework and on-the-job training |
| Partners | Two regional community colleges |
| Current Enrollment | 35 apprentices |
| Completion Rate | 82% (first cohort) |
| Conversion Rate | 90% of completers accept full-time positions |
| Annual Target (by 2027) | 15 new apprentices/year |

### Financial Impact

| Investment | Annual Cost |
|-----------|------------|
| Apprentice wages and benefits | $1.2M |
| Community college partnership fees | $0.1M |
| Training materials and equipment | $0.1M |
| **Total Annual Investment** | **$1.4M** |

The program is expected to reduce recruiting costs by approximately $200K/year (reduced reliance on staffing agencies) and improve retention (apprentice alumni show 40% lower turnover than external hires). Net cost after savings: ~$1.0M/year — effectively an investment in securing the company's long-term production capacity.
