## Add-On Strategy

Sterling's add-on strategy targets adjacent manufacturing capabilities that enhance the company's value proposition as a vertically integrated precision machining partner to aerospace and defense OEMs. The strategic objective is to reduce customer supply chain complexity by offering multi-process manufacturing — machining, tooling, surface treatment, inspection, and assembly — under one roof (or closely coordinated facilities).

### Acquisition Criteria

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Revenue | $15-30M |
| EBITDA | $2.5-5.0M |
| Multiple Target | 5.0-7.0x |
| Capability | Complementary to precision CNC machining |
| End Markets | Aerospace, defense, industrial (overlap with Sterling's customer base) |
| Geography | Midwest preferred; will consider other regions for unique capabilities |
| Certifications | AS9100, ITAR, Nadcap, or equivalent |
| Integration Complexity | Moderate — operational integration achievable within 12 months |

## Active Pipeline

### Target A: Surface Treatment Specialist (Ohio)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Type | Proprietary conversation (no competitive process) |
| Location | Northeast Ohio (~150 miles from Sterling HQ) |
| Revenue | ~$20M |
| EBITDA | ~$3.5M |
| Est. Multiple | 5.5-6.0x |
| Est. Consideration | $19-21M |
| Employees | ~60 |
| Capabilities | Anodizing, chromate conversion, passivation, painting, powder coating |
| Certifications | Nadcap, AS9100 |
| Key Customers | Defense primes (2 overlap with Sterling), commercial aerospace OEMs |

**Strategic Rationale:**
1. Sterling currently outsources ~$2.5M/year of surface treatment work. Acquiring this capability creates immediate cost savings and margin uplift.
2. Multiple defense programs require integrated machining + surface treatment. Offering both in a coordinated supply chain increases win probability on RFPs and reduces lead time by 1-2 weeks per order.
3. Growing regulatory pressure on chromate coating processes creates barriers to entry — Nadcap-certified surface treatment providers are scarce.

**Status:** Initial management meeting completed January 2026. Financial data package received. Preliminary analysis underway. Board approval to proceed with formal diligence requested at March meeting.

**Risks:**
- Environmental liability (surface treatment involves regulated chemicals — requires thorough environmental diligence)
- Founder/owner age 61 — transitional management risk
- Some equipment approaching end of life (capex requirement TBD)

### Target B: Sub-Assembly Services Provider (Indiana)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Type | Broker-represented process (early stage) |
| Location | Central Indiana |
| Revenue | ~$25M |
| EBITDA | ~$3.8M |
| Est. Multiple | 6.0-6.5x |
| Est. Consideration | $23-25M |
| Employees | ~85 |
| Capabilities | Mechanical sub-assembly, clean room assembly, electro-mechanical integration |
| Certifications | AS9100, IPC-A-610 (electronics) |
| Key Customers | Defense OEMs, satellite manufacturers |

**Strategic Rationale:**
1. Enables Sterling to move up the value chain — from machined components to assembled modules. Module-level delivery commands higher margins and deeper customer integration.
2. Clean room assembly capability opens satellite and space program opportunities that require both precision machining and controlled environment assembly.
3. Larger acquisition that would materially increase Sterling's revenue and employee base.

**Status:** CIM received January 2026. Preliminary review complete. Management is aware of the opportunity but recommends prioritizing Target A given the stronger strategic fit and proprietary dynamics.

**Risks:**
- Competitive process likely means higher multiple (6.0-6.5x)
- Geographic distance (Indiana) complicates operational integration
- Larger workforce integration (85 employees)
