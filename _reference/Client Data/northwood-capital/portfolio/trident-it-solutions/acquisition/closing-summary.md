## Transaction Context

Trident IT Solutions was a founder-led IT managed services provider serving mid-market businesses primarily in the Midwest and Southeast. The founder, who built the business over 13 years, was seeking partial liquidity while retaining a 20% stake and continuing as an advisor. The CEO role was filled by Kevin O'Malley, who had been COO under the founder and had been the de facto operational leader for 3+ years.

Northwood's thesis centered on building a mid-market IT services platform that combined managed services (help desk, infrastructure management, cloud migration) with cybersecurity — a capability gap in the existing business that was increasingly demanded by mid-market customers. The 85%+ recurring revenue model, high net revenue retention (108% at entry), and fragmented competitive landscape made Trident an attractive platform for add-on driven growth.

The 9.0x entry multiple reflected the premium commanded by recurring-revenue IT services businesses. However, the purchase price was justified by: (1) high revenue visibility (85%+ recurring, 3-year average contract duration), (2) strong unit economics (NRR above 100% indicating organic growth within existing customer base), and (3) a clearly identified add-on pipeline to accelerate growth and add cybersecurity capability.

## Original Investment Thesis

1. **Build mid-market IT platform:** Combine managed services and cybersecurity under one provider to capture the growing share of mid-market IT budgets moving from in-house to outsourced.

2. **Cybersecurity as the growth engine:** Mid-market businesses are underserved by cybersecurity providers (too small for enterprise-grade, too large for basic antivirus). Adding managed security services (MSSP) capability through acquisition creates a cross-sell opportunity across the existing 70+ customer base and differentiates Trident from pure-play MSPs.

3. **High NRR and recurring revenue:** The managed services model creates sticky customer relationships with average 3-year contracts. NRR above 100% means organic growth within the existing customer base — new services, additional seats, and scope expansion at renewal.

4. **Add-on driven growth:** The IT managed services market is highly fragmented — thousands of MSPs with $5-30M in revenue. Acquiring complementary MSPs and cybersecurity providers at 5.0-8.0x multiples builds scale, adds customers, and fills geographic or capability gaps.
