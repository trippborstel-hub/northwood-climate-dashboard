**IC Date:** December 2020
**Deal Lead:** Mark Sullivan
**Recommendation:** Approve — Proceed to Close
**IC Vote:** Unanimous approval

## Company Overview

Trident IT Solutions is a mid-market IT managed services provider headquartered in the Midwest, delivering outsourced IT infrastructure management, help desk, cloud migration, and technology consulting to mid-market businesses ($50M-$500M revenue). Founded in 2008, the company serves 72 customers across 8 states with approximately 180 employees. Revenue at underwrite was $85.0M with adjusted EBITDA of $16.1M (18.9% margin).

The business model is built on multi-year managed services contracts (average duration 3 years) providing recurring revenue of 85%+ of total. Services are delivered through a combination of a centralized Network Operations Center (NOC), field engineers, and virtual desktop support. The typical customer engagement begins with infrastructure assessment, progresses to managed services, and expands over time to include additional services (cloud migration, backup/disaster recovery, compliance).

## Investment Thesis

### 1. Build a Mid-Market IT Services Platform

The mid-market IT services market is large ($150B+ TAM), growing at 8-10% annually, and highly fragmented. Thousands of regional MSPs with $5-30M in revenue serve local markets without the scale, capability breadth, or sophistication demanded by growing mid-market businesses. Trident's 85%+ recurring revenue model, 72-customer base, and operational maturity make it an ideal platform for consolidation.

**Thesis:** Acquire and integrate complementary MSPs and cybersecurity providers to build a scaled platform that can serve mid-market customers across the full IT stack — managed services, cybersecurity, cloud, and compliance.

### 2. Add Cybersecurity — the Missing Capability

At underwrite, Trident had no dedicated cybersecurity offering. Cybersecurity was the #1 requested service by existing customers (per customer survey, 65% of Trident customers were either procuring cybersecurity from another provider or had no cybersecurity program at all). The mid-market cybersecurity gap is well-documented: these businesses face the same threats as enterprises but lack the budget for enterprise-grade security vendors.

**Plan:** Acquire a managed security services provider (MSSP) within 18-24 months of close. Cross-sell cybersecurity to Trident's existing customer base. Target: cybersecurity revenue growing to 20-25% of total by Year 5.

### 3. High NRR Validates Unit Economics

Trident's net revenue retention (NRR) at underwrite was 108%, meaning the average customer organically spends 8% more each year through additional seats, services, and scope expansion. This is a powerful indicator because:

- Revenue grows without new customer acquisition
- Customer relationships deepen over time, increasing switching costs
- Renewal rates are high (96% at underwrite) and improving

**Comparable benchmarks:** Best-in-class public SaaS/IT services companies (ConnectWise, Datto pre-acquisition, Kaseya) report NRR of 110-120%. Trident's 108% at a much earlier stage of platform development suggests significant room for improvement as cybersecurity is added.

### 4. Add-On Driven Growth

The IC memo identified a pipeline of 15+ potential add-on targets across three categories:

1. **Cybersecurity MSSPs:** 5 identified targets, $10-30M revenue, 6.0-9.0x multiples
2. **Regional MSPs:** 8 identified targets, $8-20M revenue, 5.0-7.0x multiples
3. **Specialty providers:** 3 identified targets (cloud migration, compliance consulting), $5-15M revenue, 5.0-7.0x multiples

## Competitive Landscape

Trident competes in the mid-market IT services segment — above the small MSPs serving SMBs ($1-10M revenue businesses) and below the large IT outsourcing firms (Cognizant, HCL, Infosys) that serve enterprises. This is the fastest-growing segment because:

- Mid-market IT budgets are shifting from in-house to outsourced at an accelerating rate (30%+ of mid-market companies planning to increase outsourced IT spend, per Gartner)
- Cybersecurity mandate is pushing mid-market buyers toward providers who can deliver both managed services and security
- Vendor consolidation — CIOs prefer fewer, more capable IT partners

**Key competitors cited:** Dataprise, Ntiva, InterVision, Flexential. All PE-backed, all on similar roll-up trajectories. The mid-market MSP space is early in consolidation relative to comparable services sectors.

## IC Discussion Notes

- **Valuation at 9.0x:** Zhao questioned whether 9.0x was defensible for a $16M EBITDA business. Sullivan argued that the recurring revenue profile (85%+), NRR above 100%, and clear add-on pipeline justify the premium relative to non-recurring services businesses (which trade at 6-8x). The committee agreed that the risk-adjusted return was attractive given the downside protection from recurring contracts.

- **Cybersecurity acquisition timing:** Harrison emphasized the importance of executing the cybersecurity add-on within 18-24 months to avoid losing the window. Customer surveys indicated existing customers were actively evaluating cybersecurity providers — if Trident could not offer cybersecurity, competitors would cross-sell into Trident's customer base. Sullivan confirmed a shortlist of 5 MSSP targets.

- **Founder dependency:** Kapoor flagged the risk of customer relationships residing with the founder. Sullivan noted that O'Malley had led the last 3 years of customer engagements operationally, and the founder's relationships were more strategic/advisory. The 18-month advisory agreement provides a structured transition.

Revenue is below the original Year 5 target primarily because only two of the planned three add-ons have been completed. EBITDA margin is on plan at 17.5%. The investment is performing above the base case return profile (2.8x MOIC vs. 2.5x base case) due to the higher exit multiple assumption (10.5x vs. 10.0x) reflecting the cybersecurity premium.
