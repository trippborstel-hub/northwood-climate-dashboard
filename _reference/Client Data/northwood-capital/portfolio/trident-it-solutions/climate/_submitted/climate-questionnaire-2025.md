## Section 1: Governance

**1.1 Does the company have a formal climate or environmental strategy?**

No. Trident is an IT managed services and cybersecurity company. Our environmental footprint is limited to office facilities and data center operations. We do not have a formal climate strategy document.

**1.2 Is there board-level oversight of climate-related risks?**

No. Climate risk has not been a board agenda item. Our board discussions focus on cybersecurity market dynamics, talent retention, and platform integration.

**1.3 Does the company have a Chief Sustainability Officer or dedicated sustainability role?**

No. Facility management falls under our VP of Operations. There is no dedicated sustainability role.

**1.4 How frequently does senior management discuss climate-related risks?**

Infrequently. Climate-related topics arise only in the context of client procurement requirements (see Section 5).

## Section 2: Risk Awareness

**2.1 What climate-related risks does the company face?**

Our risk exposure is modest relative to asset-heavy businesses. Primary risks include:

- Power reliability at our Cleveland Security Operations Center, which operates 24/7
- Potential business interruption from severe weather events at any of our eight facilities
- Rising energy costs, particularly for climate-controlled server environments

**2.2 Has the company experienced climate-related disruptions in the past 3 years?**

No material disruptions. Our Cleveland SOC experienced a 4-hour power interruption during a winter storm in January 2025, mitigated by on-site UPS and generator backup. No client service impact. Regional offices have had occasional weather-related closures (1-2 days) with no revenue impact due to remote work capabilities.

**2.3 Does the company have a documented business continuity or disaster recovery plan specific to climate events?**

Our BCP/DR plan covers all disruption scenarios including weather events. The Cleveland SOC has N+1 redundancy for power and cooling. All critical systems fail over to cloud-hosted backup infrastructure. This is a core competency — we sell BCP/DR services to our clients.

## Section 5: Transition Risk

**5.1 Does the company face regulatory risk related to climate or emissions?**

No. Trident is a professional services company with no regulated emissions. We fall well below any reporting thresholds.

**5.2 Are any of the company's markets or revenue streams at risk from energy transition trends?**

No. If anything, energy transition and associated regulatory complexity increase demand for IT infrastructure and cybersecurity services. Our services are sector-agnostic and not tied to fossil fuel industries.

**5.3 Are clients asking about the company's ESG or climate credentials?**

Yes. Healthcare and financial services clients increasingly require vendor ESG documentation as part of procurement processes. We can provide this documentation, and our low-emission service model is a competitive advantage in these conversations. This trend is particularly pronounced among our larger enterprise clients.

**6.2 What percentage of revenue comes from clients with their own net-zero or emissions reduction targets?**

## Section 7: Targets & Initiatives

**7.1 Has the company set any climate-related targets (emissions, energy, waste)?**

No formal targets. See 3.4 regarding SBT evaluation.

**7.2 Are there any active or planned sustainability initiatives?**

Several initiatives are underway or completed:

- **Cloud migration (ongoing):** Migration of client workloads and internal systems to cloud infrastructure has reduced our on-premises server count by approximately 40%, reducing facility energy requirements
- **Remote/hybrid work policy:** Approximately 60% of our workforce operates in a hybrid model, reducing commute-related emissions and office energy consumption
- **Cleveland SOC efficiency:** We are evaluating sub-metering options for 2026 to better understand energy allocation within our SOC operations
- **Client impact:** Our cybersecurity services enable clients to operate securely in remote and hybrid environments, reducing aggregate commute-related emissions across our customer base

**7.3 What would the company need to develop a more formal climate risk management capability?**

Minimal incremental investment. Our existing IT infrastructure and data management capabilities could support emissions tracking with relatively low effort. We would benefit from clear guidance from Northwood on the level of climate reporting expected and any specific frameworks to adopt.
