## Add-On Strategy

Trident's add-on strategy serves two objectives: (1) build scale in a fragmented market by acquiring customer bases and geographic coverage, and (2) deepen the platform's capability stack (MSP + cybersecurity + cloud + compliance) to increase wallet share per customer and create switching cost advantages.

The first two add-ons executed on different dimensions of this strategy — ShieldPoint Cyber added a critical capability (cybersecurity/MSSP), while NetWatch MSP added geographic coverage and customers. The third add-on should further extend the platform.

### Acquisition Criteria

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Revenue | $10-25M |
| EBITDA | $1.5-3.5M |
| Multiple Target | 6.0-9.0x (capability-dependent) |
| Recurring Revenue | 80%+ preferred |
| Capability | MSP, MSSP, cloud managed services, or compliance advisory |
| Geography | Northeast, Southeast, or Central U.S. (expansion from current Midwest/Southeast footprint) |
| Customer Base | Mid-market ($50M-$500M revenue) preferred; 20+ customers |
| Technology Stack | Compatible with ConnectWise/SentinelOne ecosystem preferred |
| Integration Complexity | Moderate — 6-12 month integration timeline |

## Completed Add-Ons

### 1. ShieldPoint Cyber — Q3 2022

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Location | Chicago, IL |
| Revenue at Close | ~$22M |
| EBITDA at Close | ~$3.5M |
| Acquisition Multiple | 8.0x |
| Consideration | ~$28.0M |
| Employees | 40 (including 18 SOC analysts) |
| Capability | Managed security services provider (MSSP) — SOC, MDR, incident response, vulnerability management |
| Funding | Incremental term loan ($15M) + Fund I follow-on equity ($13M) |

**Integration Results:**

| Metric | At Acquisition | Current | Commentary |
|--------|---------------|---------|------------|
| Revenue | $22.0M | $35.5M (LTM, combined cyber segment) | Includes organic cyber growth beyond ShieldPoint base |
| EBITDA contribution | $3.5M | ~$5.5M (estimated segment EBITDA) | Margin improving with scale |
| SOC Analysts | 18 | 28 | Organic hires to support customer growth |
| Cybersecurity Customers | 15 | 62 | Includes cross-sell to MSP base |
| Customer Retention (through integration) | N/A | 100% | Zero customer churn during integration |
| Integration Timeline | Planned: 6 months | Actual: 12 months | SOC integration complexity (SIEM platform differences) |

**Assessment:** ShieldPoint was the transformational add-on. It created the cybersecurity capability that is now the primary growth driver and key platform differentiator. The acquisition was more expensive (8.0x) and took longer to integrate (12 months vs. 6-month plan) than anticipated, but the strategic impact has far exceeded expectations. Cybersecurity now represents 25% of revenue and is growing 30%+ annually.

**Key Lesson:** SOC integration requires careful SIEM platform planning. The 6-month delay was caused by incompatible alert formats between ShieldPoint's SIEM (AlienVault) and Trident's NOC monitoring tools. This informed the decision to upgrade to SentinelOne XDR in the platform modernization initiative.

## Active Pipeline — Third Add-On

### Target A: Northeast MSSP (Primary Target)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Type | Proprietary conversation (no competitive process) |
| Location | Boston, MA metro area |
| Revenue | ~$12M |
| EBITDA | ~$1.8M |
| Est. Multiple | 7.5-8.5x |
| Est. Consideration | $13.5-15.3M |
| Employees | ~35 (including 12 SOC analysts) |
| Recurring Revenue | 88% |
| Capabilities | Managed security services, compliance monitoring, penetration testing |
| Key Customer Verticals | Financial services, healthcare, professional services |

**Strategic Rationale:**
1. **Geographic expansion:** Trident has no presence in the Northeast — a significant gap given the concentration of mid-market financial services and healthcare companies in the Boston-New York corridor.
2. **SOC depth:** Adds 12 SOC analysts to Trident's 28, increasing SOC capacity by 43% and enabling expansion without the 48-day average time-to-fill for cyber hires.
3. **Cybersecurity credential stacking:** Target holds several industry certifications (SOC 2 Type II, FedRAMP readiness) that complement Trident's CMMC pursuit.
4. **Proprietary dynamics:** No broker, no competitive process — management relationship through industry conference connections. Favorable pricing expected.

**Risks:**
- Owner/founder serves as primary customer relationship manager (key-man risk)
- Small SIEM platform (different from SentinelOne — integration will require SIEM migration)
- Higher cost of labor in Boston metro (analyst compensation ~15% above Trident average)

**Status:** Initial management meeting completed November 2025. Financial data package received January 2026. Preliminary analysis underway. Board alignment on strategy requested at April meeting.
