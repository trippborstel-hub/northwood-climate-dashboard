**Full Year Commentary:**

Revenue outperformed budget by $3.0M, driven entirely by cybersecurity growth. The cybersecurity segment delivered $34.5M in 2025 revenue (vs. $31.0M budget, +$3.5M above plan), growing 30%+ YoY as the cross-sell motion accelerated. The margin trajectory validates the thesis that initial margin compression from add-ons would reverse as the cybersecurity business scales.

Customer count ended slightly below target (115 vs. 118) — net additions were positive but slower than plan on new logo acquisition. The sales reorganization (dedicated cybersecurity sales specialists) implemented in Q3 is expected to accelerate new logo wins in 2026.

Headcount ended at 295 vs. 300 budget, reflecting competitive labor market challenges in cybersecurity roles. Open cybersecurity positions persisted through the year despite aggressive recruiting. The talent retention initiatives discussed later in this deck are designed to address this constraint.

### Strategic Options for Board Discussion

**Option A: Geographic Expansion (Northeast MSSP)**
- Pros: Adds Northeast coverage; brings 25 customers in underserved geography; MSSP capability creates immediate cross-sell
- Cons: Smaller transaction; integration of a 3rd SOC team adds complexity
- Est. Multiple: 7.5-8.5x

**Option B: Capability Deepening (Southeast Cloud Provider)**
- Pros: Largest revenue addition; strong cloud managed services capability; complements MSP/cyber offering
- Cons: Higher price (larger business); cloud integration technically complex; broker process = competitive pricing
- Est. Multiple: 8.0-9.0x

**Option C: Vertical Specialization (Government IT MSP)**
- Pros: Opens government vertical ($15M addressable); aligns with CMMC certification; sticky contracts
- Cons: Government contracting complexity; security clearance requirements; longer sales cycles
- Est. Multiple: 7.0-8.0x

**Management Recommendation:** Advance Option A (Northeast MSSP) as the primary target. Rationale: proprietary dynamics enable favorable pricing; geographic expansion is the most straightforward integration; MSSP capability adds SOC depth. Maintain Option B in the pipeline for H2 2026 evaluation.

## 7. Talent Retention Initiatives

### Challenge

Cybersecurity talent is the most critical constraint on Trident's growth. The cybersecurity labor market remains extremely competitive — demand for qualified SOC analysts, incident responders, and security engineers far exceeds supply. Trident's cybersecurity staff turnover was 18% in 2025 (improving from 22% in 2024), but above the 15% target.

### Retention Program — Launched Q4 2025

| Initiative | Investment | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Cybersecurity retention equity grants | $1.2M (one-time) | 2-year cliff vesting for top 15 cyber performers |
| Career ladder program (SOC I → II → III → Lead → Manager) | $0.1M (annual) | Clear progression path with defined comp bands |
| Certification reimbursement (CISSP, CEH, OSCP) | $0.2M (annual) | Full reimbursement + $5K bonus on cert completion |
| University pipeline (2 partnerships) | $0.1M (annual) | Internship program targeting 8-10 interns/year |
| Remote work flexibility (SOC shifts) | Minimal | SOC analysts can work 2 remote shifts per week |

### Results (Early)

| Metric | Q3 2025 (pre-program) | Q4 2025 | Trend |
|--------|----------------------|---------|-------|
| Cybersecurity voluntary turnover (annualized) | 20% | 15% | Improving |
| Offer acceptance rate (cyber roles) | 62% | 71% | Improving |
| SOC analyst satisfaction (survey) | 3.6/5.0 | 4.0/5.0 | Improving |

Early results are encouraging, particularly the reduction in annualized turnover from 20% to 15% in one quarter. The retention equity grants appear to be the most impactful component — exit interview data indicated compensation/equity was the primary driver of departures.
