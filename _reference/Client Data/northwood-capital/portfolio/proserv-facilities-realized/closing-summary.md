## Investment Thesis & Value Creation

### Original Thesis

ProServ was a Chicago-headquartered commercial facilities management company serving mid-market commercial real estate owners and corporate campuses. At entry, the business operated out of 5 regional offices with approximately 240 employees, generating $95.0M in revenue at $15.0M of EBITDA (15.8% margin). The company had strong client relationships but operated with informal processes, decentralized procurement, and limited technology infrastructure.

The thesis centered on three pillars:

1. **Operational professionalization:** Install professional management, implement standardized SOPs, centralize finance and HR
2. **Technology-enabled service delivery:** Build proprietary service management platform to improve dispatch efficiency, client reporting, and workforce utilization
3. **Procurement centralization and margin expansion:** Consolidate vendor relationships across regional offices to achieve purchasing scale

### Execution

**Operational Professionalization:**

- Recruited Carolyn Mitchell as CFO (Q1 2019) and installed ERP system (NetSuite) to replace spreadsheet-based accounting
- Implemented standardized operating procedures across all offices, including service-level agreements, quality inspection protocols, and safety programs
- Built a regional management layer (5 regional directors reporting to COO) to enable scalable growth without sacrificing service quality
- Expanded from 5 offices to 12 offices over the hold period, primarily through organic growth into adjacent metro markets

**Technology-Enabled Service Delivery:**

- Hired VP of Technology (Anil Krishnan) in 2020 to lead development of proprietary service management platform ("ProServ Connect")
- Platform provided real-time dispatch optimization, automated client reporting, mobile workforce management, and predictive maintenance scheduling
- Technology investment totaled approximately $4M over the hold period
- Client satisfaction scores improved from 82% to 91%, and the platform became a key competitive differentiator in client retention and new business development

**Centralized Procurement:**

- Consolidated vendor relationships across all 12 offices under a single national procurement function
- Negotiated volume-based contracts with top 20 suppliers (HVAC, janitorial supplies, electrical components), achieving 8-12% cost reductions on key categories
- Implemented vendor management system with performance tracking and competitive rebidding on an annual cycle
- Procurement savings contributed an estimated $2.5M in annual EBITDA improvement by exit

### Outcome

Revenue grew from $95.0M to approximately $155.0M over the hold period. Adjusted EBITDA grew from $15.0M to $22.2M (48% growth). EBITDA margin expanded modestly from 15.8% to approximately 14.3% on a blended basis; the margin compression reflected the geographic expansion into new, lower-density markets that had not yet reached scale. Mature markets (Chicago, Milwaukee, Indianapolis) operated at 18-20% EBITDA margins.

Headcount grew from approximately 240 to approximately 580 employees across 12 offices.

## Exit Process

Northwood retained William Blair as sell-side advisor in Q4 2022. The process was structured as a broad auction with outreach to 15 financial sponsors and 6 strategic buyers.

Financial sponsors drove the exit process. The business's essential-service characteristics, recession resilience, and technology-enabled platform attracted strong sponsor interest. Strategic buyers participated but were not competitive on price.

The winning bidder was a large-cap PE sponsor seeking a platform investment in facilities management as part of a broader business services roll-up strategy. The buyer valued ProServ at 10.0x LTM EBITDA, reflecting a 2.0x turn of multiple expansion from entry.

Key drivers of exit value:
- 12-office national footprint with scalable regional management model
- Proprietary technology platform differentiating ProServ from competitors
- Proven organic growth playbook with clear expansion runway
- Centralized procurement model creating quantifiable cost advantage
- Essential service model with high client retention (92%)

**Closing:** Q2 2023

## Key Lessons

1. **Technology investment creates exit optionality.** The ProServ Connect platform was a $4M investment that transformed the company's competitive positioning and was cited by every final-round bidder as a key differentiator. Technology-enabled services businesses command premium multiples.

2. **Operational professionalization is underappreciated as a value lever.** Replacing informal, founder-driven processes with standardized SOPs across 12 offices created a scalable business that a large sponsor could underwrite as a platform. The exit buyer's ability to envision ProServ at $500M+ in revenue was a direct result of the operational infrastructure built during the hold.

3. **Centralized procurement is a reliable margin lever.** The $2.5M in annual savings from procurement consolidation was achievable, measurable, and defensible in diligence. This lever should be prioritized in every multi-location services investment.

4. **Geographic expansion takes longer than planned.** Several new offices took 18-24 months to reach target utilization, longer than the 12-month assumption at entry. This margin drag depressed the overall EBITDA margin and should be factored into future growth-oriented investments.
