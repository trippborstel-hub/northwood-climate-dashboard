# Harvest Ridge Foods — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** April 1, 2025
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Harvest Ridge Foods, a mid-market contract food manufacturer and co-packer, in a platform acquisition. The founders (Meg Connelly and her husband, both former food scientists at Kraft) executed a partial exit with 20% equity rollover. Meg Connelly was retained as CEO. A new COO (Daniel Vasquez, former Conagra Plant Director) was installed at close to lead operational transformation.

**Sector:** Food & Agriculture — Specialty Food Manufacturing / Contract Co-Packing
**Headquarters:** Nashville, TN
**Transaction Type:** Platform acquisition — founders partial exit, 20% management rollover

## Investment Thesis at Entry

The clean-label and better-for-you reformulation wave is driving sustained demand for contract food manufacturers with dual-capability (organic and conventional) production. Harvest Ridge is well-positioned as a platform in a highly fragmented co-packing market. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Clean-label / better-for-you tailwind** — Consumer shift toward clean-label, organic, and better-for-you food products is creating sustained demand for contract manufacturers with formulation expertise and dual-line capability. Harvest Ridge's R&D heritage (founded by food scientists) and organic-certified Bowling Green facility position the company to capture this demand.
2. **Fragmented co-packing market — add-on strategy** — The contract food manufacturing market has hundreds of regional operators at 4-6x EBITDA. Strategy targets 2-3 add-on acquisitions in the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic to expand geographic reach and production capacity at attractive multiples.
3. **Founder-operator quality with capital constraints** — Founders built excellent customer relationships, deep formulation IP, and a clean regulatory record but lacked capital to scale beyond four plants. Northwood provides growth capital and operational resources to professionalize and expand.
4. **Margin expansion from operational optimization** — Production optimization, procurement consolidation across four plants, and capacity utilization improvement (currently ~72% across the plant network) offer 200-300 bps of margin expansion potential over the hold period.

## Seller / Founder Details

Harvest Ridge was founded in 2003 by Meg Connelly and her husband, both former food scientists at Kraft. They built the company from a single production line in Nashville to a four-plant, $145M revenue contract food manufacturing platform over 22 years. Growth was primarily organic, fueled by the clean-label and better-for-you reformulation wave that created demand for co-packers with food science expertise.

The partial exit was motivated by the founders' recognition that the next phase of growth — scaling beyond four plants through acquisitions and professionalizing operations — required institutional capital and operational resources beyond what the founders could deploy independently. Meg Connelly retained a 20% equity rollover and continues as CEO, providing continuity in customer relationships and formulation leadership. Her co-founder has transitioned to an advisory role focused on R&D.
