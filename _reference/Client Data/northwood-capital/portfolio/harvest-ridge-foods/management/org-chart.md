## Organizational Notes

1. **COO Hire:** Daniel Vasquez was recruited by Northwood pre-close and installed as COO on Day 1. This role did not exist under the founders — Meg Connelly managed operations directly with plant managers reporting to her. Vasquez now sits between the CEO and the four plant managers, bringing structured operational management, production scheduling discipline, and multi-plant coordination. This is the most significant organizational change since the acquisition.

2. **R&D Function:** The R&D/Food Science team reports directly to the CEO (Meg Connelly), reflecting her background and personal involvement in product development. This is intentional — R&D is a core differentiator and Connelly's direct oversight ensures formulation quality. The team operates from the Nashville HQ/R&D facility (HRF-005) with a dedicated test kitchen and sensory lab.

3. **VP Quality Independence:** Sarah Kim (VP Quality) reports directly to the CEO, not to the COO. This separation of quality from operations is a food safety best practice that ensures the quality function has independent authority to halt production if necessary.

4. **Bilingual Workforce:** Approximately 45% of production staff are Spanish-speaking. All food safety training, operational procedures, and plant signage are bilingual (English/Spanish). Bilingual shift supervisors are present at all four plants.

5. **No Dedicated Sustainability Role:** There is currently no sustainability, ESG, or environmental management role in the organization. Environmental compliance (wastewater permits, food safety) is managed by Sarah Kim's quality team and the plant managers. David Okafor (Northwood Operating Partner) has flagged this as a potential gap given customer sustainability data requirements.
