## 4. Chattanooga Plant Integration Update

**Facility:** HRF-004 — Chattanooga, TN (40,000 sq ft, cold-fill/high-acid production)

| Workstream | Status | Target Completion |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Unified production scheduling | In progress | Q2 2026 |
| Quality systems harmonization | Complete | — |
| Procurement integration | Complete | — |
| HR / payroll consolidation | Complete | — |
| Process heat assessment | In progress | Q1 2026 |
| IT systems migration | In progress | Q2 2026 |

**Key Issues:**
- The Chattanooga facility runs a different production process (cold-fill, high-acid) than the other three plants (hot-fill). Scheduling optimization across the network requires accommodating these process differences.
- Process heat configuration at Chattanooga is under assessment. The facility uses a different energy profile than the other plants, which impacts cross-plant production planning.
- The cold-fill capability is strategically valuable — it enables product categories (fresh dressings, cold sauces) that cannot be produced at the other three plants.

### Current Capacity

| Facility | Capacity Utilization | Revenue Contribution | Key Products |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| HRF-001 Nashville | 78% | $62.0M (41%) | Sauces, marinades, BBQ sauces |

| HRF-004 Chattanooga | 65% | $23.0M (15%) | Cold-fill dressings, high-acid products |
| **Network Average** | **72%** | **$152.0M** | |

### Capacity Constraints
- Nashville plant is approaching capacity on high-volume SKUs. Filling line automation ($2.5M capex in 2026 budget) will add ~15% throughput.
- Bowling Green has room to grow but water supply reliability is a concern for summer peak production.
- Chattanooga has the most available capacity but product mix is limited by cold-fill process.
- Network-wide target: 78% utilization by year-end 2026 (from 72% currently).

## Board Discussion Items

1. **Chattanooga integration timeline** — Current pace puts full integration at Q2 2026. Management recommends maintaining current timeline rather than accelerating, given the process complexity. Request board concurrence.
2. **Target A (Charlotte) engagement** — Preliminary indication that the owner may be receptive to outreach in Q2 2026. Recommend allocating deal team bandwidth for preliminary diligence. Estimated acquisition size: $22-28M EV.
3. **Customer Scope 3 requirements** — Three of our top 10 customers now require Scope 3 supplier emissions data as a condition of contract renewal. We are treating this as an administrative compliance item but want the board's view on prioritization and resourcing.
4. **Bowling Green water risk** — Summer 2025 pressure drops were managed through schedule adjustments. Recommend engaging a water engineering firm to assess backup supply options before summer 2026 production ramp.
