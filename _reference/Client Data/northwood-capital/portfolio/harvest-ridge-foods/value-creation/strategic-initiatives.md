**Last Updated:** February 2026

## Value Creation Plan Overview

The Harvest Ridge value creation plan is built on four pillars: (1) margin expansion through procurement consolidation, production optimization, and waste reduction, (2) add-on acquisitions to build a regional co-packing platform, (3) revenue diversification through DTC brand penetration and private label expansion, and (4) capacity and capability expansion to support $250M+ revenue at exit.

**Target Outcome:** Exit at 8.5-9.5x EBITDA in Year 4-5 (2029-2030) with EBITDA of $32-38M, implying enterprise value of $272-361M and gross MOIC of 2.1-2.8x.

## Initiative 1: Margin Expansion — Procurement, Production, and Waste

**Status:** On Track
**RAG:** Green
**Owner:** Thomas Nguyen (CFO — procurement), Daniel Vasquez (COO — production and waste)

### Objective
Expand EBITDA margin from 15.0% (current) to 17.0%+ by exit through three levers: procurement consolidation ($2.5-3.5M), production scheduling optimization ($2.0-2.5M), and food waste reduction ($1.0-2.0M). Combined target: 200-300 bps of margin improvement.

### Rationale
Harvest Ridge was founder-operated with four independently-managed plants. Each plant purchased ingredients and packaging separately, scheduled production through spreadsheets, and treated waste as a cost of doing business. Institutional ownership creates the opportunity to professionalize operations and capture savings that were invisible or impractical under the founder model.

| Action | Timeline | Status | Impact |
|--------|----------|--------|--------|
| Procurement consolidation Phase 1 (top 10 ingredients) | Q2-Q3 2025 | Complete | $1.2M annualized savings |
| Procurement consolidation Phase 2 (packaging) | Q1-Q2 2026 | In progress | $1.5M target |
| Procurement consolidation Phase 3 (distribution/freight) | H2 2026 | Planned | $0.5-1.0M target |
| Production scheduling software implementation | 2025-2026 | Nashville pilot complete; rollout in progress | $2.0-2.5M target (full network) |
| Preventive maintenance program | Q1 2026 | Launching | $0.3-0.5M savings from reduced unplanned downtime |

## Initiative 2: Add-on Acquisition Pipeline

**Status:** Building
**RAG:** Green
**Owner:** Thomas Chen (Deal Lead)

### Objective
Complete 2-3 add-on acquisitions over the hold period, adding $6-12M of incremental EBITDA at 4-6x acquisition multiples. Build geographic coverage across the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic, adding production capacity and customer relationships.

### Rationale
The U.S. contract food manufacturing market is a $75B+ industry with thousands of regional operators. The shelf-stable sauce/condiment segment is highly fragmented with no dominant mid-market platform. Add-on acquisitions at 4-6x create significant multiple arbitrage against Harvest Ridge's 8.0x entry valuation. Each add-on also brings incremental production capacity, geographic reach, and customer relationships that strengthen the platform.

### Pipeline Status

### 2026 Priorities
1. Advance Target A to LOI stage by Q3 2026. Charlotte is the highest-priority opportunity — strong product overlap, complementary customer base, and establishes Harvest Ridge's first Southeast production facility.
2. Evaluate Target B for potential parallel execution if Target A timeline extends. Atlanta private label specialist would accelerate grocery program growth.
3. Continue monitoring Target C. Organic specialty production at an East Coast facility would complement Bowling Green but pricing at 6-7x is at the top of the acceptable range.

## Initiative 3: Revenue Diversification — DTC and Private Label

**Status:** On Track
**RAG:** Green
**Owner:** Robert Hawkins (VP Sales), Meg Connelly (CEO — DTC brand relationships)

### Objective
Grow revenue from $152M to $200M+ organically over the hold period by expanding the DTC brand segment (target: 20% of revenue at exit, from 11% today) and deepening private label relationships (target: 30% of revenue, from 28%).

### Rationale
Harvest Ridge's customer concentration (top 5 = 45%) creates revenue risk. Diversification into the fast-growing DTC brand segment reduces concentration, adds higher-margin customers (DTC brands typically accept 2-3% higher pricing for formulation co-development), and positions the platform for the consumer trend toward direct-to-consumer food brands. Private label expansion leverages existing capabilities into a growing retailer channel.

## Initiative 4: Capacity and Capability Expansion

**Status:** Planning
**RAG:** Green
**Owner:** Daniel Vasquez (COO)

### Objective
Expand production capacity from ~$210M revenue capacity (at 80% utilization) to $250M+ through targeted automation, facility expansion, and add-on capacity. Invest in cold-fill capability (Chattanooga) and organic capacity (Bowling Green) as strategic differentiators.

### Rationale
The value creation plan targets $200M+ organic revenue at exit, plus $30-60M from add-ons. Current network capacity of ~$210M at 80% utilization is insufficient to support the growth plan without investment. Targeted automation (Nashville filling lines) and capacity expansion (Bowling Green) are the highest-ROI investments, while add-on acquisitions provide step-function capacity increases.

## Sustainability / Climate as a Value Driver

**Status:** Emerging
**RAG:** Amber

While not a standalone value creation initiative, climate and sustainability readiness is becoming material to Harvest Ridge's commercial position:

1. **Customer retention risk:** 3 of top 10 customers now require Scope 3 supplier emissions data as a condition of contract renewal. Combined revenue at risk: ~$34M. This is expected to increase to 6-7 customers within 2-3 years.
2. **Competitive differentiation:** Co-packers that can provide carbon footprint data and sustainability credentials will have a competitive advantage in winning DTC brand customers, who increasingly market sustainability claims on packaging.
3. **Food waste as dual opportunity:** Food waste reduction is both a cost savings lever ($1-2M EBITDA impact) and a sustainability KPI that customers and consumers value.
4. **Water risk management:** Bowling Green water supply reliability is both an operational risk and a climate adaptation question.

**Recommended consulting deliverables:**
1. Carbon footprint with data quality assessment
2. Water risk assessment (focused on Bowling Green)
3. Food waste quantification and reduction strategy
4. Scope 3 customer-readiness assessment
5. Sustainable packaging advisory
