## Summary

Harvest Ridge Foods is a mid-market contract food manufacturer and co-packer acquired in April 2025. The company serves regional and national CPG brands, grocery private label programs, and emerging DTC food brands across four production facilities specializing in shelf-stable sauces, dressings, marinades, and condiments. Founded in 2003 by husband-wife team Meg Connelly and her co-founder (both former food scientists at Kraft), Harvest Ridge grew organically through the clean-label and better-for-you reformulation wave.

The company is in the 100-day plan execution phase, with workstreams focused on production optimization, procurement consolidation, Chattanooga plant integration, and add-on pipeline development. Revenue has grown 5% from $145.0M to $152.0M on a LTM basis, and EBITDA has grown 4% to $22.8M. EBITDA margin is flat at 15.0% as the company absorbs early integration costs and new leadership onboarding. RAG status is Amber reflecting the early-stage nature of the investment, Chattanooga plant integration complexity, and the 100-day plan still being in execution.

## Performance Summary

Harvest Ridge is approximately 10 months post-close and executing against its 100-day plan. Revenue has grown 5% from $145.0M to $152.0M, driven by new customer wins in the emerging DTC brand segment and modest volume growth from existing CPG accounts. EBITDA has grown 4% to $22.8M, with margin flat at 15.0% as the company absorbs onboarding costs for the new COO (Daniel Vasquez, Northwood hire) and early-stage procurement consolidation investments.

The company operates five facilities: four production plants (Nashville HQ/primary production at 85,000 sq ft, Murfreesboro secondary production at 45,000 sq ft, Bowling Green organic/specialty at 55,000 sq ft, and Chattanooga cold-fill/high-acid at 40,000 sq ft) plus corporate HQ/R&D lab (11,000 sq ft in Nashville). Total headcount is 335, up from 310 at close primarily from production staffing additions at the Nashville and Bowling Green plants.

Customer concentration remains a key monitoring item — top 5 customers represent approximately 45% of revenue. The founders (Meg Connelly, CEO) retained a 20% equity rollover, providing alignment. The Chattanooga plant integration is the most complex workstream, with process heat configuration and production scheduling optimization still in progress.

Performance status is Amber, reflecting the early-stage nature of the investment and the importance of 100-day plan execution to the value creation thesis.
