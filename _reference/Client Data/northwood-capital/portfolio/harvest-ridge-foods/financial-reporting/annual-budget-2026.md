## Key Assumptions

### Revenue Assumptions
- Organic revenue growth of 10-11% driven by new customer wins and existing customer volume expansion
- No add-on acquisition revenue included in base budget (acquisitions would be additive)
- DTC brand segment budget: $21.5M (13% of total), representing ~29% growth — fastest-growing channel
- Customer count expected to grow from 82 to 95 through new DTC wins and private label programs
- Existing customer volume growth of 4-5% (contractual expansions and new SKU launches)
- New customer revenue contribution of $12-14M (pipeline of 8-10 new wins expected)

### Cost Assumptions
- Raw material costs: +3% average increase (ingredient inflation partially offset by procurement consolidation)
- Procurement consolidation savings: $2.5M gross (Phase 2 — packaging and secondary ingredients)
- Production labor wages: +3.5% average increase (market-competitive adjustment)
- Headcount additions: 20 net new hires (15 production, 3 QA, 2 G&A)
- Packaging costs: +2% inflation, partially offset by volume purchasing agreements
- Distribution/freight: +4% reflecting fuel cost increases and expanded delivery radius

### Margin Assumptions
- Gross margin improvement of 100 bps driven by procurement consolidation savings and production efficiency gains
- SG&A leverage as revenue grows faster than overhead additions
- EBITDA margin expansion of 80 bps to 15.8%
- Productivity savings from production scheduling optimization: $0.8M (reduced changeover waste, improved utilization)

## Key Risks to Budget

1. **Raw material cost inflation** — Budget assumes 3% ingredient inflation. A spike in soybean oil, organic ingredients, or packaging materials could compress gross margin. Each additional 1% of raw material inflation reduces EBITDA by approximately $0.8M.
2. **Customer concentration** — Budget relies on volume growth from top 5 customers (45% of revenue). Loss of any single top-5 customer would create a $4-8M revenue gap.
3. **Bowling Green water supply** — Summer 2025 pressure drops could recur. Production disruption at Bowling Green during peak season (Q2-Q3) would impact organic product delivery and margin.
4. **Labor market tightness** — Budget assumes 15 net new production hires. Nashville food manufacturing labor market is competitive. Failure to hire on schedule could constrain capacity ramp.
5. **Seasonal production concentration** — Q2-Q3 represent 54% of budgeted revenue. Weather events, equipment failure, or supply chain disruptions during peak season have outsized impact.
