## Executive Summary

January 2026 was in line with expectations for a seasonally soft month. Revenue of $12.2M came in slightly below the January budget of $12.5M, primarily due to a slower-than-expected post-holiday production restart at the Nashville plant (maintenance on Line 2 extended 3 days). EBITDA of $1.6M was modestly below plan ($1.7M budget), reflecting the revenue shortfall and higher-than-planned natural gas costs from a cold snap in mid-January. On-time delivery of 94.0% was below the 95% target, driven by the Nashville maintenance delay. Customer pipeline remains healthy, with 3 new DTC brand RFPs received in January.

**Month RAG: Green** — minor variances within acceptable range for seasonal period.

## Key Items for Northwood Attention

1. **Nashville Line 2 maintenance** — Unplanned extension (bearing replacement on filler). 3-day delay, ~$0.3M in deferred revenue. Recovered in February. COO Vasquez is implementing a preventive maintenance program to reduce unplanned downtime.
2. **DTC pipeline momentum** — 3 new RFPs received in January from emerging food brands. If converted, these represent $3-5M in annualized revenue. DTC is validating the thesis of this segment as a growth and diversification engine.
3. **Procurement consolidation Phase 2 kickoff** — Thomas Nguyen launched RFP process for consolidated packaging procurement across all 4 plants. Initial supplier responses expected in March. Target: $1.5M annualized savings on packaging (corrugated, labels, PET bottles).
4. **Chattanooga integration progress** — January was the first month where Chattanooga's production schedule was partially integrated into the network-wide scheduling tool. OTD of 96.0% was the highest in the network. Daniel Vasquez attributes this to better visibility into order flow and changeover planning.

## Outlook

February and March are expected to remain seasonally moderate, with monthly revenue in the $13-14M range. The production season ramp begins in April as grilling season and BBQ product demand accelerate. Q2 revenue is projected at $44.5M per the annual budget. Current customer pipeline and DTC momentum provide confidence in the Q2 ramp. The Nashville Line 2 maintenance catch-up is complete, with February production tracking to plan.
