## Summary

Ridgeline Property Services is a commercial property management and building services platform acquired in July 2025. The company manages approximately 18 million square feet of commercial office, industrial, and mixed-use properties on behalf of institutional owners (REITs, PE real estate funds, family offices) across the Mountain West and Southwest. Revenue is derived from property management fees (60%), project management/tenant improvement oversight (25%), and building engineering/maintenance services (15%). The company is in the 100-day plan execution phase, with workstreams focused on technology platform assessment, Las Vegas market entry, sustainability services development, and institutional client pipeline expansion. RAG status is Amber reflecting early-stage execution risk, the ongoing technology platform scalability assessment, and emerging building performance standard regulatory exposure in key markets.

## Performance Summary

Ridgeline is approximately 7 months post-close and in the 100-day plan execution phase. Revenue has grown 3% from $92.0M to $95.0M, driven by modest organic growth in property management fees and the commencement of new tenant improvement projects. EBITDA has grown 3% to $17.4M, though margin has compressed 10 bps to 18.3% as the company absorbs investment in the Las Vegas office opening, incremental COO compensation (Marcus Webb hired at close), and technology assessment costs.

The company currently manages approximately 18 million square feet across 6 regional offices and the Denver HQ, with a seventh regional office (Las Vegas) opened in January 2025 pre-close. The client base is predominantly institutional — REITs (35% of revenue), PE real estate funds (25%), family offices (20%), and corporate owner-occupiers (20%). Client retention is strong at 92% with an average contract tenure of 4.2 years.

The 100-day plan is progressing with some workstreams on track (Las Vegas market entry, institutional pipeline development) and others requiring more time (technology platform assessment, sustainability services scoping). Amber RAG status reflects early-stage execution risk, the critical technology platform decision, and the emerging but not yet quantified regulatory risk from building performance standards in Denver and Phoenix.
