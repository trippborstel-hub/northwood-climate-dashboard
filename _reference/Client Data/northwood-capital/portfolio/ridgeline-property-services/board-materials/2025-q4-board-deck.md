## 6. Technology Platform Assessment

### Current State

Ridgeline operates a proprietary property management software platform ("RidgelineOS") built in-house over the past 8 years by a 4-person development team. The platform handles:
- Tenant communication and work order management
- Operational dashboards and real-time building performance monitoring
- Financial reporting and budget tracking
- Vendor management and procurement

### Assessment Findings (Preliminary)

| Dimension | Assessment | Risk |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Functionality at current scale | Strong — clients rate RidgelineOS highly | Low |
| Scalability to 40M+ sq ft | Unproven — architecture concerns | Medium-High |
| Development team depth | Thin — 4 developers, no dedicated CTO | High |
| Data architecture | Monolithic — would require refactoring for multi-region | Medium |
| Integration capabilities | Limited — no open API for client system integration | Medium |
| User experience | Competitive — comparable to Yardi/MRI at property level | Low |

### Options Under Evaluation

| Option | Description | Est. Cost | Timeline | Risk |
|--------|-------------|-----------|----------|------|
| Option A: Scale existing platform | Hire CTO, expand dev team, refactor for multi-region | $2.5-3.5M over 2 years | 18-24 months | Medium — execution risk |
| Option B: Migrate to commercial platform (Yardi/MRI) | Industry-standard, proven at scale | $1.5-2.0M migration + annual license | 12-18 months | Low-Medium — proven path |
| Option C: Hybrid approach | Keep client-facing RidgelineOS, move back-end to commercial | $2.0-2.5M | 12-18 months | Medium — integration complexity |

**Recommendation to Board:** Management recommends further evaluation through Day 100 before committing to a path. CTO hire should proceed regardless of platform decision. Board input requested on risk tolerance and investment appetite.

## 7. Sustainability Services Opportunity

### Background

An emerging theme from client conversations and RFP activity is demand for building sustainability and energy management services. Four managed properties have requested energy benchmarking data in the past year, and two have asked whether Ridgeline can assist with ENERGY STAR certification. This represents a potential new revenue stream that leverages Ridgeline's existing building engineering capabilities.

### Market Context

- Denver and Phoenix are both considering building performance standards (BPS) similar to NYC Local Law 97, which would require large commercial buildings to meet energy efficiency and emissions targets or face fines
- Institutional owners (particularly REITs) are facing increasing ESG disclosure requirements that include building energy performance data
- The Big 3 PM firms have all launched sustainability services divisions; mid-market PM firms have not, creating whitespace

### Opportunity Sizing (Preliminary)

| Scenario | Addressable Portfolio | Revenue Potential | Margin |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Energy benchmarking only | 18M sq ft (all managed) | $1.5-2.0M/yr | 30-40% |
| Benchmarking + ENERGY STAR | 10M sq ft (institutional) | $3.0-4.0M/yr | 25-35% |
| Full sustainability services | New + existing clients | $5.0-8.0M/yr by Year 3 | 20-30% |

**Status:** Market research underway. Management is evaluating a pilot program with 2-3 institutional clients in Denver. Board discussion requested on strategic priority and resource allocation.
