# Ridgeline Property Services — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** July 1, 2025
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Ridgeline Property Services, a commercial property management and building services platform operating across the Mountain West and Southwest. The transaction was structured as a platform acquisition with founder partial exit — Kevin Drummond, the CEO and founder, retained a 20% equity rollover and continues to lead the business. Marcus Webb was recruited as COO at close to professionalize operations and reduce key-person dependency on Drummond.

**Sector:** Real Estate Services — Commercial Property Management / Building Services
**Headquarters:** Denver, CO
**Transaction Type:** Platform acquisition (founder partial exit, 20% rollover)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

Commercial property management is undergoing a generational shift as institutional property owners increasingly seek tech-enabled, operationally sophisticated alternatives to the Big 3 incumbents (CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield). Ridgeline is positioned to capitalize on this trend as a regional platform with institutional-quality service in the fastest-growing commercial real estate markets in the United States. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Mid-market platform opportunity** — Institutional commercial property owners are seeking alternatives to the Big 3 that offer more responsive, tech-enabled property management. The mid-market PM segment ($10-200M revenue) is highly fragmented with no dominant regional platform. Ridgeline's proprietary PM software and institutional service model differentiate it from both the Big 3 (too large, impersonal) and small local operators (lack technology, scale).
2. **Mountain West / Southwest growth** — The Mountain West and Southwest are the fastest-growing CRE markets in the US, driven by population migration, corporate relocations, and industrial/logistics expansion. Denver, Phoenix, Salt Lake City, and Boise are all top-10 growth markets for commercial office and industrial space. Ridgeline has first-mover regional scale across these markets.
3. **High retention, recurring revenue** — 92% client retention rate and 4.2-year average contract tenure provide revenue predictability. Property management fees (60% of revenue) are recurring in nature — contracts are typically 3-5 years with annual escalators. This revenue profile supports attractive valuation and debt capacity.
4. **Add-on acquisition strategy** — Target regional PM firms in Texas, Pacific Northwest, and California at 5-7x EBITDA. The fragmented market offers abundant targets, and Ridgeline's technology platform and institutional processes provide a repeatable integration playbook.

## Seller / Founder Details

Kevin Drummond founded Ridgeline Property Services in 2008 after 16 years at CBRE, where he rose to Regional Director for the Mountain West. He built Ridgeline from a single Denver office managing 2M sq ft to a 7-office, 18M sq ft regional platform over 17 years. The partial exit was driven by Drummond's recognition that the next phase of growth — building a multi-regional platform through acquisitions and technology investment — required institutional capital and professional operational infrastructure. Drummond retained a 20% equity rollover and continues as CEO, signaling his commitment to the value creation plan and his confidence in the growth trajectory. The rollover structure aligns his incentives with Northwood through exit.
