## Opportunity Summary

Ridgeline Property Services is a Denver-based commercial property management and building services platform managing approximately 18 million square feet of commercial office, industrial, and mixed-use properties across the Mountain West and Southwest. The company generates $92M in LTM revenue and $16.9M in adjusted EBITDA (18.4% margin) from three revenue streams: property management fees (60%), project management/tenant improvement oversight (25%), and building engineering/maintenance services (15%). The founder, Kevin Drummond, is seeking a partial exit after 17 years, retaining 20% equity and continuing as CEO. The opportunity is to acquire a well-positioned regional platform and execute a buy-and-build strategy in the fastest-growing CRE markets in the US, leveraging Ridgeline's proprietary technology, institutional client relationships, and strong retention metrics.

## Thesis Pillars

### 1. Institutional Owners Seeking Tech-Enabled Mid-Market Alternatives

The commercial property management market is dominated by three global incumbents — CBRE, JLL, and Cushman & Wakefield — that collectively manage billions of square feet. However, institutional property owners (REITs, PE real estate funds, family offices) increasingly report dissatisfaction with Big 3 service quality at the regional level: high staff turnover, inconsistent reporting, and a "one-size-fits-all" approach. These owners are actively seeking mid-market alternatives that combine institutional-quality reporting and technology with hands-on, responsive property management.

Ridgeline has built a proprietary property management software platform that provides real-time operational dashboards, automated tenant communication, work order management, and financial reporting. This technology, combined with Ridgeline's regional focus and senior-level client engagement (Drummond personally oversees top 20 client relationships), has enabled the company to win mandates from institutional owners who previously used Big 3 firms.

The mid-market commercial PM segment ($10-200M revenue) is highly fragmented, with no dominant regional platform. This creates a platform-building opportunity similar to what has been executed successfully in other fragmented services sectors.

### 2. Mountain West / Southwest — Fastest-Growing CRE Markets

The Mountain West and Southwest are experiencing the most significant commercial real estate growth in the US, driven by:

- **Population migration:** Denver, Phoenix, Salt Lake City, Boise, and Las Vegas are consistently top-10 domestic migration destinations
- **Corporate relocations:** Major employers relocating operations from coastal markets to Mountain West/Southwest for cost, tax, and talent advantages
- **Industrial/logistics expansion:** E-commerce-driven demand for warehouse and logistics space in these corridors
- **Data center development:** Significant data center investment in Phoenix, Salt Lake City, and Northern Nevada

Ridgeline has first-mover regional scale across these markets with 7 offices (6 regional + 1 HQ). Competitors in the mid-market segment are typically single-market operators with 1-3M sq ft under management. Ridgeline's 18M sq ft portfolio and multi-market presence create a durable competitive advantage.

### 3. High-Retention, Recurring Revenue Base

Ridgeline's client retention rate of 92% and average contract tenure of 4.2 years provide exceptional revenue visibility for a services business. Property management contracts are typically 3-5 year agreements with annual fee escalators of 2-3%. This recurring fee structure, combined with the high switching costs inherent in property management (tenant relationships, building system knowledge, vendor relationships), creates a sticky revenue base.

Revenue concentration is manageable: top client represents 8% of revenue, top 10 clients represent 45%. The institutional client base (REITs 35%, PE real estate funds 25%, family offices 20%) is diversified and creditworthy.

### 4. Add-on Acquisition Strategy at 5-7x EBITDA

The commercial property management market is highly fragmented at the regional level. Hundreds of firms operate with $5-30M in revenue and $1-5M in EBITDA. Many are founder-owned, single-market businesses with aging principals and no succession plan. The opportunity is to acquire 3-5 regional PM firms over the hold period at 5-7x EBITDA, integrating them onto Ridgeline's technology platform and cross-selling project management and building engineering services.

- **Identified target markets:** Texas (Dallas/Fort Worth, Austin, Houston), Pacific Northwest (Portland, Seattle), California (Sacramento, San Diego)
- **Integration playbook:** Ridgeline's proprietary PM software and centralized reporting platform provide a repeatable integration template
- **Multiple arbitrage:** Acquiring at 5-7x and integrating into a platform valued at 8.0-9.0x creates significant value

## Key Risks Discussed

1. **CRE market cyclicality / office vacancy rates** — Office vacancy rates nationally are elevated post-COVID. Mountain West/Southwest markets have outperformed but are not immune to correction. Mitigant: Ridgeline's property type diversification (office 55%, industrial 30%, mixed-use 15%) provides resilience. Industrial and logistics properties are countercyclical to office weakness. Long-term contracts with annual escalators provide a revenue floor.

2. **Key person dependency (Drummond)** — Drummond personally manages the top 20 client relationships (approximately 60% of revenue). His departure or diminished engagement would create significant client retention risk. Mitigant: 20% equity rollover aligns Drummond through exit. Marcus Webb (COO, hired at close) will conduct joint client meetings during Year 1 to build parallel relationships. Client relationship mapping and transition plan is a 100-day plan workstream.

3. **Technology platform scalability** — Ridgeline's proprietary PM software was built in-house by a small development team. The platform is effective at current scale (18M sq ft) but has not been stress-tested at 40-50M+ sq ft. Mitigant: Technology platform assessment is a 100-day plan priority. The assessment will determine whether to invest in scaling the existing platform or migrate to a commercial PM software solution.

4. **Geographic concentration** — 100% of revenue is generated in the Mountain West and Southwest. A regional economic downturn or CRE-specific market correction in this geography would disproportionately impact Ridgeline. Mitigant: Add-on acquisition strategy targets Texas, PNW, and California to diversify geographic exposure. Las Vegas office opened January 2025, expanding the footprint.

## IC Discussion Notes

The committee focused on two primary questions: the Drummond key-person risk and the technology platform scalability.

On Drummond, the committee was satisfied with the 20% rollover structure as an alignment mechanism but emphasized the importance of executing the client relationship transition during Year 1. The deal team committed to making this a formal 100-day plan workstream with measurable milestones (joint client meeting completion rate, client satisfaction surveys post-transition). The committee noted that Drummond's age (54) and continued engagement as CEO reduce the near-term risk, but a structured succession plan should be developed by Year 3.

On technology, the committee viewed the proprietary PM software as both an asset (competitive differentiator, client stickiness) and a liability (unproven at scale, single point of failure). The deal team presented a preliminary technology assessment timeline and committed to a Day 60 vendor evaluation that would determine the "build vs. buy" decision for the next-generation platform. The committee was comfortable proceeding with the investment contingent on the technology assessment being a top-3 priority in the 100-day plan.

Entry multiple of 8.0x was discussed relative to the 7-9x range for property management platforms. The committee viewed the multiple as attractive given the high retention metrics, recurring revenue profile, and the Mountain West/Southwest growth tailwind.

**Vote:** Approved unanimously.
