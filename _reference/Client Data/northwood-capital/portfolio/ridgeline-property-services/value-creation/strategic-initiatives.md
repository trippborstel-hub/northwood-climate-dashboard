## Value Creation Plan Overview

The Ridgeline value creation plan is built on four pillars: (1) technology platform investment to enable scale, (2) add-on acquisitions to build a multi-regional platform, (3) geographic expansion into adjacent high-growth CRE markets, and (4) sustainability services development as a differentiated revenue stream. A cross-cutting priority — reducing Drummond key-person dependency — underpins all four initiatives.

**Target Outcome:** Exit at 8.5-9.5x EBITDA in Year 4-5 (2029-2030) with EBITDA of $28-34M, implying enterprise value of $238-323M and gross MOIC of 2.0-2.5x+.

## Initiative 1: Technology Platform Investment

**Status:** In Progress
**RAG:** Amber
**Owner:** Robert Chen (Director of Technology), CTO (when hired)

### Objective
Determine the long-term technology strategy (build vs. buy vs. hybrid) and execute the platform investment to support 40-50M+ sq ft under management across multiple regions. Hire a CTO to lead the effort.

### Rationale
RidgelineOS is a competitive differentiator at current scale (18M sq ft) — clients rate it highly and it contributes to the company's 92% retention rate. However, the platform was built for a single-region operation and lacks the architecture to support multi-region scale, multi-tenant data isolation, or third-party system integration. The add-on acquisition strategy requires a scalable technology backbone — acquired firms need to be migrated onto a unified platform.

## Initiative 2: Add-on Acquisition Pipeline

**Status:** Early Planning
**RAG:** Green
**Owner:** Nadia Fernandez (Deal Lead), Mark Sullivan (Board Director)

### Objective
Complete 2-4 add-on acquisitions over the hold period, adding $6-12M of incremental EBITDA at 5-7x acquisition multiples. Expand geographic coverage to 3-4 additional markets.

### Rationale
The commercial property management market is highly fragmented at the regional level. Hundreds of firms operate with $5-30M in revenue and $1-5M in EBITDA. Many are founder-owned with aging principals and no succession plan — identical to the pre-acquisition Ridgeline profile. Acquisitions at 5-7x create meaningful multiple arbitrage against the platform's 8.0-8.5x valuation. Geographic expansion reduces Mountain West/Southwest concentration and accesses larger CRE markets (Texas, PNW, California).

### Completed Acquisitions

None yet — Ridgeline was acquired July 2025. Add-on pipeline being developed.

## Initiative 4: Sustainability Services Development

**Status:** Early Stage
**RAG:** Amber
**Owner:** Marcus Webb (COO), David Morales (VP Building Engineering)

### Objective
Develop a sustainability services offering that leverages Ridgeline's building engineering capabilities and 18M sq ft managed portfolio to generate $3-8M in incremental annual revenue by Year 3-4. Position Ridgeline as the only mid-market PM platform with integrated sustainability services.

### Rationale
Multiple converging trends create an actionable opportunity:
- **Client demand:** 6 institutional clients have requested sustainability services (energy benchmarking, ENERGY STAR, emissions reporting) in the past 12 months.
- **Regulatory tailwinds:** Denver and Phoenix are considering building performance standards (BPS) that would require commercial buildings to meet energy/emissions targets. 12-14M sq ft of managed portfolio would be affected.
- **Competitive whitespace:** Big 3 PM firms have launched sustainability divisions; no mid-market PM firm has — first-mover advantage is available.
- **Existing capabilities:** Building engineering team (85 staff) already monitors energy consumption for cost management. The data and operational expertise exist; the gap is methodology, tools, and commercial packaging.
- **Defensive value:** Failure to develop sustainability capabilities risks losing institutional clients who increasingly require these services from their PM providers.

### Phased Approach

| Phase | Timeline | Scope | Revenue Target |
|-------|----------|-------|----------------|
| Phase 1: Energy Benchmarking | Q2-Q4 2026 | ENERGY STAR benchmarking for managed portfolio; 2-3 pilot clients | $0.5-1.0M |
| Phase 2: Sustainability Advisory | 2027 | BPS compliance support, energy audits, green lease advisory | $2.0-3.0M |
| Phase 3: Full Platform | 2028-2029 | Integrated sustainability + PM offering; new client acquisition tool | $5.0-8.0M |
