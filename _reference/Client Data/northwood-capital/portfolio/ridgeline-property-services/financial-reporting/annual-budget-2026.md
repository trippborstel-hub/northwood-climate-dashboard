## Key Assumptions

### Revenue Assumptions
- Organic revenue growth of 8-10% driven by new mandate wins, annual fee escalators (2-3%), and Las Vegas market ramp
- No add-on acquisition revenue included in base budget (acquisitions would be additive)
- New mandates: target 10-12 new property management contracts adding 2.5-3.0M sq ft
- Client retention rate maintained at 92%+
- Las Vegas revenue: $7.0M (up from $2.8M annualized in H2 2025) as office reaches 18-staff capacity
- TI project pipeline: $25.5M budgeted based on current backlog and historical conversion rates

### Cost Assumptions
- Building engineer wages: +4% average increase (market-competitive adjustment)
- Headcount additions: 20 net new hires (8 building engineers, 5 property managers, 4 project coordinators, 3 G&A)
- Fleet costs: +8% reflecting Las Vegas vehicle additions and fuel cost inflation
- Insurance premiums: +9% renewal increase (Western US market hardening)
- Technology investment: $1.2M for platform assessment and CTO search
- Office lease costs: +5% reflecting Las Vegas expansion and Denver lease escalator

### Margin Assumptions
- Gross margin improvement of 50 bps driven by fee escalators flowing through at high incremental margin
- SG&A leverage as revenue grows faster than overhead additions
- EBITDA margin expansion of 70 bps to 19.0%, partially offset by technology investment costs

## Key Risks to Budget

1. **CRE market softening** — Budget assumes stable-to-improving commercial real estate market in Mountain West/Southwest. An increase in office vacancy rates could slow new mandate wins and pressure existing PM fees through portfolio downsizing.
2. **Las Vegas ramp timing** — Budget assumes Las Vegas reaches $7.0M annual revenue. If mandate wins are slower than projected, the market could remain a drag on overall margins.
3. **Technology investment cost** — CTO search and platform assessment could exceed the $1.5M budget if the "build" option is selected and requires immediate development investment.
4. **Building engineer recruitment** — Budget assumes 8 net new building engineer hires. Labor market for licensed building engineers and HVAC technicians remains competitive in Mountain West markets.
5. **Client concentration risk** — Loss of a top-5 client (unlikely given 92% retention) would require budget revision.
