## Summary

Patriot Staffing Solutions is performing below plan since its acquisition in August 2023. While top-line revenue has grown 9% from entry ($285.0M to $310.0M), EBITDA margin has compressed from 7.9% at entry to 7.7% current, driven by wage inflation for temporary workers (+8% YoY) outpacing bill rate increases to clients (+5%). The 20 bps of margin compression on a $310M revenue base represents approximately $0.6M of annual EBITDA shortfall versus underwrite. Management is implementing a bill rate renegotiation program across the top 50 clients in Q1-Q2 2026, and workers' compensation costs are trending above plan. The add-on acquisition pipeline has been paused until margin performance stabilizes.

The revolver has been drawn $5.0M to fund seasonal working capital needs, which is not unusual for a staffing business but adds to the caution profile given the margin headwinds. No distributions have been paid.

## Performance Summary — Below Plan Detail

Patriot's below-plan performance is concentrated in margin, not revenue. Revenue growth of 9% reflects healthy volume trends — temp workers on assignment have grown from ~2,800 to ~3,200, and customer count has increased from 85 to 92. The problem is the bill rate / pay rate spread.

**Root Cause:** Wage inflation for light industrial temporary workers has been running at approximately +8% YoY, driven by tight labor markets in manufacturing, warehousing, and logistics. Patriot's ability to pass through these wage increases to clients via bill rate adjustments has lagged — bill rates have increased only +5% over the same period. The resulting 300 bps spread compression has eroded gross margins and flowed through to EBITDA.

**Contributing Factor:** Workers' compensation costs have trended 15% above plan, driven by higher loss experience in warehouse and manufacturing assignments. The workers' comp loss ratio has increased from 4.2% to 4.8% of payroll, representing approximately $1.5M of incremental cost versus plan.

**Management Response:**
1. Bill rate renegotiation program launched Q1 2026 — targeting top 50 clients (representing 75% of revenue) for 5-8% bill rate increases
2. Workers' comp safety program enhancement — new pre-assignment safety training, enhanced site audits, modified return-to-work program
3. Client mix optimization — deprioritizing low-margin, high-risk warehouse assignments in favor of higher-margin manufacturing and logistics clients
4. Technology investment — new VMS (vendor management system) deployment to improve fill rate and reduce time-to-fill, enabling better margin management
