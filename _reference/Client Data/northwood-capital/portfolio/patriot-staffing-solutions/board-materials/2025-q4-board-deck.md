## 3. Margin Compression Root Cause Analysis

### Bill Rate / Pay Rate Spread Deterioration

| Period | Avg Bill Rate | Avg Pay Rate | Spread ($/hr) | Spread (%) |
|--------|--------------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| Entry (Aug 2023) | $22.50 | $16.20 | $6.30 | 28.0% |
| Dec 2023 | $22.80 | $16.50 | $6.30 | 27.6% |
| Jun 2024 | $23.00 | $17.00 | $6.00 | 26.1% |
| Dec 2024 | $23.20 | $17.20 | $6.00 | 25.9% |
| Jun 2025 | $23.50 | $17.40 | $6.10 | 25.9% |
| Dec 2025 | $23.55 | $17.50 | $6.05 | 25.7% |
| Jan 2026 | $23.50 | $17.55 | $5.95 | 25.3% |

**Key Finding:** Pay rates have increased 8.3% since entry while bill rates have increased only 4.4%. The cumulative spread compression of $0.35/hr on approximately 6.5M hours billed annually represents approximately $2.3M of annual gross profit erosion.

**Root Causes:**
1. **Market dynamics** — Tight labor market for light industrial workers, driven by warehousing/logistics demand and manufacturing nearshoring. Workers have multiple alternatives and are demanding higher wages.
2. **Client resistance** — Many clients, particularly in manufacturing, have multi-year MSA pricing and have been resistant to mid-contract rate increases.
3. **Competitive pressure** — Smaller local staffing competitors are willing to accept thinner spreads to win volume, limiting Patriot's pricing power on rate negotiations.
4. **Legacy pricing discipline** — The founder's relationship-based pricing approach left several large accounts at below-market rates. Management has been slow to address these legacy accounts.

### Workers' Compensation Cost Increase

| Period | Loss Ratio (% of payroll) | Annual Cost (est.) |
|--------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| Entry (Aug 2023) | 4.2% | $10.1M |
| 2024 | 4.4% | $10.8M |
| 2025 | 4.8% | $11.7M |
| 2026 Budget Target | 4.2% | $10.4M |

**Root Causes:**
1. **Higher claim frequency in warehouse assignments** — Warehouse vertical has grown from 30% to 34% of revenue mix; warehouse claims are 2x the rate of manufacturing claims.
2. **Insufficient pre-assignment screening** — New workers placed too quickly without adequate safety orientation.
3. **Return-to-work delays** — Average claim duration has increased from 18 to 24 days, inflating reserve costs.

## 6. Recruiter Retention & Fill Rate Challenges

### Recruiter Attrition Trend

| Period | Recruiters | Turnover (annualized) |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Entry (Aug 2023) | 60 | 20% |
| Dec 2023 | 62 | 18% |
| Jun 2024 | 64 | 22% |
| Dec 2024 | 66 | 24% |
| Jun 2025 | 67 | 26% |
| Dec 2025 | 65 | 28% |
| Jan 2026 | 63 | 30% (monthly annualized) |

**Root Cause:** Recruiter base compensation is 10-15% below market for the top-quartile performers. Larger staffing firms are recruiting Patriot's experienced recruiters with higher base salaries and better benefits. The problem is compounding — as recruiters leave, remaining recruiters carry heavier workloads, reducing satisfaction and further increasing turnover risk.

**Proposed Actions:**
1. Increase recruiter base salary by 10% for top-quartile performers (est. cost: $0.4M annually)
2. Implement recruiter retention bonuses at 12 and 24 months ($0.2M annually)
3. Career path development — create Senior Recruiter and Lead Recruiter titles with clear promotion criteria
4. Recruiter training and onboarding improvement — target 30-day productivity for new hires

**Request:** Board approval for revised recruiter compensation package (est. $0.6M annual incremental cost). This investment is necessary to stabilize fill rates and support volume growth. The ROI is clear: each recruiter generates approximately $200K of annual gross profit.
