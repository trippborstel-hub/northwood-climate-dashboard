# Patriot Staffing Solutions — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** August 1, 2023
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Patriot Staffing Solutions, a light industrial staffing and temp-to-perm platform, in a management buyout (MBO). The founder exited fully and the existing management team (CEO, CFO, COO) was retained with new equity incentive grants. The transaction was sourced through a structured auction process.

**Sector:** Business Services — Light Industrial Staffing / Temp-to-Perm
**Headquarters:** Midwest
**Transaction Type:** Management buyout (founder exit, management team retained)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

Consolidation play in the highly fragmented light industrial staffing market. Scale advantages in insurance procurement, workers' compensation management, and technology would drive margin improvement and competitive positioning. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Consolidation opportunity** — The light industrial staffing segment is highly fragmented, with thousands of local and regional operators. Patriot's $285M revenue base provides a foundation to acquire smaller competitors at 4-6x EBITDA, achieving significant multiple arbitrage and operational synergies.
2. **Scale advantages** — Larger staffing platforms achieve better economics on workers' compensation insurance (the single largest variable cost after payroll), general liability, and benefits procurement. Patriot's scale at acquisition was already enabling preferred pricing; further scale through add-ons would compound these advantages.
3. **Bill rate management** — Professional bill rate management and margin analytics, replacing the founder's relationship-based pricing approach, would protect and expand the bill rate / pay rate spread.
4. **Technology investment** — Implementation of a modern vendor management system (VMS) and applicant tracking system (ATS) to improve fill rates, reduce time-to-fill, and provide clients with better visibility — differentiating Patriot from smaller competitors.
5. **Geographic expansion** — Patriot operates in 6 states across the Midwest and Southeast. The add-on strategy targets expansion into Texas, the Mid-Atlantic, and additional Southeast markets.

## Seller / Founder Details

The founder built Patriot from a single-branch staffing agency in 2001 to a $285M revenue platform across 6 states over 22 years. The founder's management style was relationship-driven, with informal pricing and limited use of technology in operations. The exit was motivated by the founder's desire for full liquidity. The management team (Crawford, Nguyen, Mitchell) was retained with new equity incentives, having been in their roles for 2-4 years pre-close.

The founder did not retain any equity or board role. Transition was straightforward given that Crawford had been effectively running the business as CEO for two years prior to close.
