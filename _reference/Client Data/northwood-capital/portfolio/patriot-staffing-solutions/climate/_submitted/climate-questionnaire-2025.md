## Section 7: Physical Risk & Business Continuity

**Have you experienced any weather-related operational disruptions?**

Yes. 4 Southeast branches experienced weather-related closures totaling 12 days in 2025:

- **Jacksonville, FL** — 4 days closed (Hurricane season, September 2025). Branch closed, all temp assignments at client sites in the area suspended. Estimated revenue impact: ~$180K in deferred billings.
- **Tampa, FL** — 3 days closed (same storm system, September 2025). Similar impact, approximately $140K deferred.
- **New Orleans, LA** — 3 days closed (tropical storm, August 2025). Revenue impact: ~$120K deferred.
- **Birmingham, AL** — 2 days closed (severe thunderstorm/tornado warning, April 2025). Revenue impact: ~$65K deferred.

Total estimated revenue deferral from weather closures: ~$505K. Most was recovered within 2-4 weeks as client operations resumed.

**Flood zone status:** 2 of our Mid-South branches — Memphis, TN and Jackson, MS — are located in FEMA-designated flood zones (Zone AE). We have not experienced flooding at either location, but both are in low-lying commercial areas near waterways. Our leases at these locations do not include flood insurance — that is the landlord's responsibility, and I have not confirmed whether the landlords carry it.

**Heat exposure — workers' compensation connection:** This may not be what you are looking for in a climate questionnaire, but I think it is relevant from a risk perspective. Workers assigned to outdoor and warehouse environments face heat stress exposure. In 2025, we recorded 8 heat-related incidents across our temporary workforce, resulting in 3 recordable injuries and approximately $47,000 in workers' compensation claims. The incidents were concentrated in July and August at client sites in Atlanta, Memphis, Jacksonville, and Birmingham. We have implemented mandatory hydration breaks and shade requirements for assignments above 90°F, and we added a heat stress module to our pre-assignment safety orientation in Q3 2025.

This is a growing exposure for us. The number of days above 95°F at our Southeast and Mid-South client sites has been increasing year over year based on what our branch managers are reporting informally. We do not track this systematically, but our workers' comp claims data shows a clear seasonal pattern and the 2025 summer was the worst we have experienced.

**Water dependency:** N/A. We are an office-based staffing company. Water consumption is limited to restrooms and break rooms.

**Business continuity plan:** We have a general business continuity plan that covers IT systems, payroll continuity, and communication protocols. It does not specifically address climate events or weather-related disruptions. Storm response is handled ad hoc by individual branch managers based on local conditions. We do not have a centralized weather monitoring or pre-positioning protocol.
