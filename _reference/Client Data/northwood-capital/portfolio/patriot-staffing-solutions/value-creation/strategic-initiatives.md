# Patriot Staffing Solutions — Strategic Initiatives

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Owner:** Andrew Walsh (Deal Lead), Mark Sullivan (Board Director)

---

## Value Creation Plan Overview

The Patriot value creation plan was originally built on four pillars: (1) margin expansion through bill rate management and insurance optimization, (2) add-on acquisitions to build a multi-state platform, (3) technology investment to improve fill rates and operational efficiency, and (4) geographic expansion.

**Current Status:** The plan is behind schedule. Margin expansion has reversed — EBITDA margin has compressed from 7.9% at entry to 7.7%, against an original target of 9.0-9.5% by Year 3. Add-on acquisition activity has been paused until margin performance stabilizes. The value creation plan has been revised to prioritize margin recovery as the gating item for all other initiatives.

**Revised Target Outcome:** Exit at 8.5x EBITDA in Year 4-5 (2027-2028) with EBITDA of $32-38M, implying enterprise value of $272-323M and gross MOIC of 1.8-2.2x. This represents a downward revision from the original base case of 2.0x MOIC.

Restore EBITDA margin from 7.7% (LTM) to 8.5%+ by Q4 2026, and to 9.0%+ by exit. This is the gating initiative — all other value creation activities are contingent on margin recovery.

## Initiative 2: Add-on Acquisition Pipeline

**Status:** Paused
**RAG:** Amber
**Owner:** Andrew Walsh (Deal Lead)

### Objective
Complete 2-3 add-on acquisitions at 4-6x EBITDA to expand geographic coverage, add $5-10M of incremental EBITDA, and build a national platform that commands a premium valuation at exit.

### Current Status
The add-on pipeline was paused in Q3 2025 when it became clear that margin performance was not on plan. The rationale for pausing:
- Management bandwidth is constrained — margin recovery requires focused execution
- Below-plan EBITDA reduces acquisition financing capacity
- Acquiring at 5-6x while the platform is valued at 8.0-8.5x requires a clear margin story; the current margin trajectory undermines that story
- Integration risk is elevated when the core business has unresolved operational issues

### Pipeline Targets (Maintained Relationships)

| Target | Location | Revenue | EBITDA | Est. Multiple | Status |
|--------|----------|---------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Target 1 | Texas | $28M | $2.5M | 5-6x | Relationship maintained; not in active process |
| Target 2 | Mid-Atlantic | $18M | $1.8M | 4-5x | Relationship maintained |
| Target 3 | Southeast | $22M | $2.0M | 5-6x | Monitoring |
| Target 4 | Midwest | $35M | $3.2M | 6-7x | Monitoring; may be too large for current capacity |

### Reactivation Criteria
All of the following must be met before the pipeline is reactivated:
1. EBITDA margin sustained at 8.0%+ for two consecutive quarters
2. Bill rate renegotiation program substantially complete (Phase 1 and Phase 2)
3. Workers' comp loss ratio below 4.5%
4. No material client attrition from rate increase program
5. Recruiter headcount stabilized at 65+

**Estimated reactivation:** Q3-Q4 2026 if margin recovery plan executes as budgeted.

The downside case (no add-ons, margins stuck at 7.7%) yields a 1.3x MOIC — a break-even outcome that returns capital but does not generate meaningful returns.
