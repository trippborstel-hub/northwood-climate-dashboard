## Northwood Recommendations

1. **Monitor CEO performance through Q3 2026.** Crawford has the plan and the industry knowledge to execute margin recovery. The question is speed and decisiveness. The bill rate renegotiation results in Q1-Q2 will be the primary evaluation criterion. If margin recovery is not on track by Q3, Northwood should begin developing contingency plans for a CEO change.

2. **Approve recruiter compensation investment immediately.** Every month of delay compounds the recruiter turnover problem. The $0.6M annual cost is immaterial relative to the revenue and margin impact of fill rate deterioration.

3. **Strengthen VP, Sales & Client Services role.** Williams is leading the bill rate program effectively. Consider expanding her mandate to include full pricing authority — currently pricing decisions flow through the COO, which creates friction and slows rate adjustment execution.

4. **Begin COO succession planning.** Mitchell is valuable but irreplaceable in the current structure. Identify and develop an internal candidate (likely one of the regional directors) over the next 12-18 months.

5. **Evaluate independent board director candidates.** An independent director with staffing industry and turnaround experience would add credibility and operational insight. Suggest targeting a former CEO or COO of a mid-market staffing platform.
