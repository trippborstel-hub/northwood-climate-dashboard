## Budget Summary — Margin Recovery Plan

The 2026 budget is built around the margin recovery plan. The primary objective is to restore EBITDA margin from 7.7% (LTM) to at least 8.2% by year-end 2026 through bill rate adjustments, workers' compensation cost management, and operating efficiencies. Revenue growth is budgeted at a moderate 5% to reflect conservative volume assumptions while the team focuses on margin.

## Margin Recovery Plan — Detailed Assumptions

### Bill Rate Adjustment Program

| Phase | Timeline | Target Clients | Expected Outcome |
|-------|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Phase 1 | Q1 2026 | Top 20 clients (55% of revenue) | 5-8% bill rate increases; est. $3.5M gross profit impact |
| Phase 2 | Q2 2026 | Next 30 clients (20% of revenue) | 5-7% bill rate increases; est. $1.5M gross profit impact |
| Phase 3 | Q3 2026 | Remaining clients / new contracts | Standard rate cards at updated levels |

**Key Assumptions:**
- Client attrition from rate increases: 3-5% (modeled at 5% in budget = ~$15M revenue risk, partially offset by new client wins)
- Average bill rate increase achieved: 6% (vs. 8% pay rate inflation = partial spread recovery)
- Bill/pay spread target: $6.80/hr by Q4 2026 (vs. $6.13 current and $6.30 at entry)

### Workers' Compensation Cost Reduction

| Initiative | Timeline | Expected Savings |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Pre-assignment safety training (mandatory) | Q1 2026 | $0.5M annual (fewer first-30-day claims) |
| Client site safety audits (top 20 accounts) | Q1-Q2 2026 | $0.3M annual (hazard mitigation) |
| Modified return-to-work program | Q2 2026 | $0.4M annual (reduced claim duration) |
| Insurance carrier renegotiation | Q3 2026 (renewal) | $0.3M annual (competitive market) |
| **Total Workers' Comp Savings** | | **$1.5M annual** |

**Target:** Reduce workers' comp loss ratio from 4.8% to 4.2% of payroll by year-end 2026.

### Operating Efficiencies

| Initiative | Expected Impact |
|-----------|----------------|
| VMS platform deployment (Phase 1) | Improve fill rate from 83% to 86%; est. $2-3M incremental revenue |
| Recruiter productivity improvement | Target 45 placements/recruiter/month (vs. 38 current) |
| Branch consolidation analysis | Identify 1-2 underperforming branches for consolidation; $0.3-0.5M savings |
| SG&A containment | Hold G&A growth to 3% despite 7% revenue growth; leverage on fixed costs |

## Key Risks to Budget

1. **Client attrition from bill rate increases** — Budget assumes 5% attrition; if major clients push back or switch providers, revenue and volume impact could exceed plan. Mitigation: phased approach, value-add positioning, early engagement with procurement contacts.
2. **Wage inflation acceleration** — Budget assumes wage inflation moderates to 5-6% in 2026 (vs. 8% in 2025). If wage inflation persists at 8%+, bill rate adjustments may not fully close the spread gap.
3. **Workers' comp deterioration** — Budget assumes loss ratio improvement to 4.2%. If claim frequency does not decline, savings may not materialize and insurance renewal pricing could increase.
4. **Recruiter turnover** — If recruiter turnover remains at 28%+ and fill rates decline further, volume and revenue targets are at risk.
5. **Economic slowdown** — Temp staffing volumes are early-cycle. A manufacturing PMI decline below 48 would signal volume risk. Budget assumes stable macro conditions.

---

## Board Discussion Points

1. **Margin recovery plan is achievable but requires execution discipline.** The bill rate adjustment program is the single largest driver of budget improvement. Early progress in Q1 (top 20 clients) will be the leading indicator of full-year margin trajectory.
2. **Workers' comp is the second-order problem.** Even if bill rates recover, elevated workers' comp costs erode margin. The safety program investments need to show results by Q3.
3. **Add-on pipeline remains paused.** Budget does not include any acquisition activity. Pipeline reactivation is contingent on demonstrating margin recovery through Q2-Q3 2026.
