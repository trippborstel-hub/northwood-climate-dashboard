## Executive Summary

January 2026 reflects the seasonal trough period for staffing combined with the ongoing margin headwinds. Revenue of $23.5M was below January budget of $24.0M, driven by slower-than-expected post-holiday ramp-up in warehousing assignments. EBITDA of $1.7M (7.2% margin) was below the $1.8M budget. The bill rate renegotiation program launched in January with initial conversations with the top 20 clients — early reactions are mixed, with some clients accepting 5-6% increases and others requesting competitive bids. Workers' compensation claims declined in January (14 vs. 18 monthly average in Q4), a positive early signal from the enhanced safety program.

**Month RAG: Amber/Red** — below plan on both revenue and margin; bill rate program in early stages.

## Key Items for Northwood Attention

1. **Bill rate renegotiation — mixed early results.** 5 of 20 target clients have accepted rate increases averaging 5.3%. Two top-5 clients requesting competitive bids represent a risk of ~$12M revenue if lost. Management recommends holding firm on rate increases for one client and offering a tiered structure for the other.
2. **Recruiter headcount decline.** Net loss of 2 recruiters in January. Currently at 63, down from 65 at year-end 2025 and 60 at acquisition. Compensation benchmarking in progress — expect to propose revised comp structure at March board meeting. This is a structural issue: if Patriot cannot retain recruiters, fill rates and volume growth will remain constrained.
3. **Workers' comp improvement — encouraging but early.** January claims declined and loss ratio improved. Need 2-3 months to confirm trend. Safety program investments are showing early returns.
4. **Revolver remains drawn at $5.0M.** Management expects to repay from Q2-Q3 operating cash flow as seasonal volumes recover. No incremental draws anticipated.
5. **Warehousing volume softness.** Two large clients delayed ramp-up. Expected to normalize in February. If delayed further, could indicate broader warehousing demand softening — will monitor through Q1.

---

## Outlook

February is expected to show modest improvement as post-holiday volumes recover and initial bill rate increases take effect (3 clients effective February 1). Revenue forecast of $24.5M for February. The critical inflection point is Q2, when the full bill rate renegotiation program should be substantially complete and seasonal volume peak begins. Management is targeting 8.0%+ EBITDA margin by Q2 to demonstrate recovery trajectory ahead of the March board meeting.
