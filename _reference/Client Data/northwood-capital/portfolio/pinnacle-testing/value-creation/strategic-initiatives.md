**Commentary:** The technician pipeline is the most critical long-term constraint on Pinnacle's growth. The NDT industry faces a structural shortage of certified inspectors — Level III certification requires 4,000+ hours of field experience, and the average Level III inspector age is 54 with significant retirement risk over the next decade. Pinnacle's ability to recruit, train, and retain inspectors is the fundamental bottleneck for both organic growth and add-on integration capacity. The pipeline program design is complete and the organizational investments (Director of Training hire, vocational school partnerships) are on track. However, this is inherently a 12-24 month initiative — a Level I trainee requires 12-18 months to reach Level II productivity. The 2026 budget targets net +25 inspectors, which would represent a step change from the +15 achieved in the first 15 months.

**Commentary:** Technology is currently the weakest link in Pinnacle's operations and the area with the most unrealized value creation potential. The company operates on a patchwork of QuickBooks (accounting), Excel (project tracking), and a legacy Access database (scheduling) with no integration between systems. This makes real-time visibility into project profitability, inspector utilization, and customer billing status essentially impossible. The ERP migration is the foundational investment: once live, it enables the field service management system, digital reporting, client portal, and analytics dashboard to be built on top of it. The ERP implementation is budgeted at $300-500K with $150K/year ongoing licensing. Total technology investment over the hold period is expected to be $1.5-2.0M.
