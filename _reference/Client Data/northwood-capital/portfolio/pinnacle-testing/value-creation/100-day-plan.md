## Plan Overview

The 100-day plan for Pinnacle establishes the foundation for transforming a regional, founder-led NDT inspection firm into a scalable national platform. The plan focuses on six workstreams: ERP assessment, safety program audit, technician pipeline development, add-on target evaluation, organizational structure finalization, and growth plan development. Given the recent close (October 2024), the plan is designed to assess the current state, stabilize operations through the founder transition, and lay the groundwork for the platform build strategy.

## Workstream 1: ERP Assessment (Day 15)

**Owner:** Karen Xu, CFO
**Target:** Assess current ERP/project management systems and recommend path forward
**Status:** COMPLETE

| Milestone | Target | Actual | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Current systems inventory | Day 5 | Day 4 | Complete |
| Stakeholder interviews (10 users) | Day 10 | Day 9 | Complete |
| Gap analysis and recommendation | Day 15 | Day 14 | Complete |

**Findings:**
- Pinnacle currently uses a combination of QuickBooks (accounting), Excel (project tracking), and a custom Access database (inspector scheduling) — none of which are integrated.
- The lack of a unified system limits real-time visibility into project profitability, inspector utilization, and customer billing status.
- Financial reporting requires manual consolidation, introducing error risk and adding 3-5 days to month-end close.

**Recommendation:**
Migrate to a unified construction/field services ERP platform (Procore or Viewpoint). RFP planned for Q2 2026. Implementation expected H2 2026. Estimated cost: $300-500K implementation, $150K/year SaaS licensing.

**Benefits Expected:**
- Real-time project profitability tracking
- Integrated inspector scheduling and utilization monitoring
- Automated customer billing and collections
- Month-end close reduced from 12 days to 5 days
- Foundation for multi-location management as add-ons are integrated

**Workforce Assessment Key Findings:**
- Average inspector age: 48 (Level III avg: 54). Retirement risk within 5 years: 15% of certified workforce.
- Level III inspectors are the most critical and hardest to replace (4,000+ hours of experience required).
- Compensation is competitive for the Gulf Coast market but below national platform competitors.
- Turnover at 14% annualized is manageable but needs to trend lower (target: 10%).

**Pipeline Program Design:**

| Pipeline Source | Annual Target | Time to Productive | Investment |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| Vocational school partnerships | 8-10 Level I hires | 3 months to field-ready | $5K per candidate (tuition subsidy) |
| Direct hire (experienced) | 5-8 Level II/III hires | Immediate | Market comp + signing bonus |
| Internal promotion (Level I to II) | 6-8 promotions | 12-18 months | Training + exam fees ($3K/person) |
| Internal promotion (Level II to III) | 2-3 promotions | 24-36 months | Mentorship + exam fees ($5K/person) |
| Military transition program | 3-5 hires | 2-3 months | Veterans pipeline program |

**Target:** Net 20+ inspector additions per year (to support organic growth + add-on integration capacity).

## Key Lessons Learned (Early)

1. **The founder transition is the most delicate element.** Hal Pinnacle's 32-year customer relationships cannot be transferred in 90 days. The 12-month consulting arrangement is proving valuable — two key customers specifically asked for Hal's continued involvement during the transition. Paul Stevens' long tenure and field credibility have helped, but the CEO/President pairing needs time to establish its own customer trust.
2. **ERP is worse than expected.** The Excel/Access/QuickBooks patchwork creates real blind spots in project profitability and inspector utilization. The ERP migration (planned H2 2026) is a prerequisite for operating as a multi-location platform.
3. **Safety is a competitive differentiator.** Multiple customer RFPs have weighted safety record at 20-30% of evaluation criteria. The TRIR improvement from 0.95 to 0.82 is already contributing to higher win rates on competitive bids.
4. **The add-on pipeline is real.** At 4-6x EBITDA for regional NDT providers, the acquisition math is highly compelling when combined with Pinnacle's platform infrastructure. The challenge will be integration bandwidth — each add-on requires significant management attention for 6-12 months.
