## Quarterly Revenue Budget

| Quarter | Revenue | EBITDA | EBITDA Margin | Commentary |
|---------|---------|--------|---------------|------------|
| Q1 2026 | $27.0M | $4.0M | 14.8% | Seasonal low; maintenance season, limited turnaround work |
| Q2 2026 | $31.0M | $5.8M | 18.7% | Ramp in pipeline, infrastructure; new customer onboarding |
| Q3 2026 | $33.5M | $6.7M | 20.0% | Pre-turnaround ramp; peak utilization; pricing takes hold |
| Q4 2026 | $33.5M | $6.5M | 19.4% | Turnaround season; strong but partially offset by new hire ramp costs |
| **Full Year** | **$125.0M** | **$23.0M** | **18.4%** | |

**Seasonality Notes:** Pinnacle's revenue follows the industrial inspection cycle. Q1 is the seasonal trough as refineries and pipelines are in planned maintenance mode with limited inspection activity, and infrastructure work pauses in winter months. Q2 ramps as spring project schedules begin. Q3-Q4 are the strongest quarters, driven by refinery turnaround season (fall) and year-end infrastructure inspection demand. The 2026 budget assumes a similar seasonal pattern to 2025, with stronger absolute levels reflecting capacity growth and pricing improvements.

## Key Budget Assumptions

### Revenue
- Organic revenue growth of 9.6%, driven by capacity expansion (+25 inspectors), pricing improvements (~3%), and new customer wins (5-8 targeted)
- No add-on acquisition revenue included in budget — acquisition pipeline active but timing uncertain
- Service line mix shifts toward higher-margin advanced NDT and pipeline integrity
- Customer attrition assumption: 7% (below historical 9%, reflecting improved retention)

### Margin
- EBITDA margin expansion of 100 bps (17.4% to 18.4%), driven by:
  - Elimination of integration costs ($1.0M favorable)
  - Procurement consolidation ($0.4M savings)
  - Mix shift toward advanced NDT and pipeline integrity
  - Improved utilization (73% to 77%)
- Partially offset by capability investments (VP BD, Director Training, ERP prep, sales)

### Cash Flow
- Free cash flow of $14.5M (63% FCF conversion on EBITDA)
- Capex of $4.5M (3.6% of revenue)
- Working capital: DSO improvement from 48 to 45 days targeted through billing process improvements

## Budget Note — Add-On Acquisition Considerations

The 2026 budget is prepared on an organic-only basis. Key considerations regarding the add-on pipeline:

1. **Pipeline activity:** Four targets are in various stages of evaluation. If an acquisition closes in 2026, it would represent incremental revenue and EBITDA above the organic budget. Targets range from $12-22M in revenue and $2.0-4.0M in EBITDA.
2. **Acquisition costs:** Estimated transaction costs of $0.5-1.0M per acquisition (legal, accounting, diligence) are not budgeted and would be classified as non-recurring adjustments.
3. **Integration resources:** Each acquisition will require 6-12 months of management attention and approximately $0.3-0.5M in integration costs. The organizational investments in the 2026 budget (VP BD, Director Training, regional VP structure) are designed in part to build the management bandwidth necessary for add-on integration.
4. **Financing:** Add-on acquisitions at 4-6x EBITDA for targets in the $10-30M revenue range are expected to be funded through a combination of revolver draws, incremental term loan, and cash on hand. No equity co-invest required for targets in this size range.
