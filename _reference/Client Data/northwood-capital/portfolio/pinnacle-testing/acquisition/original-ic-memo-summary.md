**IC Date:** September 6, 2024

**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.
**Deal Lead:** Andrew Walsh

## Investment Recommendation

Acquire 80% of Pinnacle Testing & Inspection for $155.0M enterprise value (8.0x LTM Adj. EBITDA) with $93.0M total equity ($74.4M fund equity, $18.6M management rollover). The transaction represents an opportunity to build a national non-destructive testing (NDT) and industrial inspection platform in a market with strong structural demand drivers and a meaningful technician-supply barrier to entry. The base case targets a 2.5x gross MOIC and ~20% gross IRR over a 5-year hold.

---

## Market Overview

Non-destructive testing (NDT) is a $12B+ U.S. market growing at 6-8% annually. NDT encompasses inspection, testing, and evaluation of materials, components, and structures without causing damage. End markets include oil & gas (refineries, pipelines), infrastructure (bridges, highways), power generation, aerospace, and general manufacturing.

**Key demand drivers:**

- **Aging infrastructure** — U.S. infrastructure receives a C- grade from ASCE. 46,000+ structurally deficient bridges. Average pipeline age exceeding 50 years in many segments.
- **Federal infrastructure spending** — IIJA ($1.2T, signed 2021) allocates $550B+ in new spending for roads, bridges, water systems, and broadband. Pipeline Safety Improvement Act increasing inspection mandates.
- **Regulatory tightening** — OSHA, DOT, EPA, and state regulators increasing inspection frequency requirements. PHMSA pipeline inspection mandates expanding.
- **Safety liability** — Asset owners face increasing litigation risk from uninspected or under-inspected infrastructure. Insurance carriers requiring documentation of inspection programs.

**Supply constraint:**

- Estimated 5,000+ Level III NDE inspector shortage nationally
- Level III certification requires: 4,000+ hours of field experience, passing three certification exams, continuous education requirements
- Average Level III inspector age is 52; retirement wave expected over next decade
- New entrant inspector requires 3-5 years to reach Level III certification

## Value Creation Plan

### Pillar 1: Geographic Expansion via Add-Ons (50% of value creation)
- Acquire 4-6 regional NDT providers at 4-6x EBITDA over 5 years
- Priority geographies: Midwest (OH, PA, IN), Northeast (NJ, NY, CT), West Coast (CA, WA)
- Target profile: $10-30M revenue, 30+ inspectors, established customer base
- Identified 25+ potential targets; 8 in active monitoring

### Pillar 2: Recurring Revenue Shift (20% of value creation)
- Transition from project-based work (65%) to recurring maintenance contracts (target 50%)
- Implement long-term inspection agreements with asset owners
- Digital inspection records and predictive analytics as retention tools
- Target 50% recurring revenue by Year 3 (from 35% at entry)

### Pillar 3: Margin Improvement (15% of value creation)
- Centralize equipment procurement and calibration services
- Implement digital reporting and workflow management
- Improve technician utilization from 72% to 82%
- Target EBITDA margin expansion from 18% to 20%+ by Year 3

### Pillar 4: Technician Pipeline (15% of value creation)
- Establish Pinnacle Training Academy for NDE certification
- Partner with 5+ vocational schools and community colleges
- Internal Level I --> II --> III promotion pipeline
- Target: net 20+ inspector additions per year

## IC Discussion Notes

- Committee unanimously approved. Industrial services thesis aligns with Northwood's industrial technology focus and Operating Partner Okafor's direct experience.
- Committee emphasized the importance of moving quickly on the first add-on acquisition to demonstrate platform credibility and attract additional targets.
- Committee noted the 8.0x entry multiple provides a margin of safety — at this price, meaningful value creation can come from organic growth alone even if the add-on strategy takes longer than planned.
- James Harrison to take the board seat given his industrial sector focus and prior experience with Sterling Precision Manufacturing (similar platform build).
- Committee discussed the founder exit risk at length. Paul Stevens (COO/President) holds the customer relationships and operational knowledge. Frank Morrison (CEO) brings the strategic and M&A capability. The pairing is designed to cover both bases. The 12-month founder consulting arrangement provides additional transition support.
