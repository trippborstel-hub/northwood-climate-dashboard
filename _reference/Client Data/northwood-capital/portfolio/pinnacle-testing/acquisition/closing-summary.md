# Pinnacle Testing & Inspection — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** October 1, 2024
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Pinnacle Testing & Inspection, a non-destructive testing (NDT) and industrial inspection services platform, in a management buyout with a full founder exit. The founder (age 64) retired upon close, and the COO (Paul Stevens) was promoted to President. A new CEO (Frank Morrison) was recruited externally to lead the platform growth strategy. The transaction was sourced through a structured auction process managed by Baird.

**Sector:** Industrial Technology — Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) / Industrial Inspection Services
**Headquarters:** Gulf Coast / Southeast
**Transaction Type:** Management buyout (founder exit, COO promoted to President)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

Critical infrastructure inspection demand is growing structurally as the nation's bridges, pipelines, refineries, and industrial facilities age. The technician shortage creates a meaningful barrier to entry. Pinnacle is positioned as a platform to build a national NDT services provider through geographic add-on acquisitions. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Infrastructure demand tailwinds** — Aging U.S. infrastructure (avg. bridge age 44 years, 42% of bridges over 50 years old). Federal infrastructure spending (IIJA) adding $550B+ over 5 years. Refinery and pipeline operators increasing inspection budgets in response to regulatory pressure.
2. **Technician shortage as barrier** — Estimated national shortage of 5,000+ certified NDE inspectors. Level III certification requires 4,000+ hours of field experience. New entrants cannot easily replicate Pinnacle's certified workforce.
3. **Geographic consolidation** — Fragmented market with hundreds of regional NDT providers. Identified 25+ acquisition targets at 4-6x EBITDA. Add-on strategy to build geographic coverage across Gulf Coast, Midwest, and Northeast.
4. **Recurring revenue shift** — Transition from project-based inspection work to recurring maintenance inspection contracts. Target 50% recurring revenue within 3 years (currently 35%).
5. **Margin improvement** — Centralize procurement (equipment, calibration), implement digital reporting systems, and improve technician utilization from 72% to 82%.

## Seller / Founder Details

The founder (Harold "Hal" Pinnacle) built the company from a two-person inspection shop in 1992 to a $108M revenue regional platform over 32 years. Exit was motivated by retirement (age 64) with no family succession plan. The COO (Paul Stevens) was identified as the logical operational successor, having been with the company for 18 years. An external CEO (Frank Morrison) was recruited by Northwood to bring platform-building and M&A experience that Stevens does not have.

The founder agreed to a 12-month consulting arrangement ($200K) to support customer relationship transitions and provide institutional knowledge transfer.
