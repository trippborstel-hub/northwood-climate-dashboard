## Section 3: Refrigerants & HVAC

All 16 facilities use standard commercial HVAC systems with R-410A refrigerant. Meridian does not own HVAC equipment at most locations — building landlords maintain the systems. For the two facilities where we hold the lease directly (Chicago HQ and Jacksonville DC), HVAC maintenance is contracted to the building management company. We are not aware of any refrigerant recharges or leakage events in 2025.

## Section 5: Emissions Tracking & Targets

**Do you currently track greenhouse gas emissions?** No. We track utility costs through our accounting system but have not calculated emissions.

**Do you have emissions reduction targets?** No.

**Do you report to any sustainability frameworks?** No.

**Is there a designated sustainability or ESG role?** No. Facility cost management is part of the finance team's responsibilities.

**Is there board-level oversight of climate or ESG issues?** Climate has not been a board agenda item. The topic was raised by David Okafor in Q4 2025 in connection with this data request. Our buyer diligence process may include ESG assessment, so we are responsive to providing baseline data for that purpose.

## Section 6: Customer & Supply Chain Requirements

**Have any customers requested emissions or sustainability data?**

Not directly. Two RFP processes in 2025 included sustainability questionnaire sections:

1. A financial services client's vendor management office included a sustainability section in their annual vendor review. We responded with our basic environmental policy statement.
2. A healthcare customer's procurement team asked whether we had a carbon footprint. We indicated we did not but that our operations are office-based with a low emissions profile.

Neither situation resulted in a contract issue.

**Do your suppliers provide sustainability data?** No. Our procurement is primarily technology, software licenses, and facility services. We have not requested sustainability data from vendors.

## Section 7: Physical Risk & Business Continuity

**Have you experienced any weather-related operational disruptions?** No material disruptions. A winter storm in February 2025 caused the Minneapolis office to close for one day. Our BPO model allows work to be rerouted to other delivery centers, so client impact was negligible.

**Flood zone status:** Not assessed. All facilities are in commercial office buildings and we are not aware of flood exposure.

**Water dependency:** Minimal. Standard office water usage only.

---

## Section 8: Energy Efficiency & Improvement Opportunities

**Have you implemented any energy efficiency measures?**

No dedicated energy efficiency initiatives. Building landlords manage HVAC and lighting systems at most of our locations. When we have renewed leases, we have generally moved into newer or recently renovated spaces with more efficient building systems, but this has not been driven by an energy strategy.

**What efficiency improvements have been identified but not implemented?**

None formally evaluated. Energy is not a material cost category for Meridian relative to labor, which represents approximately 65% of operating expenses.

## Section 9: Workforce & Community

**Workforce overview:** 580 full-time employees across 16 locations. Our workforce is primarily operations staff (delivery center agents, back-office processors) and corporate functions (finance, HR, IT, sales).

**Community engagement:** Meridian participates in United Way campaigns at several office locations and sponsors local job fairs.

**Workforce demographics:** Average employee tenure: 3.8 years. Voluntary turnover: ~18% (consistent with BPO industry norms).

Meridian's environmental footprint is straightforward — we are an office-based services business.
