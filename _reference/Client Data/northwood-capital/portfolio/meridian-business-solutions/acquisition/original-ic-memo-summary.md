## Investment Thesis

Acquire a well-positioned mid-market BPO and managed staffing platform at 9.0x EBITDA with a clear path to value creation through add-on acquisitions, organic revenue expansion, margin improvement, and deleveraging. The fragmented BPO/staffing sector offers significant consolidation opportunity, and Meridian's established relationships and management team provide a strong foundation for platform growth.

## Value Creation Pillars — Original Plan vs. Actual

### 1. Add-on Acquisitions

**Plan:** Complete 2-3 add-on acquisitions at 5-7x EBITDA within years 1-3. Target BPO niche players and regional staffing firms to expand geographic footprint and service capabilities.

**Actual:** Two add-ons completed.
- **Workforce Plus** (Q2 2019) — Expanded managed staffing capabilities in healthcare vertical. Acquired at 6.5x EBITDA.
- **TalentBridge** (Q4 2020) — Added RPO (recruitment process outsourcing) capabilities and 45 enterprise clients. Acquired at 6.0x EBITDA.

Both integrations completed within plan. TalentBridge delivered revenue synergies ahead of schedule through cross-sell into Meridian's existing client base.

### 2. Organic Revenue Growth

**Plan:** 6-8% annual organic growth through cross-selling managed services to staffing clients and entering healthcare and technology verticals.

**Actual:** Organic revenue CAGR of approximately 7% (excluding add-on contribution), at the midpoint of plan. Healthcare vertical has become a meaningful contributor. Technology vertical penetration ahead of plan, driven by demand for tech staffing and BPO services.

### 3. Margin Improvement

**Plan:** Expand EBITDA margin from 16.7% to ~16.4% (margin dilution expected from lower-margin add-ons, offset by cost savings).

**Actual:** EBITDA margin expanded to 17.0%, exceeding plan. Shared services consolidation, vendor procurement at scale, and back-office automation drove savings that more than offset mix effects from add-ons. Implemented centralized ERP in 2021, reducing G&A by approximately 120 bps.

### 4. Deleveraging

**Plan:** Reduce leverage from 3.6x to ~1.3x over hold period through mandatory amortization and excess cash flow sweeps.

**Actual:** Leverage reduced to 1.1x, exceeding plan. Paid down $36.0M of debt ($78.0M to $42.0M). Strong FCF conversion (~90% of EBITDA) enabled faster paydown plus $20.0M dividend recap in Q2 2022.
