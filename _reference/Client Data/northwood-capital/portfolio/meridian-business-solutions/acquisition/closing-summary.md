# Meridian Business Solutions — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** March 15, 2018
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund I, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Meridian Business Solutions, a business process outsourcing and managed staffing platform, in a management buyout (MBO) with a partial founder exit. The founder retained a 20% equity rollover stake alongside Northwood's 80% ownership. The transaction was sourced through a structured auction process.

**Sector:** Business Services — Business Process Outsourcing / Managed Staffing
**Headquarters:** Midwest
**Transaction Type:** Management buyout (founder partial exit, 20% rollover)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

Revenue-driven growth through add-on acquisitions and organic expansion into mid-market. Significant deleveraging expected from strong free cash flow generation. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Add-on acquisitions** — Fragmented BPO/staffing market with numerous sub-$50M bolt-on targets. Identified 15+ prospects at 5-7x EBITDA.
2. **Organic revenue expansion** — Cross-sell managed services to existing staffing clients. Expand into healthcare and technology verticals.
3. **Margin improvement** — Centralize back-office, implement shared services, negotiate vendor procurement at platform scale.
4. **Deleveraging** — Strong FCF conversion (85%+ of EBITDA) enables rapid paydown of 3.6x entry leverage.

## Seller / Founder Details

The founder built Meridian from a single-office staffing agency in 2003 to a $130M revenue platform over 15 years. Partial exit was motivated by estate planning and desire to de-risk while maintaining upside through 20% rollover. The founder transitioned off the board within 6 months of close, with David Harrington (previously President/COO) stepping into the CEO role.
