**Confidential — Northwood Capital Internal**

## Exit Recommendation

Northwood Capital recommends pursuing a full exit of Meridian Business Solutions in H2 2026 via a structured sale process. Meridian is the strongest performer in Fund I, and the business is at an optimal point for exit — scaled platform, diversified revenue, strong management team, low leverage, and favorable market conditions for BPO/staffing assets.

## Key Value Creation Achievements to Highlight

### For All Buyers

1. **Revenue growth:** 73% increase from $130.0M to $225.0M through organic growth and two disciplined add-ons
2. **EBITDA growth:** 76% increase from $21.7M to $38.3M, demonstrating operating leverage
3. **Margin expansion:** 30 bps net improvement (180 bps on same-store basis) through shared services, procurement, and technology
4. **Customer diversification:** Top customer reduced from 15% to 11%; added healthcare (22%) and technology (18%) verticals
5. **Management team:** Proven team with CEO, CFO, and COO all in place for 7+ years
6. **Technology platform:** Modern ERP, client portal, analytics capabilities
7. **Deleveraging:** Reduced leverage from 3.6x to 1.1x
8. **Demonstrated add-on playbook:** Two completed acquisitions at 5.9-6.6x, both fully integrated

### For Financial Sponsor Buyers (Additional)

9. **Identified add-on pipeline:** 3 monitored targets at 5-7x providing immediate acquisition opportunities
10. **Fragmented market:** BPO/staffing sector remains highly fragmented with significant additional consolidation runway
11. **Cross-sell opportunity:** RPO attach rate of 18% leaves significant whitespace in existing client base

### For Strategic Buyers (Additional)

12. **Mid-market positioning:** Strong relationships with 145 clients in the underserved mid-market segment
13. **Healthcare and technology vertical strength:** Two of the fastest-growing verticals in BPO
14. **Scalable operations model:** Regional structure supports geographic expansion

## Process Considerations

### Sell-Side Advisor Selection

Three investment banks have received the RFP, selected based on:
- BPO/staffing sector expertise and recent transaction experience
- Depth of buyer relationships (strategic + sponsor)
- Quality of marketing materials and execution discipline
- Valuation perspective and process recommendations

Advisor presentations in January-February 2026. Selection expected by end of Q1 2026.

### Management Incentive Plan (MIP)

Management incentive plan being designed with the following parameters:
- Total pool: 3-5% of equity value at exit
- Allocation: CEO (30%), CFO (25%), COO (20%), other executives (25%)
- Vesting: Contingent on continued employment through close + 6-month transition
- Objective: Align management fully with exit outcome and ensure retention through process

### Key Risks to Exit Execution

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Economic cycle / recession | BPO contracts are multi-year with high switching costs; staffing is more cyclical but diversified across verticals |
| Management distraction during process | Additional support hires in Q1 2026; process managed by Northwood with advisor support |
| Buyer financing environment | Low leverage (1.1x) means minimal refinancing risk; business supports 4-5x new leverage |
| Customer consent / change of control | Review all material contracts for CoC provisions; none expected to be problematic |
| Competitive process dynamics | Strong buyer universe with both strategic and sponsor interest expected |
