## Key Budget Assumptions

### Revenue
- Organic revenue growth of 9.2%, driven by new client wins (15-18 targeted), cross-sell, and contract expansion
- No add-on acquisition revenue included in budget — pipeline activity may pause during exit process
- Pricing increases of 3-4% across existing contracts (reflecting labor cost pass-through)
- Customer attrition assumption: 7% (below historical 9%)

### Cost of Services
- Wage inflation of 4-5% for direct labor (staffing and BPO associates)
- Gross margin expansion of 30 bps through mix shift toward higher-margin BPO and RPO
- Benefits cost increase of 6% (healthcare premium inflation)

### SG&A
- Headcount additions: 15-20 internal staff (sales, operations, technology)
- Technology investment: $2.5M (ERP enhancements, client portal, analytics platform)
- Exit preparation costs: $1.5M (sell-side advisor fees, vendor due diligence preparation — classified as non-recurring adjustments to Adj. EBITDA)

### Capital Expenditures
- Maintenance capex: $2.5M
- Growth capex: $2.5M (technology platform, office expansion)

### Debt Service
- Mandatory amortization: $4.0M
- Expected year-end debt balance: $38.0M
- Year-end leverage: 0.9x

## Budget Note — Exit Process Considerations

The 2026 budget has been prepared with the assumption that the exit process will launch in H2 2026. Key considerations:

1. **Add-on activity:** No acquisitions are budgeted for 2026. Pipeline activity may be paused during the exit process to minimize complexity in the sell-side narrative.
2. **Exit costs:** Approximately $1.5M of sell-side advisory and vendor due diligence preparation costs are expected, classified as non-recurring adjustments.
3. **Management retention:** Exit-related management incentive payments (MIPs) are not included in the operating budget and will be funded from transaction proceeds.
4. **Operational continuity:** The budget assumes no disruption to customer-facing operations during the exit process. Management bandwidth allocation for the process has been factored into SG&A through additional support hires.
