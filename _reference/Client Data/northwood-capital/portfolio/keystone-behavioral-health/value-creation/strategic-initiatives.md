## Initiative 1: Therapist Recruitment & Retention

**Priority:** Critical — this is the binding constraint on all other growth initiatives

### Objective

Build a sustainable pipeline of BCBA therapists to support geographic expansion and patient capacity growth. Improve therapist retention from 78% to 85%+ to reduce recruitment burden and maintain clinical continuity.

### Current State

| Metric | At Acquisition | Current | Target (2027) |
|--------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| BCBAs | 70 | 85 | 120 |
| RBTs | 150 | 180 | 250 |
| BCBA Retention | 72% | 78% | 85% |
| RBT Retention | 65% | 70% | 78% |
| Avg. Compensation (BCBA) | $75K | $82K | $90K |
| Time-to-Fill (BCBA) | 85 days | 68 days | 45 days |

### Workstreams

**1.1 Compensation Competitiveness**
- Benchmarking study underway (delivery February 2026)
- Proposed: $5K base increase + $7.5K signing bonus for experienced BCBAs
- Estimated annual cost: $600K
- Expected impact: 3-5 additional hires, 200+ bps retention improvement

**1.2 University Partnership Program**
- 3 partnerships signed (University of North Carolina, Virginia Commonwealth University, George Mason University)
- 4 additional partnerships in discussion
- Scholarship program: $5K per candidate in exchange for 2-year employment commitment
- Pipeline: 8 candidates graduating May/June 2026

**1.3 RBT-to-BCBA Internal Pipeline**
- 7 current RBTs enrolled in BCBA certification programs
- Keystone subsidizes 75% of tuition costs ($8-12K per candidate)
- Expected 5 internal promotions in 2026
- Goal: 10+ internal BCBA promotions annually by 2027

**1.4 Retention Programs**
- Clinical career ladder: RBT --> Lead RBT --> BCBA --> Senior BCBA --> Clinical Director --> Regional Clinical Director
- Mentorship program: Each new BCBA paired with senior BCBA for 6 months
- Continuing education budget: $2,500/year per BCBA
- Flexible scheduling: Hybrid clinic/telehealth models where clinically appropriate

## Initiative 2: Clinic Expansion (De Novo + Add-On)

### Objective

Expand from 20 clinics to 30-34 clinics by end of 2028 through a combination of de novo openings (60%) and add-on acquisitions (40%). Target 6 new states over 3 years.

### De Novo Pipeline

| Year | Planned Openings | Markets | Capex |
|------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| 2026 | 2-3 | Charlotte NC, Richmond VA, Raleigh NC (contingent) | $3.3M |
| 2027 | 3-4 | Atlanta GA, Nashville TN, Columbus OH, Tampa FL (contingent) | $4.0M |
| 2028 | 3-4 | Baltimore MD, Austin TX, Phoenix AZ, Jacksonville FL (contingent) | $4.5M |

### Add-On Acquisition Pipeline

| Target Profile | Criteria |
|---------------|----------|
| Revenue | $5-20M |
| EBITDA | $1-4M |
| Multiple Range | 5-7x EBITDA |
| Geography | New state entry or adjacent market fill-in |
| Quality | Established clinical reputation, licensed BCBAs on staff |
| Pipeline | 20+ identified targets; 5 in active conversation |

### Site Selection Model

Markets scored on 5 factors (weighted):
1. **Autism prevalence / demand gap** (30%) — CDC data, state wait list data, referral volume
2. **BCBA supply** (25%) — Certified BCBAs per capita, university programs in market
3. **Reimbursement rates** (20%) — Commercial and Medicaid rates by state
4. **Competition** (15%) — Number and quality of existing ABA providers
5. **Regulatory environment** (10%) — Licensing requirements, insurance mandates

## Initiative 4: Billing & Revenue Cycle Improvement

### Objective

Reduce revenue leakage through claims denials, improve collections speed, and achieve best-in-class billing operations across the ABA industry.

### Results to Date

| Metric | Acquisition | Current | Target | Gap |
|--------|------------|---------|--------|-----|
| Claims Denial Rate | 12.0% | 9.5% | 5.0% | 4.5 pts |
| DSO | 55 days | 48 days | 40 days | 8 days |
| Clean Claims Rate | 82% | 88% | 95% | 7 pts |
| Collections Rate | 94% | 96.5% | 98% | 1.5 pts |

### Phase 2 Roadmap (Q1-Q3 2026)

| Initiative | Timeline | Expected Impact |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Automated prior authorization tracking | Q1 2026 | Reduce authorization lapses by 50% |
| Real-time insurance eligibility verification | Q2 2026 | Reduce denials from eligibility issues by 60% |
| Predictive denial analytics | Q2 2026 | Identify high-risk claims before submission |
| Patient responsibility collection improvement | Q3 2026 | Improve patient co-pay collection from 82% to 92% |

### Financial Impact

Closing the gap from current to target across all billing metrics represents approximately $1.5M in annualized EBITDA improvement:
- Denial rate reduction (9.5% to 5.0%): +$0.7M
- DSO improvement (48 to 40 days): +$0.3M (working capital / interest savings)
- Collections rate improvement (96.5% to 98%): +$0.5M

## Initiative 5: Clinical Quality Standardization

### Objective

Establish standardized clinical protocols, training curricula, and outcome measurement across all clinics. Clinical quality is both a differentiator for payor negotiations and a foundation for consistent patient outcomes at scale.

### Workstreams

**5.1 Treatment Protocol Standardization**
- Developing standardized treatment protocols for the 10 most common behavioral targets
- Progress: 70% complete (7 of 10 protocols finalized)
- Remaining: Social skills, self-care, and community integration protocols
- Expected completion: Q2 2026

**5.2 Clinical Outcome Measurement**
- Implementing standardized outcome tracking across all patients
- Metrics: Skill acquisition rate, behavior reduction rate, caregiver satisfaction, treatment plan goal completion
- Baseline data collection underway; first cross-clinic benchmarking report expected Q2 2026

**5.3 Training Curriculum**
- Standardized onboarding curriculum for new RBTs: reduced ramp from 12 weeks to 8 weeks
- BCBA supervision protocol standardized across all clinics
- Quarterly clinical continuing education program launched (4 sessions per year)

**5.4 Quality Assurance Program**
- Monthly clinical chart reviews (10% sample per clinic)
- Quarterly clinical peer review sessions
- Annual patient satisfaction survey (first survey Q3 2026)

## Initiative Interdependencies

```
Therapist Recruitment (1)
  └── enables --> Clinic Expansion (2) [can't open clinics without staff]
  └── enables --> Authorization Utilization [more therapists = more hours delivered]

Billing Improvement (4)
  └── enables --> Payor Negotiations (3) [clean data required for rate discussions]
  └── enables --> Financial visibility for expansion decisions

Clinical Quality (5)
  └── supports --> Payor Negotiations (3) [outcomes data for rate justification]
  └── supports --> Therapist Retention [quality environment attracts/retains clinicians]
```

The therapist recruitment initiative is the binding constraint. Billing improvement and clinical quality are foundational enablers. Clinic expansion and payor optimization are the primary value creation drivers but require the foundations to be in place.
