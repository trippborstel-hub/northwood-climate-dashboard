**Plan Initiated:** February 1, 2024 (Day 1 = Close Date)
**Plan Owner:** Jennifer Martinez, COO (with oversight from Lisa Brennan, Northwood Operating Partner)

## Plan Overview

The 100-day plan establishes the operational foundation for Keystone's transformation from a founder-led, single-state ABA therapy provider to a scalable multi-state platform. The plan focuses on five critical workstreams: centralized billing, EHR migration, therapist recruitment pipeline, clinic expansion planning, and payor contract renegotiations.

**Note:** While the calendar has moved well past 100 days from close, this document tracks the original 100-day plan milestones and their actual completion status. Some workstreams that were initiated within the 100-day window remain in execution phase.

| Hiring run rate: 3 BCBAs / month | Day 90 | Not achieved | Current: 1-2 / month |

**Assessment:**
The structural investments are in place (university partnerships, internal promotion program, recruitment marketing), but conversion from pipeline to hires is below target. The primary barrier is compensation competitiveness — Keystone's compensation package is below the top quartile for the BCBA market.

**Corrective Actions:**
1. Compensation benchmarking study (in progress, February 2026 delivery)
2. Proposed signing bonus program ($5-10K for experienced BCBAs)
3. Clinical mentorship program to improve retention
4. Accelerated RBT-to-BCBA promotion timeline

## Key Lessons Learned

1. **Billing centralization was the fastest win.** Moving to a unified platform generated measurable ROI within 6 months. This should be the first workstream in any healthcare services 100-day plan.
2. **EHR migrations take longer than planned.** Legacy system heterogeneity added 30+ days to the timeline. Budget 90 days, not 60, for ABA-specific EHR migrations.
3. **Therapist hiring is a structural constraint, not an execution failure.** The BCBA market is genuinely supply-constrained. Compensation competitiveness and pipeline development are necessary but not sufficient — the constraint limits growth velocity regardless of operational excellence.
4. **Data-driven payor negotiations require clean data first.** The billing centralization workstream had to complete before meaningful payor negotiations could begin. The sequencing was correct.
