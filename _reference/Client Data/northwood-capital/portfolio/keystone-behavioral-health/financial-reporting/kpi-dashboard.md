## Commentary

Revenue is $2.0M below budget at $138.0M, primarily driven by slower-than-planned therapist hiring which constrains patient capacity. EBITDA margin at 17.5% is 40 bps below budget due to investment in centralized billing infrastructure and EHR migration costs. The BCBA recruitment pipeline remains the most critical bottleneck — 12 open positions versus a target of 5. Therapist retention at 78% is improving but still below the 85% target. Authorization utilization has improved from 78% at acquisition to 81% but remains well below the 88% target, representing an estimated $1.5M annualized EBITDA opportunity.

The centralized billing platform went live in Q3 2025. Claims denial rates have improved from 12% at acquisition to 9.5%, with the target of 5.0% expected as the team builds proficiency on the new platform. DSO has improved from 55 days to 48 days. These are encouraging early results but further improvement is needed.
