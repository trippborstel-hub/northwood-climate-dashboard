## Key Budget Assumptions

1. **Revenue growth** of 12.6% driven by same-store growth (5.5%), new clinic openings (partial year), and payor rate increases.
2. **EBITDA margin expansion** of 50 bps to 18.0% as centralized billing reduces denials and improves collections, partially offset by new clinic ramp costs.
3. **BCBA net adds of 15** — aggressive but achievable with university partnership program and internal promotion pipeline. This is the most critical assumption.
4. **Payor rate increases** of 3-5% assumed effective H2 2026 for BlueCross and UnitedHealth contracts. Negotiations in progress.
5. **No add-on acquisitions** budgeted. Any M&A activity would be incremental to these figures.
6. **Seasonal pattern** assumes Q1 dip (deductible resets), relatively flat Q2-Q3, and slight Q4 uptick.

## Budget Risks

| Risk | Impact if Realized | Probability | Budget Sensitivity |
|------|-------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| BCBA hiring misses target | Revenue -$3-5M, EBITDA -$1.5M | Medium-High | Clinic 23 deferred; same-store growth limited |
| Payor rate increases denied | Revenue -$2.0M, EBITDA -$2.0M | Medium | Margin target of 18.0% not achieved |
| New clinic ramp slower than plan | Revenue -$1.5M, EBITDA -$0.5M | Medium | Extended breakeven, higher capex-to-revenue ratio |
| Therapist wage inflation >5% | EBITDA -$1.0M | Medium | Market-driven; limited short-term mitigation |
