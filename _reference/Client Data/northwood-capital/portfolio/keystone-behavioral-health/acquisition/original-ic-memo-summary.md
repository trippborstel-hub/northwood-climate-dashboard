## Investment Recommendation

Acquire 80% of Keystone Behavioral Health for $200.0M enterprise value (9.0x LTM Adj. EBITDA) with $120.0M total equity ($96.0M fund equity, $24.0M management rollover). The transaction represents an opportunity to build a multi-state ABA therapy platform in a market where demand structurally exceeds supply. The base case targets a 2.5-3.0x gross MOIC and ~20% gross IRR over a 5-year hold.

---

## Market Overview

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy is the evidence-based, gold-standard treatment for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The ABA therapy market is estimated at $6-8B and growing 15-18% annually, driven by:

- **Rising diagnosis rates** — CDC prevalence now 1 in 36 children (up from 1 in 150 in 2000). Increased awareness and earlier diagnosis drive volume.
- **Insurance mandates** — All 50 states require commercial insurers to cover ABA therapy. Medicaid coverage expanding.
- **Supply shortage** — Estimated national shortage of 10,000+ BCBAs. Wait lists average 6-12 months. Supply constraints limit competition.
- **Fragmented market** — Highly fragmented, with the top 10 providers representing <15% of the market. Hundreds of single-state operators with $5-30M revenue.

The market is defensive, with counter-cyclical demand characteristics. ABA therapy is medically necessary and insurance-mandated, providing resilience through economic cycles.

## Value Creation Plan

### Pillar 1: Revenue Growth (60% of value creation)
- **De novo clinics** — Open 8-12 new clinics over 5 years in underserved markets with wait list data. Target $3-5M revenue per mature clinic.
- **Add-on acquisitions** — Acquire 3-5 single-state ABA providers at 5-7x EBITDA. Identified pipeline of 20+ targets.
- **Patient volume** — Increase active patients from ~1,100 to 2,500+ through geographic and capacity expansion.
- **Payor rate optimization** — Renegotiate rates at platform scale; target 3-5% increases on commercial contracts.

### Pillar 2: Margin Expansion (20% of value creation)
- **Centralized billing** — Implement unified billing platform. Reduce denials from 12% to 5%. Improve collections cycle from 55 days to 40 days.
- **Authorization utilization** — Increase authorized hours utilization from 78% to 88%. Each point of improvement adds ~$1M EBITDA.
- **Clinical training standardization** — Reduce new therapist ramp time from 12 weeks to 8 weeks through standardized training curriculum.

### Pillar 3: Therapist Pipeline (20% of value creation)
- **University partnerships** — Establish relationships with 10+ graduate programs in Applied Behavior Analysis. Fund scholarships in exchange for 2-year employment commitments.
- **Retention programs** — Implement clinical career ladders, mentorship programs, and competitive compensation. Target BCBA retention >85%.
- **RBT-to-BCBA pipeline** — Fund and support RBT employees pursuing BCBA certification. Internal pipeline of 15-20 BCBA candidates per year.

## IC Discussion Notes

- Committee unanimously approved the transaction. Healthcare services platform thesis is well-understood within the firm (parallel to NovaCare Health Partners in Fund I).
- Committee flagged the importance of moving quickly on therapist pipeline initiatives — the BCBA supply constraint is both the opportunity (barrier to entry) and the risk (limits growth velocity).
- Operating Partner Lisa Brennan to provide hands-on support for the first 100 days, focusing on billing centralization and clinical operations standardization.
- Committee noted the 9.0x entry multiple is fair for a platform-quality ABA provider but leaves limited room for multiple compression. Value creation must come from EBITDA growth, not multiple arbitrage.
