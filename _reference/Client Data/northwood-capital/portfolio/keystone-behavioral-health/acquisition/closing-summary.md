# Keystone Behavioral Health — Closing Summary

**Transaction Close Date:** February 1, 2024
**Fund:** Northwood Capital Fund II, L.P.

---

## Transaction Overview

Northwood Capital acquired Keystone Behavioral Health, a multi-state Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy platform, in a platform acquisition with a partial founder/clinician exit. The founder, a licensed BCBA and clinical psychologist, retained a 20% equity rollover stake alongside Northwood's 80% ownership. The transaction was sourced through a limited auction process with healthcare-focused advisors.

**Sector:** Healthcare Services — Behavioral Health / Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) Therapy
**Headquarters:** Mid-Atlantic
**Transaction Type:** Platform acquisition (founder/clinician partial exit, 20% rollover)

## Investment Thesis at Entry

ABA therapy demand far exceeds supply, with autism diagnosis rates increasing 10%+ annually and wait lists averaging 6-12 months across most markets. Keystone is positioned as a platform to build a multi-state ABA provider through de novo clinic openings and add-on acquisitions. Key thesis pillars:

1. **Demand-supply imbalance** — Autism prevalence now estimated at 1 in 36 children (CDC, 2023). ABA therapy is the gold-standard intervention with strong insurance mandate coverage across 50 states. Wait lists of 6-12 months indicate severe supply constraints.
2. **Multi-state platform build** — Consolidate fragmented, single-state ABA providers. Identified 20+ acquisition targets at 5-7x EBITDA in adjacent geographies.
3. **Centralized infrastructure** — Centralize clinical training, billing, credentialing, and compliance across all locations. Reduce administrative burden on clinicians to improve therapist retention.
4. **Payor optimization** — Renegotiate reimbursement rates at platform scale. Improve authorization utilization rates from ~78% to industry best practice of 88-92%.
5. **Therapist pipeline** — Build university partnership program and internal training academy to address BCBA supply constraint.

## Seller / Founder Details

Dr. Rachel Adams founded Keystone Behavioral Health in 2015 as a single-clinic ABA therapy provider. Over nine years, she built the company to a multi-state platform with $125M in revenue and 290 employees. The partial exit was motivated by a desire to de-risk personal wealth while maintaining clinical leadership and upside through 20% equity rollover. Dr. Adams remains as CEO, focusing on clinical quality and program development, with William Park and Jennifer Martinez handling financial and operational execution.

The founder's clinical reputation and relationships with referring physicians and school districts are significant intangible assets. Transition planning includes building a clinical leadership bench (Regional Clinical Directors) to reduce dependence on Dr. Adams for clinical oversight.
