## Market Overview

The private equity mid-market remained active in Q4 2025, though deal flow moderated from Q3 levels. Fundraising conditions stabilized as LPs gained clarity on portfolio valuations and distributions improved across the industry. Several dynamics shaped our operating environment during the quarter:

- **M&A activity** in the mid-market ($75M-$250M EV) held steady, with business services and healthcare services transactions maintaining healthy volume. Industrial technology saw increased interest driven by reshoring themes and defense spending.
- **Credit markets** remained constructive for leveraged buyouts, with direct lending spreads holding in the S+425-500bps range. Lender appetite for services businesses with recurring revenue profiles stayed strong.
- **Valuation multiples** in our core sectors remained elevated relative to historical averages, reinforcing the importance of disciplined entry pricing and thesis-driven value creation.
- **Our core sectors** — business services, healthcare services, and industrial technology — continue to benefit from structural tailwinds. Environmental regulatory momentum (PFAS testing, infrastructure investment), healthcare capacity constraints (behavioral health, rehabilitation), and reshoring-driven industrial demand are durable themes underpinning our portfolio and pipeline.

### Outlook

Fund I is in the harvesting phase with four mature portfolio companies, all of which are performing at or above plan. The fund's 2.2x net MOIC and ~18% net IRR position it in upper-quartile territory for the 2017 vintage.

**Meridian Business Solutions**, the fund's largest holding, is preparing for a formal exit process. We anticipate engaging a sell-side advisor in Q1 2026 and launching the process in H2 2026. At a 9.5x exit multiple on current EBITDA of $38.3M, the implied enterprise value is $363.9M, representing significant value creation from the $195.0M entry. We will provide additional detail on exit timing as the process advances.

The remaining three active companies — NovaCare Health Partners, Sterling Precision Manufacturing, and Trident IT Solutions — continue to execute on their value creation plans. Each has identifiable exit paths within a 12-24 month horizon, though we remain focused on maximizing value rather than forcing exits to meet an arbitrary timeline.

### New Investment Activity

**Pinnacle Testing & Inspection** (closed October 2024) is our most recent platform investment. Pinnacle is a non-destructive testing and industrial inspection services company headquartered in the Midwest with 275 employees serving 95 customers across the energy, infrastructure, and industrial sectors. We acquired the company at $155.0M enterprise value (8.0x entry multiple). The business benefits from critical infrastructure inspection demand driven by aging bridges, pipelines, and refinery assets, while technician certification requirements create meaningful barriers to entry. We see an opportunity to build a national inspection services platform through geographic add-on acquisitions. The management transition from the departing founder has proceeded smoothly, and the integration is on track through the first four months.

## Portfolio Highlights

### Fund I

**Meridian Business Solutions** (Business Services) — Revenue of $225.0M and EBITDA of $38.3M, representing 73% and 76% growth from entry, respectively. Leverage has decreased from 3.6x at entry to 1.1x. $30.0M in distributions returned. Exit process preparation underway with sell-side advisor selection targeted for Q1 2026. Gross MOIC: 3.0x.

**NovaCare Health Partners** (Healthcare Services) — Revenue of $175.0M and EBITDA of $28.0M. The company has grown from 14 clinics at entry to 28 clinics through a combination of de novo openings and two add-on acquisitions (PhysioFirst Clinics, RehabWorks). 2026 expansion pipeline under review. Gross MOIC: 2.7x.

**Sterling Precision Manufacturing** (Industrial Technology) — Revenue of $168.0M and EBITDA of $30.2M. Benefiting from reshoring tailwinds and strong defense sector demand. One add-on completed (MicroTech Tooling). Evaluating a second add-on to extend capabilities. Gross MOIC: 2.9x.

**Trident IT Solutions** (Technology Services) — Revenue of $142.0M and EBITDA of $24.9M. The IT managed services and cybersecurity platform continues to perform above plan, driven by strong recurring revenue (85%+) and net revenue retention of 112%. Two add-ons completed (ShieldPoint Cyber, NetWatch MSP). Gross MOIC: 2.8x.

### Fund II

**Clearview Environmental Services** (Environmental Services) — Revenue of $195.0M and EBITDA of $31.2M. Performing on plan with environmental regulatory tailwinds. One add-on completed (GreenTech Testing, PFAS specialist). PFAS testing demand continues to accelerate as EPA and state-level regulations expand. Gross MOIC: 1.6x.

**Patriot Staffing Solutions** (Business Services) — Revenue of $310.0M and EBITDA of $23.8M. Revenue growth has been healthy (+9% from entry), but EBITDA margins have been under pressure from wage inflation in the light industrial temporary labor market. Management is implementing a comprehensive bill rate adjustment program across the client base, and we are closely monitoring margin recovery through the first half of 2026. Gross MOIC: 1.3x.

**Keystone Behavioral Health** (Healthcare Services) — Revenue of $138.0M and EBITDA of $24.2M. The applied behavior analysis (ABA) therapy platform is executing its growth plan. Demand for ABA services continues to exceed supply, with autism diagnosis rates growing 10%+ annually. The company is building its clinical training and therapist recruitment infrastructure to support multi-state expansion. Gross MOIC: 1.4x.

**Pinnacle Testing & Inspection** (Industrial Technology) — Revenue of $115.0M and EBITDA of $20.1M. Early-stage integration proceeding on plan. Management transition smooth. First add-on evaluation underway. Gross MOIC: 1.2x.

## Outlook & Key Themes

We enter 2026 with a constructive outlook. Several themes inform our positioning:

**Thesis continuity.** Our core sectors continue to offer attractive risk-adjusted returns. Healthcare services demand growth is structural and supply-constrained. Environmental services benefit from a multi-year regulatory tailwind. Industrial technology is supported by reshoring and infrastructure investment. Business services with recurring revenue profiles remain in demand from both strategic and financial buyers.

**Fund I — value realization.** With four mature, well-performing portfolio companies, Fund I is positioned for a productive harvesting phase over the next 12-24 months. The Meridian exit process will be a key milestone for the fund.

**Fund II — disciplined deployment.** We are pacing deployment to maximize risk-adjusted returns. The fund has substantial remaining capacity and a healthy pipeline. We are focused on finding the right opportunities rather than deploying capital to meet a deployment schedule.

**Portfolio management.** We continue to invest in operational improvement across the portfolio. Operating Partners David Okafor (industrial) and Lisa Brennan (healthcare) are providing hands-on support to portfolio management teams. We are addressing the margin headwinds at Patriot Staffing through active engagement with the management team and expect to see improvement through 2026.
