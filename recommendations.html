Climate Is Three Things You Already Manage

This isn't a sustainability report. It's a synthesis of physical risk, transition risk, and decarbonization analysis across your portfolio — translated into the language of value creation, risk management, and capital allocation.

Protect Value

Physical Risk. Heat stress and water stress are measurable at the facility level. Some of your sites face quantifiable EBITDA erosion over the hold period. This is downside protection — the same discipline as insurance or business continuity planning.

Position for Where the Market Is Going

Transition Risk. Carbon pricing, customer ESG requirements, and regulatory shifts are creating cost and market access risks. Your buyers, customers, and LPs are already modeling this. You should be too.

Create Value

Decarbonization. The same actions that reduce emissions also reduce energy costs, improve exit positioning, and build your LP narrative. Many have positive NPV and payback periods under 3 years.

Physical Risk — Where It Matters Most

Three Companies Need Facility-Level Attention

You'd never acquire a building without knowing the flood zone. This is the same discipline applied to your operating portfolio.

Ridgeline Property Services

Highest water stress in portfolio — 66.7 baseline score (high category). Mountain West / Southwest concentration. 115 days above 35°C by 2035.

$13.7M EV at risk. Managed properties (~18M sq ft) amplify exposure beyond corporate footprint. HVAC load increases directly compress margins on fixed-fee management contracts.

Sterling Precision Manufacturing

Highest heat exposure in portfolio — 107.7 days above 35°C by 2035. Manufacturing operations are temperature-sensitive (precision tolerances, worker safety).

$24.0M EV at risk. But strong positive-NPV mitigations available: OSHA heat action plan ($90K capex, $328K EBITDA saved), HVAC upgrade ($1.8M capex, $492K saved), shift scheduling (zero capex).

Harvest Ridge Foods

Water dependency + cold storage — 28M gal/yr water usage. Bowling Green plant had 3 pressure drops in summer 2025 (~$280K production losses). Nashville facility has 17-year-old R-404A refrigeration system.

$13.9M EV at risk. Heat action plan ($150K capex, $149K EBITDA saved) and drought-resilient sourcing (zero capex, $142K saved) are immediate wins.
The takeaway: Physical risk is concentrated, not distributed. Three companies represent 68% of portfolio EV-at-risk. Targeted interventions at these three — totaling ~$3.1M in capex — protect $16.5M in enterprise value. That's a 5.3x return on mitigation spend.

Transition Risk — The Two That Matter Now

Harvest Ridge and Meridian Need Action Before Year-End

Harvest Ridge Foods — Customer Mandate Risk

The problem: Top-5 customers = 45% of revenue. These are F500 brands (Unilever, Nestlé, PepsiCo, General Mills) with SBTi-validated Scope 3 commitments. Three customers (~$34M revenue) have already requested supplier emissions data. One makes it mandatory by 2027.

What's at stake: $8–12M EBITDA/yr. Not because Harvest Ridge will lose these customers overnight — but because RFP scoring increasingly weights supplier sustainability data. Without it, Harvest Ridge moves from preferred to replaceable.

The action: Within 60 days, identify top-5 customer Scope 3 programs. Commission per-SKU cradle-to-gate product carbon footprints for top 3 customers. The carbon inventory baseline is already built — this extends it to the customer-facing format they need.

Meridian Business Solutions — Exit Multiple at Risk

The problem: 8-year hold. Sell-side advisor discussion on March board agenda. Buyer universe is financial services (highest SBTi-adoption sector). PE buyers now routinely include ESG in due diligence. Showing up without climate data is like showing up without a quality of earnings report.

What's at stake: 0.5–1.0x multiple compression. On $38.3M EBITDA at 9.5x, that's $19–38M in enterprise value. The cost of preparing the climate data: effectively zero — the baseline is already calculated.

The action: Pre-DD climate package within weeks: Scope 1/2 baseline, intensity metrics and trajectory, customer SBTi/CDP map, transition risk one-pager, climate section for the CIM. This is exit value protection with asymmetric upside.
Both of these are time-sensitive. Meridian's exit process has a fixed timeline. Harvest Ridge's customer mandates have a fixed deadline. Unlike efficiency capex (which you can schedule), these windows close.

Opportunity Spotlight

Clearview Is a Net Beneficiary of Climate Transition

Not every climate finding is a risk. Clearview Environmental is positioned to capture upside from the same regulatory and market forces that create pressure elsewhere in the portfolio.

Demand Tailwinds

PFAS regulation — EPA final rule creates remediation demand across municipal and industrial water systems. Clearview's lab and field capabilities are directly positioned.

Infrastructure bill — Federal funding for environmental cleanup and water infrastructure. Clearview's 185-client diversified base captures this spend.

Methane regulations — Tighter leak detection and remediation requirements for O&G operators. Clearview's field operations serve this market.
Exit positioning: Quantified sustainability credentials + growth-from-regulation narrative is a compelling buyer thesis. Fleet electrification feasibility study strengthens this — shows the business is practicing what its customers are paying for.
