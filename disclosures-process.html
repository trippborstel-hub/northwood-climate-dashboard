Why productize disclosures the assignment, in plain terms

The disclosure market isn't standardized. Big 4 firms charge $150–300K over four-to-six months. Boutique ESG consultancies charge $75–150K. AI-first entrants sell software subscriptions that don't actually produce a publishable document. None of them sell a repeatable fixed-price service with a guaranteed four-week turnaround.

A Kith-style disclosure service fits in that gap. The value is not that we have better climate scientists — it's that we have a process that produces the same quality output every time, at a price LPs can justify and GPs can predict. The forcing function is pricing: committing to a fixed fee forces us to build a process that actually runs at the quoted cost.

This page shows the process. Subsequent pages show the reference files that make it repeatable, and the TCFD and ESRS E1 outputs produced for Northwood as the demonstration client.

Three Principles That Make This Work

The Week 6 lecture flagged three hurdles for AI-first consulting: cognitive overload (too much AI output to QA), backend complexity, and the need for human experts to validate AI-generated novelty. Each principle below maps to one hurdle.

Reference Files Constrain Output

Every drafting task reads specific reference files before producing output. Structure is fixed by datapoint, not open-ended. The AI can't write a 60-page TCFD disclosure because the pillar template caps it at 15–25 pages and names exactly what each section contains.

Reference Files Are Living IP

Six reference files — service overview, TCFD template, ESRS E1 datapoint map, interview script, QA checklist, voice guide — amortize across every future engagement. Generated iteratively with AI per Diego's guidance; judged by output quality, not content inspection.

Human QA Is Non-Negotiable

Step 6 is 100% human by design. 8 hours of senior reviewer time running a structured checklist. No AI "second opinion." The QA checklist has hard pass/fail gates per section; a failed section rejects the whole draft. The forcing function is that this cost is baked into the price.

Page-Count Discipline

TCFD disclosures: 15–25 pages. ESRS E1: 30–50 pages. Length is not a proxy for quality. Over-length is a signal of AI-overload leakage and triggers a QA rejection. Concision is mandatory — LPs read disclosures they can digest in under an hour.

One Evidence Base, Two Views

TCFD and ESRS E1 share all underlying data. Emissions figures match. Risk assessments match. Target statements match. What differs is presentation — TCFD is pillar-structured; ESRS E1 is datapoint-structured. One process produces both.

Honest First-Year Posture

First-year disclosures gain credibility through acknowledged limitations, not polish. "Not yet quantified" with a remediation date beats a dressed-up estimate. "No target adopted; IC decision Q3 2026" beats a committed-sounding non-commitment. The voice file enforces this.
