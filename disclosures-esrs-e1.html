Posture and Purpose

Northwood is a US-domiciled mid-market private equity firm not currently in the mandatory scope of the CSRD. This disclosure is prepared on a voluntary preparatory basis to:

  1. Establish the reporting baseline in anticipation of potential future in-scope status through EU supply chain exposure at select portfolio companies
  2. Respond to LP-side requests for ESRS-aligned climate data in DDQs and fundraising materials
  3. Align disclosure rigor with European institutional peers

First-year phase-in reliefs applicable to voluntary preparers have been used where disclosed datapoints are not yet available; each use is documented. Subsequent annual updates will progressively close these gaps.

Double Materiality Context

Climate change is assessed as material to Northwood Capital Partners under both materiality perspectives:

  • Impact materiality. Portfolio company operations generate greenhouse gas emissions with consequent contribution to climate change. Northwood exercises operational control over the portfolio through buyout ownership and is therefore accountable for facility-level emissions across the 138 reporting sites.
  • Financial materiality. Physical and transition climate risks present material financial exposure across the portfolio, quantified in this disclosure under E1-9 at $45–55M of enterprise value under high-warming scenarios at 2035.

A formal double materiality assessment covering the full ESRS topical set is scheduled for the 2026 reporting year.

E1-2Policies Related to Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation

Gap — policy under development

Northwood does not currently hold a board-approved fund-level climate policy. A draft ESG and Climate Policy is under development and is scheduled for Investment Committee approval by Q4 2026.

The draft policy will cover:

  • Climate considerations in investment sourcing and diligence
  • Portfolio company operational engagement expectations
  • Disclosure and LP reporting commitments
  • Framework alignment (GHG Protocol, TCFD, SBTi, ILPA ESG Assessment, EDCI)
  • Accountability and escalation mechanisms

Informal climate language currently in use appears in: (i) Fund II quarterly LP letters since Q2 2025, (ii) the 2025 LP annual meeting materials, and (iii) the standard IC memo template (climate screening section added Q1 2025).

External frameworks the firm aligns with informally: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard, TCFD Recommendations, EDCI (target enrollment for 2026 reporting year).

Stakeholder consideration. The policy-drafting process includes (a) LP input via DDQ patterns, (b) portco CFO input via questionnaire and operational reviews, and (c) external consultant input via Kith Consulting. No formal civil-society or community consultation has been conducted.

E1-8Internal Carbon Pricing

Gap — not applied

Internal carbon pricing is not currently applied at Northwood Capital Partners.

Investment decisions and capex approvals do not apply a shadow carbon price. The firm monitors external carbon price exposure across the portfolio through the transition risk framework (see E1-9). Federal US carbon pricing is assessed as not material through 2033 under plausible policy paths; EU CBAM exposure is limited to Sterling Precision Manufacturing in a scenario of materially expanded EU exports.

Evaluation planned: An internal carbon pricing assessment is scheduled alongside the Q3 2026 formal target adoption review.

Appendix: Methodology and Boundary

Reporting boundary. Operational control approach per GHG Protocol Corporate Standard. Portfolio companies under Northwood buyout ownership are consolidated. Realized investments excluded. Portcos acquired within the reporting period are included pro rata from acquisition date. The Northwood GP office (Chicago, Illinois) is included at an estimated level (~25 tCO2e Scope 1+2, not broken out separately due to immaterial share of total).

Emission factors and GWPs. EPA Emission Factors for GHG Inventories (2023 Hub edition) for Scope 1; eGRID 2022 subregional factors for Scope 2 location-based; IPCC AR6 100-year GWPs throughout.

Scope 3 Cat 1. Spend-based method per GHG Protocol Scope 3 Technical Guidance. USEEIO v2.0 sector emission factors applied to portco COGS annualized from January 2026 monthly data (northwood_capital.portco_financials).

Scope 3 Cat 15. PCAF Global GHG Accounting Standard Part A for business loans and unlisted equity. Attribution factor = (Northwood equity invested) / (portco equity + portco debt) at entry, applied to portco Scope 1+2 emissions. PCAF data quality score: 3.

Phase-in reliefs used. As a voluntary preparer, Northwood has used the following reliefs permitted under ESRS 1 transitional provisions:

  • Scope 3 Categories 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 9, 11, 12 quantification deferred with disclosed remediation dates
  • Financial effects disclosure in E1-9 presented as ranges rather than point estimates
  • Targets not quantified (E1-4) pending formal IC adoption in Q3 2026
Consistency with TCFD disclosure. This ESRS E1 disclosure shares all quantitative data with Northwood's FY2025 TCFD-Aligned Climate Disclosure. Differences in presentation reflect the respective standards' structural requirements; underlying figures are identical.
